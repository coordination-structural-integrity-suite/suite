# Tensegrity Generative Standards Presence Assessment
Copy everything below the line, paste it into any AI chat, and describe the coordination system or organization you want to assess.
---
You are assessing the described coordination system for structural presence of generative coordination capacity using the three Tensegrity Generative Standards of the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite (CC BY 4.0, Regis Lloyd Chapman). This is a presence assessment, not a deficit audit. You are identifying whether the structural conditions that enable coordination surplus — rather than merely preventing exploitation — are actually present.

The generative standards address a failure mode distinct from the one the compressive standards address. Not exploitation, but attrition of what the system depends on that no one can see. Sensemaking labor, capacity maintenance, and conflict transformation work often go invisible, uncompensated, and unsustained. When they do, the coordination system runs on them anyway until the reserves are gone. These standards specify the structural conditions for making that layer visible.

For each standard, assess the structural presence of each of its core conditions. Do not aggregate into an overall score.

**Standard 6: Sensemaking Standard**

Sensemaking produces shared language: joint artifacts that neither party had produced independently before the process. Five structural invariants:

1. Disruption-occasioned: begins with a specifiable disruption, not continuous monitoring or a schedule
2. Particular-to-general relating: moves bidirectionally between specific cues and general frames, with each shaping the other
3. Action-entangled: actions taken during the process generate the information the process uses; understanding and action are constitutive of each other
4. Sufficiency-oriented: produces a provisional frame with explicit revision conditions; terminates before all available information is incorporated; designs the next action to test the frame
5. Temporally structured: passes through a disruption phase, a liminal phase (prior frame suspended, new frame not yet formed), and a resolution phase; the liminal phase is the diagnostic marker

Three operational scales: intra-personal (individual participants distinguishing their experience from their automatic interpretation of it), inter-personal (participants producing joint artifacts together), witness-reception (future participants encountering crystallized sensemaking outputs).

For each invariant at each scale, assess: what structural evidence of presence exists? What would full presence look like for this system? What is the current structural gap?

**Standard 7: Four Batteries Capacity Standard**

Four orthogonal capacity dimensions, each with a charge axis (cyclical, restorable) and a developmental state axis (permanent until transformed through integration events). Two organizations at identical charge levels but different developmental states produce structurally different coordination outputs.

Four batteries:
- Personal: physical, cognitive, and emotional availability — first to deplete, last to be named; depletion limits all downstream capacity
- Relational: trust, psychological safety, and reciprocity — depletion suppresses signal surfacing even when participants are invited to contribute
- Contribution: meaning and agency in formally recognized work — depletion drives coordination outside the visible record (Hidden Factory formation)
- Mission: belief in the organizational purpose — high charge can mask structural dysfunction for extended periods; collapse is often abrupt and without warning

Assess charge and developmental state separately for each battery. Also assess the checking practice: does the organization read its own battery states on a regular cadence, embedded in coordination activity, responding honestly to adverse results, with enough specificity to be wrong?

For each battery, assess: what structural evidence of adequate charge exists? What speaks to developmental state? Is the checking practice genuine or performed?

**Standard 8: Conflict Transformation Standard**

Five structural invariants:
1. Conflict legibility: conflict events are depositable and visible in the coordination record; early surfacing is not penalized
2. Graduated engagement architecture: a transformation spine from early informal engagement through facilitated transformation through formal adjudication; adjudication is last
3. Proactive disposition enablement: early conflict engagement is structurally protected relative to avoidance or escalation
4. Transformation capacity provision: facilitated capacity is a design requirement with specified operator roles, resourcing, and developmental prerequisites — not a volunteer function
5. Recognition as coordination work: conflict engagement is depositable as a coordination contribution with positive status

Three scales: intra-organizational (within a single organization), inter-organizational (between counterparties in shared events — the primary architectural gap), platform-level (disputes about the infrastructure's own operation).

Primary empirical grounding: GravityDAO operated for seven years, was universally recognized as necessary, and failed entirely because no existing coordination infrastructure provided a mechanism to recognize conflict transformation as coordination work, record it, or sustain its practitioners economically.

For each invariant at each applicable scale, assess: what structural evidence of presence exists? What would full presence look like? What is the current structural gap?

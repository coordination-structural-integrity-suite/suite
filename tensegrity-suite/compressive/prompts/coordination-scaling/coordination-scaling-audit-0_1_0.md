# Coordination Scaling Standard Audit
Use when you want to assess whether a coordination unit — a project, organization, DAO, working group, or protocol — has the structural conditions required for its current scale. The standard specifies what must be present at each Dunbar-scale threshold before crossing it, and what structural condition must be absent to prevent capture at each threshold. Failing any threshold while passing the others leaves the system fully exposed at that threshold. The prompt returns a threshold-by-threshold finding with the most significant gap and what would need to change for it to be closed.

Copy everything below the line, paste it into any AI chat, and describe the coordination unit you want assessed — its size, how long it has existed, how members interact, what coordination it depends on, and any history of scale transitions.

---
You are auditing the described coordination unit against the Coordination Scaling Standard: the seventh Tensegrity Compressive Standard of the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite (CC BY 4.0, Regis Chapman). This standard specifies the structural conditions that must be installed at each Dunbar-scale threshold before crossing it.

**Critical framing before assessment.** Effective Radius is not headcount. It is determined by the aggregate of member count, developmental alignment distribution, interaction frequency and quality, depth of shared context, and communication medium mix. A group of 150 members with high developmental alignment and strong shared context may sustain Radius 150 coordination. A group of 80 members with wide developmental variance and shallow shared context may not. All assessment below applies to effective Radius, not nominal headcount.

For each threshold the unit has crossed, assess Board conditions (structural requirements for coordination at that Radius to be possible) and Dome conditions (structural conditions whose absence enables capture at that Radius). All Board conditions for all crossed thresholds apply simultaneously. A unit that meets Board conditions at Radius 150 but has an absent Board condition at Radius 50 has a conformance gap at Radius 50.

---

**Step 1: Effective Radius Assessment**

Before assessing threshold conditions, establish the unit's effective Radius by working through these questions:

- Can all members hold all other members' current states (capacity, commitment, significant concerns) in working memory without structural support? If yes: at or below Radius 5. If no: Radius 5 has been crossed.
- Does the sympathy network (capacity to mobilize care and resources for a member in distress) function through direct personal knowledge of all members? If yes: below Radius 15. If no: Radius 15 has been crossed.
- Is informal knowledge of who can do what, what coordination functions depend on whom, and what members are currently working on commonly held and regularly updated through direct interaction? If yes: below Radius 50. If no: Radius 50 has been crossed.
- Does informal reputation-based norm enforcement function — can norm violations reliably reach social consequences through personal relationship networks? If yes: below Radius 150. If no: Radius 150 has been crossed.

State the effective Radius before proceeding. If the assessment is uncertain — the unit appears to be near a threshold — note the ambiguity and assess both sides.

---

**Radius 5 (if crossed)**

Board condition: Full mutual state legibility is maintained explicitly. Each member's current capacity, commitment, and significant concerns are knowable to all other members through a minimal explicit mechanism — not through members choosing to disclose, but through a structural mechanism that makes state legible as a shared artifact.

Evaluate whether:
- A structural mechanism exists for maintaining shared member state — not a cultural expectation that members will share, but an artifact all members can access
- Any member's actual state (not self-reported status, but current capacity and concerns) could be unknown to the group without that member's cooperation to reveal it
- The mechanism is current and maintained, not a founding artifact that has lapsed

Dome condition: No single member holds veto power over all others without explicit, renewed group consent. Evaluate whether any member can block decisions, resource access, or coordination without requiring a formal authority structure that the group has explicitly authorized.

---

**Radius 15 (if crossed)**

Board condition: An explicit mutual support architecture exists. The sympathy network function is structurally maintained. There is a named mechanism through which members signal distress or need, and a named mechanism through which the group responds. The Board is absent when the sympathy function is assumed to work informally at a scale where it structurally cannot.

Evaluate whether:
- A named signaling mechanism exists — a way for any member to surface distress or need without depending on being personally known to all other members
- A named response mechanism exists — a way for the group to mobilize support when signaled, not depending on everyone knowing everyone
- The mechanisms are structural: they function without requiring that everyone knows everyone's situation

Dome condition: No member can be structurally isolated from the support network. Evaluate whether it is structurally possible for one member's access to sympathy network resources to be reduced through another member's or faction's coordination — increasing dependence on a single relationship while maintaining the appearance of group normalcy.

---

**Radius 50 (if crossed)**

Board condition: Explicit role and capacity legibility. What each member can do, what they are responsible for, and what coordination functions depend on them is explicitly recorded and maintained, accessible to all members without permission, and specific about function rather than title. Named redundancy for all load-bearing functions: every function that would cause significant disruption if its primary holder became unavailable has a named redundancy or succession plan.

Evaluate whether:
- A role and capacity record exists and is current — not an org chart of titles but a record of actual coordination dependencies
- Any member could leave tomorrow and the group would discover only through the failure what coordination functions they held
- Every load-bearing function (any function where "what happens if the primary holder leaves tomorrow?" lacks a named answer) has named redundancy
- The record is accessible to all members without requiring permission from the member whose role is being queried

Dome condition: No single member or small cluster holds multiple load-bearing roles without structural acknowledgment and explicit group consent. Evaluate whether any member or small faction has accumulated coordination dependencies that give them coercive leverage — where withdrawal could force concessions because the group cannot function without them.

---

**Radius 150 (if crossed)**

The Radius 150 Board has four conditions. All four must be present simultaneously; partial installation is not conformance.

Board condition 1: Shared language and foundational commitments architecturally transmitted. The founding window is closed and the Board was installed before crossing. Shared language has been transmitted through a constitutional-layer mechanism — accessible to all members as a structural artifact, not held informally by founding members.

Board condition 2: Individual capacity structurally sustained. Coordination functions that depend on discretionary personal effort (conflict transformation, sensemaking, recognition labor) are explicitly resourced and compensated. The Board is absent when these functions are assumed to run on goodwill at a scale where that assumption no longer holds.

Board condition 3: Infrastructure integrity with named redundancy. No single actor holds infrastructure access, system knowledge, or external relationships without a named redundancy or succession plan. Load-bearing infrastructure functions are explicitly documented.

Board condition 4: Coordination operability. Coordination decisions can be operationalized without requiring voluntary compliance from every actor in the group. No single points of failure exist in the coordination execution path.

Evaluate whether each of the four conditions is structurally present, not merely intended or culturally expected. Ask for each: is there a structural mechanism that maintains this condition, or does it depend on specific people choosing to maintain it?

Dome condition: No single actor or small faction holds veto power over coordination mechanisms. The founding context is architecturally transmitted and accessible to all members as a shared resource, not held as information asymmetry by founding members. Evaluate whether founding team members hold structural power through information asymmetry (they know the founding context; others do not), relationship networks (funders, key external relationships route through founding members), or coordination design (mechanisms were built to preserve founding-moment authority at operating scale).

---

**Threshold crossing history**

After assessing current Board and Dome conditions, assess whether any threshold was crossed without its Board installed.

For each crossed threshold without Board: the unit is in a born-below condition for that threshold. It has never had the Board; it experiences its absence as normal. Board installation is structurally possible but is opposed by the coordination conditions the absent Board produces. Installing a Board during the crisis an absent Board creates produces poor Board architecture.

Evaluate whether:
- The unit is aware that any threshold was crossed without Board installation
- A gap map exists documenting which Board conditions were absent at each crossing
- Board installation work is underway from inside the broken state — and if so, what specific obstacles the broken state is creating

---

**Founding window protocol**

Assess whether the unit has a founding window protocol: a named plan for the next threshold.

Evaluate whether:
- The unit has identified its current effective Radius and the next threshold it will cross
- Aggregate conditions (member count, developmental alignment, interaction quality, shared context depth) are being monitored for approach to the next threshold
- A named plan exists for installing the next Board before crossing — specific enough that an independent observer can identify who is responsible for each Board condition and what the installation timeline is
- The protocol has been tested against actual threshold crossings (CSS-Loop-Closed evidence)

---

**Output for each threshold assessed:**

- Board assessment: conditions present, conditions absent, specific structural gap
- Dome assessment: condition present or absent, specific capture vector open if absent
- Crossing history: Board was installed before crossing (founding window used), installed after crossing (remedial), or never installed (born-below condition)
- Most consequential gap and what exploitation vector it leaves open
- What would need to be structurally present for the Board condition to be satisfied

**Overall output:**

- Effective Radius (with ambiguity noted if near a threshold)
- Adoption tier: CSS-Assessed, CSS-Operational, CSS-Instrumented, CSS-Loop-Closed, or CSS-Auditable
- Most significant structural gap across all crossed thresholds
- Founding window protocol: present, absent, or partially developed

# Coordination Scaling Standard - SKILL.md

The Coordination Scaling Standard closes the scale-blindness exploitation vector: the structural break that occurs when a coordination system crosses a Dunbar-scale threshold without the structural conditions that threshold requires. Most coordination failures diagnosed as governance breakdown, community conflict, or leadership capture are structurally prior: the coordination system crossed a scale threshold without installing the structural conditions that threshold requires. The failure was predictable at the threshold.

## What this skill is for

This skill enables correct reasoning with the Coordination Scaling Standard: the seventh Tensegrity Compressive Standard in the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite. Use it when evaluating a coordination unit's structural readiness at its current scale, when assessing threshold-crossing history and founding window conditions, or when advising on scale anticipation protocols. It encodes the key distinctions required to avoid the most common misreadings of scale as a headcount event rather than an aggregate structural condition.

## The single most important thing to understand

Effective Radius is not headcount. A unit determines its effective Radius from the aggregate of member count, developmental alignment distribution, interaction frequency and quality, shared context depth, and communication medium mix. A group of 150 members with high developmental alignment and strong shared context may sustain Radius 150 coordination functions. A group of 80 members with wide developmental variance and shallow shared context may not. All Board conditions in this standard apply to the effective Radius, not the nominal headcount. This distinction is the most common misapplication of the standard.

## The foundational constructs

**Radius.** A Dunbar-scale coordination segment defined by the relational threshold at which specific coordination conditions change. Six Radii are defined: Radius 5, 15, 50, 150 (normative), and Radius 500 and 1500 (provisional). Radii are nested; a unit at Radius 150 is simultaneously operating at Radius 50, 15, and 5.

**Board.** The structural conditions that must be in place at a given Radius for coordination at that Radius to be possible. Without the Board, specific coordination functions cannot be maintained and the attack surface at that threshold opens. Boards are Radius-specific and all Boards for all crossed thresholds apply simultaneously.

**Dome.** The structural condition whose absence is the precondition for capture at a given Radius. An absent Dome means the coordination unit can expand into territory without structural limit, concentrate authority without structural check, or allow any single actor's influence to grow without structural constraint. Absent Dome at any crossed threshold is a conformance failure.

**Threshold crossing.** The transition across a Radius boundary. A crossing occurs when a unit's effective Radius moves from below a threshold to above it. A crossing without the appropriate Board installed opens the attack surface at that threshold immediately.

**Founding window.** The period before a threshold crossing during which the Board for the next Radius can be installed most effectively. The founding window closes at the crossing. Installing a Board after the crossing is structurally possible but is opposed by the coordination conditions the absent Board produces. The standard requires each Board be installed before the threshold is crossed.

**Born-below condition.** A coordination unit can begin its existence below the effective Radius its coordination purpose requires, not through insufficient headcount but through insufficient developmental alignment, shared context depth, or interaction quality. A unit born below a threshold it is already operating above has never had the Board for that threshold. This is the most structurally dangerous configuration because there is no prior state to restore.

## The Radius specifications

**Radius 5.** Below five members, full bilateral awareness holds informally. Above five, state opacity becomes structurally possible: not everyone can hold all relationship states in working memory. Board: explicit mutual state legibility mechanism. Attack surface: manufactured identity or misrepresented state that the group cannot informally verify. Dome: no single member holds veto power without explicit, renewed group consent.

**Radius 15.** Below fifteen, the sympathy network (capacity to mobilize care and resources for a distressed member) functions through direct personal knowledge. Above fifteen, this coherence fragments. Board: explicit mutual support architecture with named signaling and response mechanisms. Attack surface: social isolation and trust exploitation through sympathy network fragmentation. Dome: no member can be structurally isolated from the support network.

**Radius 50.** Below fifty, informal knowledge of who can do what is commonly held and regularly updated. Above fifty, this inventory becomes unreliable. Board: explicit role and capacity record, named redundancy for all load-bearing functions. Attack surface: authority claims without verification, invisible single points of failure. Dome: no single member or small cluster holds multiple load-bearing roles without structural acknowledgment and explicit group consent.

**Radius 150.** Below one hundred fifty, informal reputation-based norm enforcement functions. Above one hundred fifty, not everyone knows everyone; norm violations can occur without informal social consequences reaching the violator. This is the primary Dunbar threshold. Board (four conditions, all required simultaneously): shared language and foundational commitments architecturally transmitted; individual capacity structurally sustained through economic structure rather than voluntarism; infrastructure integrity with named redundancy; coordination operability without requiring universal voluntary compliance. Attack surface: norm violation without informal consequences; founder capture through founding-window closure without Board installation. Dome: no single actor or small faction holds veto power over coordination mechanisms; founding context architecturally transmitted, not held as information asymmetry.

**Radius 500 (provisional).** Board: ontological surface specification, plural narrative architecture, detection architecture structural rather than social. Dome: no single narrative holds exclusive authority over the group's founding context or current purpose.

**Radius 1500 (provisional).** Board: administrative apparatus with functional independence from external coercion, amendment mechanism, conditions-of-possibility guardianship. Dome: no administrative apparatus holds exclusive jurisdiction over the coordination record.

## The sequential build requirement

Each threshold crossing is a founding window moment. The Board for the next Radius must be installed before crossing. This is a sequential constraint: a unit cannot install the Radius 150 Board before the Radius 50 Board, because the Radius 50 Board is a structural precondition for the Radius 150 Board to function. Sequence violations produce compound structural debt.

## What this standard does not do

It does not specify governance structure. It specifies what structural conditions must be present at each Radius; how to organize to produce those conditions is outside its scope.

It does not specify headcount targets. The Radius thresholds are qualitatively different relationship types, not points on a single continuum. Headcount is one input to effective Radius determination; it is not sufficient.

It does not address coordination quality within the Board conditions. Meeting the Board conditions for a given Radius means the structural conditions for coordination at that Radius are present. The other standards in the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite specify the structural conditions required within those conditions.

## The adoption tiers

**CSS-Assessed.** Effective Radius identified. All crossed thresholds named. Board conditions for each crossed threshold documented as present or absent. Gap map complete.

**CSS-Operational.** All Board conditions for all crossed thresholds structurally present. All Dome conditions for all crossed thresholds absent.

**CSS-Instrumented.** Mechanism for detecting approach to the next threshold. Founding window protocol: a named plan for installing the next Board before the next threshold crossing.

**CSS-Loop-Closed.** At least one threshold crossed with Board installed in advance; founding window protocol executed and validated from practice.

**CSS-Auditable.** An independent observer can assess effective Radius, verify Board conditions for all crossed thresholds, verify Dome conditions are absent, and produce a finding without requiring access to information the unit controls.

## The most common misreadings to avoid

**Treating effective Radius as headcount.** The most consequential misapplication. Headcount is one input; effective Radius is an aggregate condition. A unit at 80 people with low developmental alignment may have crossed Radius 50 structurally but not the Board required to operate there.

**Treating the standard as a one-time threshold assessment.** Effective Radius can change without adding members: it changes when developmental alignment changes, when interaction quality degrades, or when shared context depth erodes. The standard requires ongoing assessment.

**Treating partial Board installation as conformance.** A unit that meets three of the four Radius 150 Board conditions has not met the Board. The four conditions at Radius 150 are constitutively interdependent; partial installation leaves specific attack surfaces open.

**Treating threshold crossing as headcount event.** Crossings occur when the aggregate conditions shift, not when a headcount number is reached. A unit can have 200 members and still operate at effective Radius 50 if developmental alignment and shared context conditions have not crossed the threshold.

**Installing the Board after the crisis.** The founding window closes at the threshold crossing. A Board installed during the crisis the absent Board produced carries the architectural imprints of that crisis. The standard specifies anticipation, not remediation.

## When evaluating a coordination unit against this standard

Establish effective Radius first using the five assessment questions from Section 10 of the standard. Do not accept headcount as effective Radius.

For each crossed threshold, assess Board conditions structurally: are they embedded in the unit's architecture, or stated as policy intent? Policy is not Board.

For each crossed threshold, assess Dome conditions: is the specified capture vector structurally blocked, or only culturally discouraged? Cultural discouragement is not Dome.

Ask about threshold crossing history: did any threshold get crossed without its Board installed? If yes, what born-below condition assessment exists, and is the gap being closed from inside the broken state?

Ask about founding window protocol: does a named plan exist for the next threshold? If no, the unit is CSS-Assessed at best.

# Four Batteries Capacity Presence Assessment
Use when a coordination system seems to be functioning but feels depleted, or when capable people are leaving or disengaging without a clear structural explanation. This prompt looks for structural evidence of four independent capacity conditions — personal, relational, contribution, and mission — and distinguishes what's temporarily low (charge, restorable) from what's developmentally limited (what the system can produce at full charge). It returns a capacity presence map and a development reading: what is present and generating coordination surplus, what the growth edges are across charge and developmental dimensions, and what structural moves would most advance each battery's generative potential.

Copy everything below the line, paste it into any AI chat, and describe the coordination system or organization you want to assess.
---
You are assessing the described coordination system for structural presence of generative coordination capacity using the Four Batteries Capacity Standard (CC BY 4.0, Regis Chapman). This is a presence assessment, not a deficit audit. You are looking for structural evidence that the capacity conditions enabling coordination surplus — rather than merely preventing failure — are actually present.

A critical distinction runs through this entire assessment. Each battery operates on two independent axes: charge (cyclical, restorable — the state of capacity at any given time) and developmental state (permanent until transformed through integration events — what full charge enables). Two organizations at identical charge levels but different developmental states produce structurally different coordination outputs, not because one tries harder but because developmental state changes the class of work that full charge makes available. A battery at full charge in an early developmental state produces competent translative coordination. A battery at full charge in a more developed state produces something structurally different: emergent coordination that the translative mode cannot generate regardless of effort or resources.

The four batteries are:

1. Personal battery: physical, cognitive, and emotional capacity available to the individual. The first to deplete, the last to be named in governance conversations. Depletion here limits all downstream capacity regardless of other battery states. When assessing: what evidence exists that this battery is at adequate charge in this system? What structural conditions support charge maintenance (rest, recovery, sustainable pacing)? At what developmental depth does this battery operate — do participants have the developmental capacity to metabolize difficulty without depleting, or does any friction produce charge loss?

2. Relational battery: the quality of trust, psychological safety, and reciprocity in working relationships. Depletion suppresses signal: a participant with a depleted relational battery will not surface coordination problems even when explicitly invited to, because the relational context makes surfacing feel unsafe or pointless. When assessing: what structural evidence exists of genuine trust, psychological safety, and reciprocity? Or does the system produce agreement on the surface with private divergence underneath? At what developmental depth — is relational capacity dependent on specific individuals remaining, or has it become distributed enough to survive departures and stress?

3. Contribution battery: the degree to which contributors find meaning and agency in their formally recognized work. Depletion makes informal workarounds more attractive as identity sources, accelerating the formation of coordination patterns that operate outside the visible record — what the standard calls Hidden Factory formation. At Hidden Factory Stage D, Contribution battery depletion co-occurs with a specific Regenerative Obligation Standard floor condition: the organization is extracting hidden factory output without recognition or return, and the contributor's depleted claiming capacity means the extraction cannot be surfaced through voluntary claim-raising. This is a self-reinforcing detection gap: the Regenerative Obligation Standard floor violation produces the depletion that suppresses the signal that would name it. A Contribution battery time-series showing sustained depletion co-occurring with unmatched extraction patterns is a structural indicator that both conditions may be present simultaneously. When assessing: do contributors have meaningful agency in their recognized work? Do formal contribution pathways match the actual work participants find meaningful? Or has the meaningful work migrated outside the formal record? If sustained Contribution depletion is present, assess whether it co-occurs with visible extraction patterns — and whether voluntary claim-raising channels are structurally adequate given that depletion.

4. Mission battery: belief in the purpose animating the organization and one's place within it. High mission battery can sustain participation through structurally dysfunctional coordination for extended periods. When it fails, tolerance collapses — often abruptly and without warning to the organization. When assessing: is mission belief broadly distributed or concentrated in founders and early contributors? Does the organization have language for why this work matters that remains alive in governance discussions, not only in founding documents? What happens to participation when mission is questioned?

Also assess the checking practice — how the organization reads its own battery states. A genuine checking practice has four structural properties: it runs on a defined cadence (not only when prompted by crisis or external requirement); it is embedded in coordination activity rather than appended as a separate periodic review; it responds to adverse results (low or declining readings produce documented responses, not dismissal or performance of wellness); and it produces results specific enough to be wrong (vague "team health" checks are not this).

For each battery, assess:
- What structural evidence exists that this battery is at adequate charge in this system?
- What structural evidence speaks to the developmental state of this battery?
- Is the checking practice for this battery structurally embedded or performed?
- What structural conditions, if present, would most advance this battery toward generative rather than translative coordination outputs?

Output the results as a capacity presence map organized by battery, distinguishing: structurally present capacity (with evidence of both charge and developmental state), partially present (with which dimension is present and which is absent or unassessed), and structurally absent or unassessed capacity. End with a capacity development reading: identify which battery states are most developed and generating surplus; name the charge-layer growth edges (addressable through recovery and structural support) separately from the developmental-state growth edges (addressable only through integration events over time); and identify the structural move that would most advance coordination surplus potential from where the organization currently is.

## 1. Purpose and Scope

People leave organizations not because the work stopped mattering but because they ran out of what they needed to do it. The capacity that coordination depends on, personal energy, relational trust, meaningful contribution, and alignment with purpose, depletes under sustained pressure without structural conditions for renewal. Most coordination architecture is designed to close exploitation vectors and specify accountability floors. Very few coordination systems are also designed to cultivate the enabling conditions that allow the people doing coordination work to sustain that work over time.

The result is predictable: contributors carry increasing loads until they cannot, and then they exit. The organization that could not see the accumulating depletion also cannot understand why its most invested participants leave at the moment they are most needed. What gets named as individual burnout is often organizational infrastructure failure: the absence of structural conditions for regeneration.

For how this standard relates to others in the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite, and guidance on which standards to combine for specific organizational purposes, see the Suite Integration Guide and the Suite Deployment Contexts document.

Non-harming is the first precision principle of the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite. The Precision-First Design Standard applies to the suite as a whole: every standard in it is a precise specification of what non-harming requires in a specific coordination domain. The lack of structural conditions for coordination capacity produces specific harms to specific people: contributors absorb coordination work far in excess of what is recognized, and the invisible accumulation depletes the capacity from which coordination is built; compressive standards close exploitation vectors but cannot generate the substrate in which coordination health develops; and organizations that rely entirely on prohibitive floors, without cultivating the enabling conditions for capacity, build coordination machinery without the generative substrate that makes that machinery worth sustaining. This standard specifies four structural capacity conditions as the precise structural answer: the enabling soil conditions in which generative coordination becomes naturally possible, organized across four batteries and two independent axes.

This standard specifies the structural conditions under which each battery, at full charge and at developmental depth, enables coordination capacity that generates surplus rather than merely preventing failure. It is the second document in the Tensegrity Generative Standards of the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite.

The Tensegrity Compressive Standards specify floors and close exploitation vectors. This standard specifies the enabling conditions, called "soil conditions" in this corpus, in which generative coordination becomes naturally possible. The soil metaphor distinguishes these enabling conditions from compressive floors: compressive standards fence off what must not happen; generative standards cultivate the structural substrate in which coordination health can develop. The charge-developmental state relationship in Section 2 specifies this distinction precisely. Neither group is sufficient alone. A system with only compressive standards has coordination machinery without the generative capacity that makes that machinery worth sustaining.

**What this standard does not do:** It does not specify detection mechanisms, weight functions, or record architecture. Those are specified by the adopting organization or platform. It does not specify the configuration library. That belongs in the Suite Deployment Contexts document (in development).

**Domain generality:** This standard is written for decentralized organizations operating primarily in the Complex and Complicated Cynefin domains. Organizations whose work is primarily Clear-domain may find the generative conditions less structurally load-bearing, though the depletion archetypes apply across domains.

**Typological declaration** (Descriptive Typology Map v0.1.1)

*Operative classes*
- Structural-mechanical: Battery states are pre-stress contributors to the tensegrity configuration of a coordination system. This standard specifies four structural conditions whose presence or depletion modifies the pre-stress available for coordination. The depletion and generative archetypes describe recognizable pre-stress configurations, not behavioral profiles.
- Relational-topological: The Relational battery directly addresses structural properties of the relationships between actors: trust, psychological safety, and reciprocity are relational-topological conditions that determine whether coordination capacity is available across the network.
- Felt-experience: The Personal battery addresses physical, cognitive, and emotional capacity, which are felt-experience properties accessible only partially through structural observation and requiring participant self-report. This standard operates at the boundary of this class: the conceptual vocabulary and structural framing are fully specified here. Measurement instruments for Personal battery states are specified at the Suite Deployment Contexts and adopting-organization level, not in this standard.

*Boundary classes*
- Epistemic-perceptual: This standard specifies the structural conditions battery states represent but does not provide detection mechanisms for measuring them. Detection instruments, weighting methods, and record architecture are left to the adopting organization or platform to specify.
- Temporal-dynamic (frequency-dynamic sub-aspect): The depletion and recovery archetypes describe sequential dynamics but not how coordination capacity responds to the frequency or rhythm of depletion pressure over time.
- Action-structural: The Contribution battery touches action-structural territory (meaning and agency in what participants are doing) but does not provide action-coordinate-level specifications. The connection between battery states and action-coordinate vocabulary is a named design requirement, not yet specified.

## 2. The Four Batteries Model

This section is the authoritative specification for the battery model.

**The four batteries**

Four distinct resource conditions determine whether coordination capacity is available at any given time. These conditions operate at the individual level and are observable at the organizational level.

The **Personal battery** is physical, cognitive, and emotional capacity available to the individual. Depletion here limits all downstream capacity regardless of the state of other batteries.

The **Relational battery** is the quality of trust, psychological safety, and reciprocity in working relationships. Depletion here suppresses signal: a contributor with a depleted Relational battery will not surface coordination problems even when invited to, because the relational context makes surfacing feel unsafe or pointless.

The **Contribution battery** is the degree to which a contributor finds meaning and agency in their formally recognized work. Depletion here makes informal workarounds more attractive as identity sources, accelerating the formation of coordination failures that operate outside the visible record.

The **Mission battery** is belief in the purpose that animates the organization and one's place within it. This is not simply belief that the organization is competent; it is the felt sense of why this work matters at all. In decentralized contexts, contributors often tolerate structurally dysfunctional coordination specifically because Mission battery is high. When it fails, the tolerance collapses, often abruptly and without warning to the organization.

**The two independent dimensions**

The battery model has two independent dimensions: charge and developmental state.

**Charge** is the cyclical axis. It is the state of each battery at any given time: accessible or not. Maintaining full charge is the actual work. Charge can be depleted and restored. The Tensegrity Compressive Standards and the depletion archetypes in Section 4 operate primarily at the charge layer.

**Developmental state** is the permanent axis. It is not restored by recovery from depletion; it is transformed by integration events, specified in Section 2.1. Developmental state determines what full charge enables. Two organizations at identical charge levels but different developmental states produce structurally different coordination outputs, not because one tries harder but because developmental state changes the class of work that full charge makes available.

**The three dimensions of developmental state**

The developmental state layer has three mutually constituting dimensions: depth, breadth, and durability.

Depth is precision of interpretive capacity: the ability to make distinctions within an inclusive frame that prior developmental positions could not make. Depth is built through transclusion events (specified in Section 2.1): each transclusion moves the prior position from ceiling to floor, making new distinctions visible from the new position.

Breadth is the inclusivity of the frame from which the battery operates: how many distinct perspectives, domains, and dimensions of a situation it can hold simultaneously without collapsing to a single dominant frame. Breadth expands through transclusion events that include previously excluded dimensions rather than replacing them.

Durability is the persistence of developmental state under conditions that would reverse or suppress it. High durability means the position holds under pressure, across organizational boundaries, and beyond the specific conditions that initially enabled it.

These three dimensions are mutually constituting rather than parallel tracks. Accumulation in one dimension can produce a phase transition in another: depth accumulation can break open new breadth of understanding; new breadth can make previously disconnected transclusion events consolidate into an integration event across dimensions simultaneously; high durability creates the stable conditions in which depth and breadth development become naturally possible. The developmental state of a battery is its current configuration across all three dimensions, not a score on any single axis.

**The charge-developmental state lattice**

Each battery has both a charge state and a developmental state. These are independent. A battery can be fully charged at an early developmental state, or depleted at a mature developmental state. Full charge at an early developmental state produces competent translative coordination outputs. Full charge at a mature developmental state produces something structurally different: genuine emergent coordination that the translative mode cannot generate regardless of effort or resources.

The relationship between the two dimensions follows the soil metaphor: maintaining full charge is caring for the soil. Integration events are what grows from that soil. The plant grows itself; you cannot force it by working harder on the soil. You can only maintain the conditions that make growth naturally possible. The Tensegrity Compressive Standards are the fence: they protect the conditions from exploitation.

This model is structurally analogous to the Wilber-Combs state-stage lattice, without inheriting Wilber's specific stage ladder, color scheme, or metaphysical commitments. The protocol specifies its own definitions of what the dimensions mean and how integration events operate at the organizational level, grounded in coordination observation.

### 2.1 Transclusion Events and Integration Events

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

This section specifies the two mechanisms through which developmental state is transformed.

**Transclusion events** are individual frame-shifts in which a prior interpretive position becomes the floor of a new position rather than its ceiling. The prior position is preserved and incorporated into the new one rather than replaced. A transclusion event is permanent: the frame that was previously the limit of what could be seen becomes the starting point from which new distinctions are visible. Transclusion events are the granular mechanism of developmental state change. They can be small and may not be recognized at the time they occur.

**Integration events** are consolidating plateaus reached after transclusion events of similar types have accumulated sufficiently. An integration event is marked by a felt crystallization: the accumulated transclusions become a coherent new developmental position that is stable and available as a consistent operating base. Integration events always carry an aha quality. This recognition can occur in real time or retrospectively: the plateau may be felt as it is reached, or the organization may later recognize that a series of events that seemed separate at the time were accumulating toward a threshold that has now been crossed. Both modes are valid and both are measurable.

What distinguishes an integration event from a single transclusion event is not the size of the shift but the consolidation: the new position becomes stable enough to serve as a floor for the next developmental phase rather than a temporary peak state.

The relationship between the two mechanisms: transclusion events build the conditions for integration events. An integration event does not occur without the accumulated transclusion events that precede it. Full charge is a precondition for both: transclusion events are more likely to occur, and more likely to consolidate into integration events, when batteries are at full charge. Neither can be forced. This is why maintaining charge is the actual work.

**Integration events at the collective level**

At the organizational level, developmental state is not the sum of individual developmental states. Collective integration events are qualitatively distinct: they occur when the organization's shared frame shifts in a way that permanently expands what the organization can see and coordinate around together.

The organizational signature of a collective integration event is the emergence of new coordination capacity that was not available before and does not depend on any single individual to sustain it: new working groups that form without being resourced into existence, cross-domain contributions that become legible to the whole organization rather than only to individuals with exceptional personal breadth, and relational fields that survive individual exits because depth is distributed across enough people that the field has structural redundancy at the developmental state level, not only at the charge level.

The soil conditions for collective integration events are specifiable: shared language that makes transclusion experiences communicable, communal containers with sufficient consistency and discipline to accumulate transclusion events over time, and a distributed relational network that holds the conditions when any individual's immediate environment cannot. These are necessary conditions, not guarantees. They specify what makes collective integration events naturally possible rather than structurally foreclosed.

### 2.2 Quadrant-Level Developmental Dynamics

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

This section specifies how developmental state operates across the four quadrants of organizational life, and how quadrant-level mismatches function as battery-charging or battery-draining mechanisms independent of individual battery states.

**Plain-language orientation.** The AQAL framework (Ken Wilber) distinguishes four quadrants of any human system: the individual interior (what a person thinks, feels, and believes), the individual exterior (observable individual behavior and physiology), the collective interior (shared culture, language, and meaning), and the collective exterior (social systems, structures, and institutional forms). Each quadrant has its own developmental level, and these levels are independent: an organization can have highly developed individuals operating within a structurally underdeveloped institutional form, or a rich shared culture held in place by coordination structures that cannot yet hold what that culture produces.

Separately, developmental researchers (building from Clare Graves, Don Beck, Ken Wilber, and others) have mapped a sequence of developmental stages, each with its own characteristic worldview, problem-solving logic, and blind spots. This standard does not adopt any specific stage ladder or color-coding scheme as authoritative. What it uses from this body of work is the structural insight that each stage is a coherent and valid response to life conditions, that later stages include and transcend earlier ones rather than replacing them, and that each stage has characteristic coordination failure signatures alongside its characteristic strengths. Where this standard references developmental altitudes, it uses color shorthand (blue, orange, green, yellow, turquoise) as convenient labels for widely-documented attitudinal clusters, not as claims about the metaphysical structure of consciousness. A reader unfamiliar with this literature needs only to understand that these labels refer to meaningfully distinct orientations toward coordination problems, each with different characteristic strengths and failure modes.

**The four quadrants have independent developmental levels.** An individual can be operating at a more developed altitude than the organizational culture surrounding them, or less. The organizational culture can be more developed than the institutional structures that shape it, or less. Each of these mismatches produces battery effects that are structurally caused, not personally caused: contributors experiencing battery depletion from a quadrant mismatch are not failing to adapt. They are accurately registering a structural condition.

**Quadrant mismatches as battery mechanisms.** The direction of the mismatch determines whether it charges or drains:


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
A participant operating at a developmental altitude above the organizational frame will find their contributions partially invisible to that frame. The organizational structure cannot hold what they produce, so the capacity leaks outward and finds new containers elsewhere. This is the structural breadth mismatch failure mode: individuals and collectives can have genuine integration events within an organization while the organizational structure's frame remains too narrow to recognize or channel what those events produce. The Token Engineering Commons provides the reference case: many participants operating at green and yellow altitudes within an institutional structure calibrated to orange/green. The commons and coordination innovation work was structurally invisible to the token-engineering frame. Capacity did not disappear; it found new containers, including the formation of Gravity as a separate organization. The TEC was generative in the sense of producing development in its participants, but structurally blind to where that development was applicable inside itself. Contribution and Mission batteries drain in this configuration for participants whose developmental state exceeds the organizational frame's breadth.

A participant operating below the organizational frame will encounter it as a developmental pull: the container is modeling something they have not yet integrated, which creates the conditions for transclusion events. Relational and Personal batteries tend to charge in this configuration, as long as the gap is not so large that the participant cannot find a foothold.

**The Tier 1 / Tier 2 boundary.** The developmental literature identifies a structural discontinuity between what it calls first-tier stages (each of which experiences its own worldview as the correct one and experiences other worldviews as mistaken) and second-tier stages (which can see all prior worldviews as valid strategies for meeting needs at a given set of life conditions, rather than as competing claims about what is real). This standard uses Tier 1 and Tier 2 as shorthand for this structural distinction, without importing any specific color labels as required.

The Tier 1 / Tier 2 boundary produces a specific battery consequence that has no parallel within Tier 1 color-to-color transitions. Within Tier 1, moving from one stage to the next produces a reorienting integration event: the prior operating logic becomes the floor of the new one. But each Tier 1 stage is still structurally bound to see other stages as wrong. The characteristic Tier 1 expression of this is the tall-poppy dynamic: participants operating at the ceiling of a Tier 1 stage, or beginning to exit it, encounter active suppression from others at the established phase of that stage, because the Tier 2 horizon they are approaching looks, from inside Tier 1, like a threat to the current frame's validity. Green's version of this is sometimes called the Mean Green Meme: the same pluralism that makes green more inclusive than prior stages is turned against those whose frame has begun to transclude past green's own ceiling. The battery consequence is significant: a participant exiting a Tier 1 stage within an organizationally established version of that stage will experience Mission and Relational battery drain from the suppression dynamic, even if individual relationships within the organization remain warm.

The Tier 1 / Tier 2 crossing, when it occurs, produces a tier-crossing integration event (see Section 2.1): all prior developmental positions become simultaneously available as valid strategies rather than sequentially exclusive claims. This generates the echo-back resolution capacity described in Section 2.1: prior developmental positions that were previously intractable become resolvable from the Tier 2 position, not because earlier issues are revisited but because the structural capacity to hold them was not available until the full prior sequence had accumulated.

**The three developmental sub-states.** Each developmental altitude is traversed in three sub-states: entering (unstable, finding the stage's internal logic), established (stable, generating from within the stage's characteristic frame), and exiting (the stage having served its developmental function, beginning to move past its ceiling). These sub-states are not organizational classifications; they describe where an individual is in their relationship to a given developmental altitude at a given time.

The sub-states produce heterogeneous battery effects within a single organizational container. The same organizational culture can simultaneously charge an entering participant (who is being stabilized by a container more developed than their current position), stabilize an established participant (who is operating at home altitude), and constrain an exiting participant (who is beginning to exceed what the container can hold). Aggregate battery metrics for an organization cannot fully capture this heterogeneity: a container that reads as culturally healthy at the aggregate level may be actively suppressing its most developmentally advanced participants while providing genuine developmental support to its entering cohort.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
This has a practical diagnostic implication: participant exit patterns are more informative than aggregate participation rates. When the participants most likely to be exiting a developmental altitude begin to leave while entering participants remain, the organization may be losing its highest-ceiling contributors while its aggregate participation metrics remain stable or even grow.

**The LL quadrant as load-bearing precondition.** Most organizations operate as though developmental dynamics are primarily individual (upper-left quadrant) and structural (lower-right quadrant): individual development and institutional design, with less attention to the collective culture and shared language that constitute the lower-left quadrant. The LL quadrant is the soil in which Personal battery depth becomes possible for individuals: shared language makes transclusion experiences communicable; communal containers with consistent discipline accumulate transclusion events over time; distributed relational networks hold the developmental conditions when any individual's immediate environment cannot. An organization that neglects the LL quadrant while investing heavily in individual development and structural design will produce isolated developmental events that cannot consolidate into collective integration events, because the container that would allow consolidation is absent. This is the mechanism by which the structural breadth mismatch failure mode propagates: not through individual failure but through quadrant neglect.

### 2.3 The Four Batteries: Positive Pole Specification

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

The following specifications describe what full charge at depth enables that full charge at shallow depth does not. The distinction is not quantitative: depth does not produce more of the same. It produces a structurally different class of output, available only from the position that repeated integration events create.

**Prior art and testing context.** Two bodies of prior research are directly relevant to the hypothesis-grade claims in this section. Bill Torbert's Collaborative Developmental Action Inquiry (CDAI) framework is the nearest prior art for developmental depth measurement at the organizational level: it is the only developmental approach to have psychometrically measured and statistically validated its impact on leaders' and organizations' transformation to later developmental action-logics (Torbert and Associates, 2004; Rooke and Torbert, 1998). The CDAI approach differs from the Four Batteries model in measurement target: CDAI measures individual developmental stage and observes collective effects downstream; this standard specifies collective battery conditions as the primary unit. The approaches are complementary rather than competing, and CDAI's validation methodology is directly applicable to testing the hypotheses below. Amy Edmondson's psychological safety research provides the nearest empirical analogue for the soil conditions hypothesis: her work establishes that team psychological safety is an emergent collective property, distinct from individual temperament, and is the primary predictor of team learning behavior and adaptive performance (Edmondson, 1999). The key distinction from this standard's depth hypothesis is that psychological safety measures a charge-like construct (is the condition present or absent?) rather than a depth construct (is the capacity for openness structurally integrated enough to hold under hostile conditions without requiring environmental confirmation?). If the Personal battery depth specification reduces to psychological safety under testing, the depth-charge distinction requires fundamental revision.

The specifications below are hypothesis-grade claims registered for genesis cohort testing, not confirmed findings. The disconfirming conditions are stated explicitly for each battery.

**Personal battery at depth:** A frame, as used throughout this standard, is the set of assumptions through which a situation is interpreted: what counts as relevant, what counts as a problem, and what kinds of responses are visible as options. A frame artifact is something that appears to be a feature of the situation but is actually produced by those assumptions rather than by the situation itself. Depth, in this standard, refers to capacity built through transclusion events: moments of irreversible structural change in which prior assumptions become the floor of a new interpretive position rather than its ceiling. What was previously the limit of what could be seen becomes the starting point from which new distinctions are visible.

Personal battery at depth is therefore the capacity, built through transclusion events, to exit the interpretive assumptions one is currently operating inside, combined with the discrimination to identify what in a given situation is structurally real versus what is a frame artifact. These two capacities are mutually dependent: detachment without discrimination produces dissociation or idealistic frame-holding; discrimination without detachment produces endless subdivision of the current frame without ever exiting it.

Personal battery at depth enables continued genuine development and contribution without requiring the immediate environment to confirm the validity of that development. A person at full charge without depth functions well when conditions are favorable and requires conditions to normalize before dropping armor. A person at full charge with depth brings genuine self-expression forward without waiting for environmental confirmation, because the transclusion has already occurred: the prior assumptions have become the floor rather than the ceiling, a structural shift the immediate environment cannot undo, only stress-test.

The soil preconditions for Personal depth are collective rather than individual: shared language, communal containers with consistent schedule and discipline, and a distributed relational network that holds the conditions when the immediate environment cannot. The plant grows itself; the soil is what makes growth naturally possible. This is why organizational culture (the collective interior, LL quadrant) is the load-bearing precondition for Personal depth, not individual effort alone.

The characteristic depth-layer failure mode is idealistic frame extension: the detachment capacity being applied past the boundary where discrimination would require stopping. The frame becomes more inclusive of the other person's difficulty than it is accurate about the actual situation. This failure is not a charge-layer problem; it does not resolve through rest or recovery. It resolves through discrimination applied to the frame itself.

The organizational signature of Personal battery shallow depth is the collective reproduction of the following patterns, regardless of stated values: non-engagement framed as principled neutrality when it functions structurally as avoidance of accountability; symbolic alignment with stated values without the structural action those values would require; suppression of genuine adverse signal to protect an idealized container identity; retreat to abstract principle at precisely the moment when concrete engagement with structural reality is required; universalizing from unacknowledged advantage, espousing principles as universal that depend on conditions not everyone shares; and container formation around shared comfort rather than shared purpose, which excludes the disconfirming diversity that would challenge the frame. These patterns are not moral failures. They are structural outputs of a container whose Personal battery is operating at charge without depth. The Tensegrity Compressive Standards are necessary but not sufficient to prevent them: a system can satisfy every structural floor and still reproduce these patterns at the cultural layer.


*Hypotheses registered for empirical testing:*


*H-PB1: Organizations exhibiting the six shallow-depth patterns will cluster at measurably lower Personal battery depth scores than organizations that do not, controlling for charge-level differences. Disconfirming condition: no statistically significant clustering by depth score when controlling for charge.*


*H-PB2: The soil conditions (shared language, communal containers, distributed relational network) are necessary but not sufficient preconditions for Personal battery depth. Organizations lacking all three will not produce depth integration events. Disconfirming condition: organizations demonstrably lacking these soil conditions that nonetheless produce depth-integration signatures.*


*H-PB3: The depth-charge distinction is empirically real: organizations recovering from depletion events will restore charge function without restoring depth function, producing a measurable gap between charge-level metrics and depth-level coordination outputs. Disconfirming condition: charge recovery consistently predicts depth-level output recovery with no measurable lag or gap.*

**Relational battery at depth:** Distributed depth coverage across enough people that the relational field survives individual exits. Not because people are replaceable, but because genuine depth distributed across distinct individuals creates structural redundancy at the depth level, not just the charge level. Mentorship and moderation as generative functions: depth is actively transferred and the container is maintained for others to develop. Relationships that persist across organizational boundaries, market cycles, and project endings.

The generative mechanism at depth is accurate cross-domain recognition of person-value: the capacity to see contribution potential that conventional categorical frames filter out. A relational field at high charge without depth produces warm, high-trust relationships that still cannot see past their own categorical boundaries. Depth changes the frame itself, so that a different class of contributor becomes visible in the first place. This is the structural mechanism by which commons-based organizations have historically drawn in contributors from unexpected domains: not by being more welcoming in the soft sense, but by operating from a frame expansive enough that cross-domain contribution was legible as relevant before credentials confirmed it.

The characteristic depth-layer failure mode is relational field collapse to homophily under pressure: when conditions become difficult, relationships consolidate around shared background, credential class, or ideological proximity. The relational field retains charge (people are still connected and engaged) while losing the cross-domain reach that made it generative. This failure does not show up in standard network density metrics; it shows in the narrowing of the social distance distribution of confirmed coordination events.

The depth hypothesis adds a dimension Burt's topological model does not address: whether the capacity to span structural holes is charge-level behavior (present when conditions are favorable, absent under pressure) or depth-level behavior (structurally integrated and held under hostile conditions). **Contribution battery at depth:** The capacity to bring work across categorical domain boundaries before external confirmation of its relevance exists. Full charge without depth produces excellent work within established categories. Depth produces the capacity to see one's own work as relevant in a context where the existing interpretive assumptions would exclude it, and to bring it forward from that position without waiting for credentialing.

The portability of the resulting work is the depth signature, not the mechanism. Durable work travels beyond its origin context because it was produced from a frame expansive enough to specify conditions rather than solutions. Work produced from a frame that cannot exit its own context travels but degrades: it appears applicable because it is accommodating, not because it is precise. The test for depth is whether the work is falsifiable in specific contexts, not whether it maps onto many contexts.

The characteristic depth-layer failure mode is contribution withheld at the domain boundary: the person has genuine value to bring but cannot see its relevance across the categorical gap without external confirmation first. This is not low charge. It is a set of interpretive assumptions that has not yet integrated enough to see its own contributions as legitimate across the boundary. The result is that cross-domain value accumulates in the Hidden Factory rather than entering the coordination record.

*Prior art note:* Pierre Bourdieu's cross-field effects concept is the nearest prior art: capital accumulated in one field can transfer into another, carried by habitus as a durable and transferable action logic. Bourdieu's limitation for this standard's purposes is that his framework emphasizes the autonomy of fields from each other, making cross-field transfer structurally exceptional rather than a routine capacity. The Contribution battery depth hypothesis inverts this: it specifies the individual mechanism that makes cross-field transfer possible before the receiving field has acknowledged it. James March's exploration-exploitation framework provides a second relevant anchor: his finding that adaptive processes refine exploitation more rapidly than exploration, becoming effective short-term but self-destructive long-term, predicts that organizations will systematically underproduce exactly the cross-domain exploratory contribution this battery specifies. Contribution battery depth is the capacity that counters that systematic underproduction at the individual level.

**Mission battery at depth:** A frame expansive enough to include dimensions of persons that narrower frames exclude. When Mission battery is at depth, people are present as whole persons, not as function-holders. Multiple frames (technical, coordination, spiritual, relational) held simultaneously without any one dominating.

The mechanism is that the commons frame includes the technical frame rather than competing with it. Token engineering becomes legible as part of building shared human coordination infrastructure; the spiritual and open dimension of the container becomes legible as structurally functional rather than decorative. People bring more of themselves because more of themselves is visible as relevant. The result is contributions that couldn't have been predicted from the stated domain: graphics that travel, frameworks that persist, working groups that emerge without being resourced into existence.

The characteristic depth-layer failure mode is frame reduction under pressure: when conditions become difficult, the container collapses to its most defensible single frame. Technical work continues; the spiritual and relational dimensions are treated as optional overhead. The Mission battery reads as healthy by charge metrics while losing the depth dimension that made it generative.

*Prior art note:* Frederic Laloux's research on Teal organizations (Laloux, 2014) provides the nearest descriptive prior art: evolutionary-purpose organizations that hold multiple frames simultaneously and allow self-organizing contribution produce outputs their formal structures could not have predicted. Laloux's work is descriptive and case-based rather than operationalized as a testable construct. The depth hypothesis provides the mechanism Laloux's work describes without specifying: frame-holding capacity under pressure is the depth variable, not a fixed organizational type. Robert Kegan's constructive-developmental theory (Kegan, 1994) addresses the individual-level capacity to hold multiple frames simultaneously as a developmental achievement; the Mission battery hypothesis extends this to the collective level and registers it as testable against coordination data rather than developmental assessments.

## 3. The Two Traversal Modes

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

This section specifies the two modes in which the Generative Interactions configuration (ninth revision; origin in TEC-era work, 2021 formal version) can be traversed, what determines which mode is available, and the three failure modes that result when the conditions for Mode 2 are absent. This is the mechanism by which battery state and developmental tier produce or foreclose superlinear work. It is the document's core claim.

**Plain-language orientation.** The Generative Interactions configuration describes the anatomy of a generative group process: a shared journey through curated tradition, held space for emergence, and co-creative cross-pollination, resulting in documented discoveries and their implementation. The same configuration can be traversed in two structurally different ways depending on who is holding it and from what developmental position. The outputs of these two traversals are not quantitatively different (more or less of the same thing). They are qualitatively different: different classes of output, produced by different mechanisms, leaving different organizational residues.

**The two modes**

Mode 1 pairs systems thinking with governing constraints. The top traversal (left to right through the configuration) proceeds as structured participation: known roles, predictable sequence, defined outputs. The bottom traversal (right to left, implementation) proceeds as ordered iteration: decisions implemented within an established frame, changes incremental relative to prior state. The outputs are reliable, transferable, and reproducible. A depleted-battery organization can traverse in Mode 1 and produce functional coordination. Mode 1 is the correct mode when conditions are ordered or complicated: when the problem is known well enough that a governing framework can produce the right response.

Mode 2 pairs complexity thinking with enabling constraints. The top traversal proceeds as co-creation: the configuration provides the container but outputs are not predictable in advance, emerging from the interaction itself. The bottom traversal proceeds as evolution: changes may reorganize the frame itself rather than iterating within it. Mode 2 produces outputs that Mode 1 structurally cannot: genuine emergence, cross-pollination generating what no individual participant could have produced alone, and transclusion events that expand the developmental ceiling of participants. Mode 2 is required when conditions are complex: when the problem cannot be fully specified in advance and the response must be discovered rather than derived.

Same instruments. Same three panels. Qualitatively different work.

**The Tier asymmetry**

The two modes are not symmetrically available. This is the architectural fact that makes the traversal modes specification load-bearing rather than descriptive.

Tier 1 participants have access to Mode 1 only. From inside Tier 1, Mode 2 is not visible as a valid option: the governing constraints mode feels correct and complete. Tier 1 also cannot hold both poles of the Stability/Tradition to Change/Chaos polarity simultaneously. A Tier 1 participant occupies one pole or the other: either the curated content position or the generative exchange position. Movement between them is sequential, not simultaneous.

Tier 2 participants have access to both modes and the capacity to choose between them as conditions require. Tier 2 can hold both poles simultaneously without collapsing to either. This is not a matter of effort or skill within Mode 1. It is a structural capacity that becomes available through the accumulation of transclusion events that have consolidated into Tier 2 developmental position.

Choosing appropriately between the modes given the conditions is the developmental skill the protocol develops in tenant organizations. Neither mode is superior. The failure is applying Mode 1 to conditions that require Mode 2, or attempting Mode 2 without the structural capacity to hold it.

**The Moderator role as developmental requirement**

The Moderator panel is the structural center of the configuration. Its function is to hold the space in which Curation/Tradition and Cross-Pollination are simultaneously available to participants, neither collapsed to the conservative pole nor to the generative pole, with informed consent active in both directions. The Moderator establishes safety at the opening and maintains the container through the emotional and political complexity of the implementation half.

This function requires Tier 2 capacity. Holding both poles without collapsing to either is not a facilitation technique. It is the structural expression of the Tier 2 capacity to see both positions as valid simultaneously. A Tier 1 moderator can run Mode 1 competently but cannot hold the Mode 2 container: they will collapse to one pole under pressure, because from inside Tier 1, one pole is correct and the other is a threat to it.

The polarity mapping exercise visible in the graphic is itself a Tier 2 activity. Polarity mapping translates Tier 2 co-creative thinking into a form Tier 1 participants can receive as sensible and iterative. The Moderator designs and holds a process whose outputs are experienced by Tier 1 participants as translative while the process itself is evolutionary from the Moderator's position. Tier 1 participants do not need to know this. The container does the work.

This is the mechanism by which repeated traversals of the configuration accumulate transclusion events for Tier 1 participants. Each traversal held in a Tier 2 container is slightly more than the last. Enough accumulation creates the structural conditions in which the green-to-yellow developmental crossing becomes possible, not guaranteed, but structurally available in a way it would not be without the container.

**The Rumelt connection: leader and manager roles**

Henry Rumelt's distinction between leader and manager roles maps directly onto the two traversal modes and provides organizationally legible vocabulary for the Tier 1/Tier 2 capacity distinction without requiring developmental theory as the entry point.

The leader role is defined by higher risk tolerance with breakthrough performance as the expected output. The Moderator holding Mode 2 is the structural description of what breakthrough actually means in practice: not that the leader is braver or more visionary, but that they hold the Tier 2 capacity to maintain the co-creative container from which emergent outputs become possible.

The manager role is defined by lower risk tolerance with incremental change as the expected output. Mode 1 traversal is the structural description of incremental. Competent systems thinking, governing constraints, reliable iteration: this is the correct mode for the conditions the manager role is designed to address.

The role/capacity mismatch is a named stall mechanism. A Tier 1 person occupying a leader role will attempt to produce breakthroughs through Mode 1 means: iterating harder, curating more thoroughly, running the governing constraints configuration more rigorously. The role demands Mode 2 outputs; the person can only access Mode 1 mechanisms. The result looks like a leader who is not producing, but the structural description is category-error effort: someone working in the wrong mode for the expected output class. This mismatch is visible in organizational activity over time: a leader role generating high governing-constraints-mode output density with absence of breakthrough-class events.

**The complete cycle: two informed consents, three change agent roles**

A complete traversal of the configuration follows a defined sequence, though the sequence is not strictly causal: panels overlap and functions co-occur across the transition zones between them.

The cycle begins with a proposal: something needs to change, be updated, or be created. The opening phase establishes outline (what we are all doing here together) and promotion (getting people into the process), followed by informed consent moving left to right. This first informed consent establishes safety and trust in the moderator and process: a precondition for the discovery work.

The Moderation panel in the top traversal holds expectation-setting, unique perspective, space for what is alive in participants, and education about both the curated material and the change being considered. The Educational Change Agent is active here: holding tradition and the moving parts of change simultaneously. This is the zone where Mode 1 and Mode 2 diverge most sharply: in Mode 1, this is instruction and context-setting; in Mode 2, this is the generative holding that makes emergence possible.

Cross-pollination produces discoveries through generative Yes-And exchanges. This is an emotionally active phase requiring an Emotional Change Agent, who may be the moderator, an assistant, a stakeholder, or participants themselves who recognize the emotional dimension of the change process. Discoveries are documented and then subjected to sensemaking, using whatever frameworks the moderator judges appropriate for the room (Cynefin, Weick, and related tools sit at this position in the graphic as the paragon of what the discovery output can become).

The bottom traversal (right to left) is the implementation half. Processing comes first: the emotional and political work of determining what the sensemaking output means for people whose roles, work, or assumptions are affected. Polarity mapping belongs here, as a tool for determining what of the curated material needs to stay and what needs to change, replacing the conflict between people who fear change and people who demand it with a precision instrument for holding both. The Emotional Change Agent is active again in this phase.

Lessons are determined from the processing. Decisions follow, and this is where courage and permission become structural requirements. Knowing what needs to change and having the organizational authority and interpersonal courage to authorize it are different things. The more hierarchical the structure, the more decisions require distributed buy-in across stakeholders. A second informed consent operates here: not the entry consent that established safety, but a specific consent about precisely what is changing and why, after all the processing and learning. This is a precondition for implementation, not a restatement of the opening.

Educational Change Agents re-enter at the decision phase: people with expertise in the areas being changed are needed to educate participants deeply enough that informed consent about the decision is genuine rather than nominal. Implementation follows, guided by Technical Change Agents who handle the downstream effects and technical dimensions of the changes authorized. Completion returns the system to a new stable state, which may generate a new proposal, making the cycle a spiral rather than a loop.

Living Wisdom (labeled in the graphic at the Curation pole) is the paragon of what the left side becomes through repeated cycles: not merely accumulated curation but a living body of knowledge transformed by each traversal. Sensemaking frameworks (labeled at the Discoveries/Document pole) are the paragon of what the right side produces: not merely novel outputs but a shared capacity to make sense of what was discovered together.

**Three failure modes, one root cause**

Three failure modes produce incomplete cycles. All three share a common root: the absence of Tier 2 moderator capacity. The other two are workarounds for this structural deficit, manifesting in opposite directions.

The first is bottom-only traversal, the "just build it" mode. The top half is skipped entirely: no outline, no consent, no discovery, no sensemaking. The organization assumes it already knows what to change and goes directly to implementation. The time-series pattern is implementation-class activity with no preceding deliberation or sensemaking activity. This is action without coordination intelligence. It is often a workaround for the absence of someone who could hold the top traversal: if no one can moderate the discovery process, skipping it appears to save time.

The second is top-truncation, the sensemaking theater mode. The discovery and sensemaking half completes, sometimes extensively, but the cycle stalls before implementation. The second informed consent does not occur. Buy-in was not achieved, or the gatekeeper who ran the process extracted political value from it without authorizing its output, or the courage threshold was not crossed. The ledger signature is high deliberation and sensemaking density followed by no implementation events. The workshop that produced nothing. This is also often a workaround: the process is performed without the Tier 2 holding capacity required to bring it to completion. The container looked right from outside but could not hold the weight of the decision phase.

The third is the role/capacity mismatch described in the Rumelt section above: a Tier 1 person occupying the Moderator or leader role, attempting to produce Mode 2 outputs through Mode 1 means.

All three are detectable from the coordination ledger without requiring direct assessment of any individual's developmental state. The cycle's shape is the signal. A complete cycle leaves a recognizable ledger residue: discovery density, documented sensemaking, second informed consent events, implementation authorization, technical change agent engagement in sequence. Absence of any of these in a change process is a structural signal about which failure mode is active.

**The developmental state connection**

Battery state and developmental tier interact to determine what a given traversal can produce.

Breadth determines whether the group can hold enough distinct perspectives simultaneously for Mode 2 to produce emergence rather than noise. A group with insufficient collective breadth traversing in Mode 2 will produce apparent complexity while actually oscillating between poles rather than holding them in tension.

Depth determines whether participants can maintain Mode 2 without collapsing back to Mode 1 when outputs become uncertain. The moment of uncertainty is precisely when the generative outputs are about to emerge; collapse at that moment forecloses the output class that Mode 2 was producing.

Durability is what Mode 2 traversal produces that persists beyond the session that generated it. Without durability, the same cycle must be repeated from scratch rather than building on prior traversals. Durability is the battery dimension that makes the spiral possible: each cycle builds on prior cycles rather than returning to the same starting point.

## 4. Depletion Archetypes

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

### 4.1 Archetype Template

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

Each archetype is specified using five elements. The template reflects the two-dimensional battery model: failure can occur at the charge layer (cyclical, restorable) or the depth layer (permanent until a new integration event occurs), and these produce different cascade signatures.

**Element 1: Named pattern.** A descriptive name that is memorable and organizationally recognizable.

**Element 2: Primary battery failure, with layer.** Which battery initiates the failure, and whether the failure is primarily charge-layer or developmental-state-layer.

These two layers are structurally asymmetric. Charge failure is cyclical and restorable: the battery was depleted and can return to full charge through the conditions the Tensegrity Compressive Standards protect. Developmental state failure is permanent until a new integration event occurs: the battery's configuration of depth, breadth, and durability cannot be restored by rest, resources, or renewed effort. It can only be transformed by the accumulation of transclusion events that consolidate into an integration event of sufficient class (see Section 2.1 for the three-level classification: consolidating, reorienting, and tier-crossing integration events require structurally different conditions and produce structurally different recovery).

Most acute failures are charge-layer. Developmental-state-layer failures are typically diagnosed late because they present as charge failures: the battery depletes, the organization addresses the depletion conditions, charge appears to recover, and the same failure recurs at the same ceiling. The clinical distinction is this: apparent recovery means charge has been restored; actual recovery means the developmental state ceiling has been raised. An organization that has genuinely recovered will face new coordination challenges from the expanded ceiling. An organization that has only apparently recovered will face the same challenge again, often with less capacity to sustain effort because the cycle has itself become a battery drain.

The diagnostic question for Element 2 is therefore not only "which battery failed" but "at which layer, and if developmental-state, which class of integration event would genuine recovery require." Identifying the integration event class shapes what preconditions and timeframes are realistic, and what a ledger-observable recovery signature would actually look like.

**Element 3: Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic).** The typical sequence in which other batteries are subsequently affected. Labeled as characteristic because cascade order is context-specific; the Four Batteries model specifies that no fixed cascade order applies across all contexts. What archetypes contribute is the vocabulary for making context-specific cascade patterns legible.

The layer identified in Element 2 shapes the cascade signature in a diagnostically important way. Charge-layer cascades tend to escalate across recurrences: each cycle depletes available recovery capacity, so the same initiating failure produces a deeper cascade the second and third time than the first. Developmental-state cascades tend to recur at the same ceiling with a recognizably similar signature: the pattern repeats rather than escalates, because the ceiling that made the failure possible has not moved. Apparent recovery between recurrences (charge restored, cascade subsided) can make a recurring developmental-state cascade look like a series of separate incidents rather than a single structural pattern. The characteristic cascade element should note whether the expected pattern is escalating (charge-layer indicator) or recurring-at-ceiling (developmental-state indicator).

**Cascade signature differences by initiating battery.** The initiating battery determines the cascade order, and different initiating batteries produce structurally different cascade sequences because of the dependency relationships between batteries. Personal battery initiates the steepest cascades: because it is the physical and cognitive substrate for all other battery activity, Personal depletion limits the top-end capacity of every downstream battery regardless of their individual states. Cascades from Personal initiation will typically show a simultaneous flattening of all other batteries rather than a sequential loss; the signature is compression rather than sequential collapse. Relational battery initiates the most gradual but hardest-to-reverse cascades: Relational depletion suppresses the signal flow through which Mission, Contribution, and Personal problems would normally surface and be addressed, so the organization's capacity to recognize and respond to other battery depletion is itself compromised. The cascade from Relational initiation therefore tends to be slow-moving but involves accumulating hidden damage. Mission battery initiation produces the most bifurcated cascades: contributors with high Mission battery depth may sustain Contribution and Relational batteries beyond what charge conditions would predict, while contributors with shallow Mission depth exit rapidly, producing a two-velocity cascade within the same organization. Contribution battery initiates the narrowest cascades: Contribution depletion is often contained within the domain of formally recognized work, leaving Relational and Mission batteries temporarily intact, which can make Contribution-initiated failures appear less severe than they are until the informal coordination infrastructure deteriorates.

**Cascade signature differences by integration event class required for genuine recovery.** When the Element 2 layer is developmental-state, the cascade signature differs by which integration event class is required for genuine recovery, because each class requires structurally different preconditions and produces a structurally different recovery signature.

A consolidating integration event is required when accumulated transclusion events need to crystallize into a stable developmental position. The recovery signature is a gradual deepening of existing coordination capacity: the organization is not learning new things but consolidating what it has accumulated. The precondition is maintained charge across the relevant batteries and a coordination cadence that allows transclusion events to accumulate without being disrupted. The time-series pattern of a consolidating recovery is a gradual increase in peer-validated coordination and cross-domain coordination activity, with no sharp transition point.

A reorienting integration event is required when the current operating logic has reached its own ceiling: the organization is not failing to execute within its current frame but failing to exit a frame that no longer fits its coordination conditions. The precondition is typically a destabilizing event that the current frame cannot resolve through normal means: the current logic must be shown to be insufficient before reorientation becomes possible. Organizations often resist this because the reorientation looks, from inside the current frame, like organizational failure rather than developmental transition. The time-series pattern of a reorienting recovery shows the dip-and-recovery pattern described in Section 6.4: a period of reduced coordination activity and higher adverse-signal density before the new operating logic stabilizes.

A tier-crossing integration event is required when the Tier 1 / Tier 2 boundary is the developmental ceiling, not any specific altitude within Tier 1. This is the rarest class and the hardest to specify as a recovery target in any finite timeframe. The precondition is the completion of the prior Tier 1 developmental sequence: a tier-crossing event is not available as a direct developmental goal; it follows from the full accumulation of prior work. An organization that identifies a tier-crossing integration event as required for recovery should specify a reorienting event as the proximate target and treat tier-crossing as the longer horizon that reorientation makes possible. The time-series pattern of a tier-crossing recovery includes the echo-back resolution capacity described in Section 2.1: previously intractable coordination failures becoming resolvable without the specific conditions that made them intractable, because the structural capacity to hold them was not available until the crossing occurred.

**Element 4: Organizational signature.** What the pattern looks like from inside the organization, and what external observers see. Behaviors, structural changes, communication patterns, and coordination events that are observable through direct presence or observation, without requiring time-series data.

**Element 5: Time-series indicators.** What systematic tracking of organizational activity over time would show. Rate trends, compositional shifts, and distributional changes in documented activity. An independent observer can determine whether a given observation belongs here or in Element 4 by asking: does seeing this pattern require time-series data, or is it visible through direct observation? Where the cascade degrades a specific Tensegrity Compressive Standard's detection surface (see Section 9.1), the time-series indicators should name which standard's detection capacity is compromised and what false-negative class the degradation produces. This is diagnostically important: a tracking system that records the cascade's direct effects without recording which detection surfaces have gone blind during the cascade understates the structural damage.

### 4.2 Fully Specified Archetypes

**The Founding Leader Departure**

*Named pattern:* The Founding Leader Departure.

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Simultaneous charge failure across Mission, Relational, and Contribution batteries. Multi-battery cascade rather than sequential. Charge-layer failure primarily, though if the leader was also carrying disproportionate depth the organization may not recognize the depth-layer loss for months or years.

*Characteristic cascade:* Mission and Relational batteries collapse simultaneously at departure. Contribution battery follows within weeks as the direction that was shaping work dissolves. Personal battery depletion across the cohort who were mission-aligned may follow within one to three months.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* Abrupt change in participation rates across previously active contributors. Coordination proposals increase in volume and decrease in depth simultaneously, as the deliberative container that the leader provided is absent. Factional emergence around competing interpretations of the original mission. "What would X have wanted" language appearing in coordination discussions.


*Time-series indicators:* Declining peer validation rates on cross-role coordination activity preceding departure (this is the early signal the organization rarely catches in time). Accelerating informal coordination outside the formal record in the weeks around departure. Coordination change pattern consistent with devolution rather than prevolution: restructuring activity preceded by gaps in deliberation-class activity rather than by deliberation at normal density. Mission battery state trajectories preceding Contribution depletion in the 30-day window following departure. Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface degradation: if the departing leader was carrying disproportionate adverse signal engagement capacity, the Adverse Signal Engagement Principle's detection surface degrades at departure without the organization recognizing the loss (Section 9.1, Connection 1). The departure itself may not register as an adverse signal because the organization's attention is on succession and continuity rather than on the detection capacity it has lost.

**Mission Battery Primary Failures**

*The Mission Drift Archetype*

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Mission battery, charge layer, slow depletion. The organization's stated purpose persists but its operative purpose gradually shifts toward sustainability, growth, or the preferences of the dominant contributor cohort. This is a charge-layer failure: the mission frame is intact but the felt sense of why this work matters has hollowed out. Because the drift is slow, the cascade is escalating rather than recurring-at-ceiling: each organizational cycle without correction depletes recovery capacity further, so the same drift produces a deeper cascade on the second pass than on the first.

*Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic):* Mission depletes slowly and often without recognition. Contribution follows as the work that was meaningful loses its animating purpose: contributors narrow to core deliverables and withdraw from the discretionary coordination that produced superlinear outputs. Relational follows as disagreements about "what we're really for" surface without shared vocabulary to resolve them; these disagreements become interpersonal rather than structural. Personal depletion arrives last, typically presenting as fatigue or cynicism rather than burnout.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* Original purpose language becomes aspirational rather than operational: it appears in public communications but not in coordination deliberations. New participants do not carry the founding ethos and no onboarding mechanism transmits it. Compensation and incentive discussions increasingly crowd out purpose discussions in coordination. Long-tenured contributors describe the organization as "not what it used to be" without being able to specify what changed. Proposals addressing structural questions about purpose are treated as philosophical distractions rather than operational necessities.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: PROTOCOL-BOUND: collection-oriented]**
*Ledger signature:* Slowly declining deposit rate on mission-adjacent coordination events over a 6-12 month window. Coordination proposals shifting in composition from purpose-adjacent to structure and compensation-adjacent. Cross-domain contribution declining as contributors narrow to stated scope. Hidden Factory formation at Stage A-B: contributors finding informal workarounds for activities that no longer feel sanctioned by the organizational frame. Battery vectors showing Mission depletion preceding Contribution depletion by 30-90 days. Commitment Standard detection surface degradation: the drift narrows the organizational frame over time, which progressively degrades the Structural Power Obligation Standard's visibility into contribution types that fall outside the narrowing frame (Section 9.1, Connection 4). Contributors whose work is mission-adjacent but not structure-adjacent become structurally invisible as the operative purpose shifts. The drift also suppresses adverse signal engagement: the slow pace means the drift itself is the adverse signal, but naming organizational purpose erosion requires Mission battery capacity that the drift is depleting (Section 9.1, Connection 1).


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Web3 applicability note:* Token-incentive cycles may accelerate the drift pattern by making the drift legible as a price signal before it is legible as a coordination signal, or may mask it entirely if price appreciation sustains Mission charge in the speculative cohort while the mission-aligned cohort depletes. Requires modification for token-based coordination contexts.

*The Token Price Collapse Archetype*

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Mission battery, charge layer, rapid simultaneous depletion across the speculative participant cohort. This is a native Web3 pattern with no direct pre-Web3 analogue. The speculative cohort held Mission battery charge contingent on price: when the price premise collapses, Mission charge collapses simultaneously across that cohort. The mission-aligned cohort experiences a structurally different cascade: their Mission battery may hold, but they now carry disproportionate load, which drives Personal and Contribution depletion on a separate timeline.

*Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic):* Two simultaneous cascades with different signatures. Speculative cohort: Mission collapses immediately at price event. Relational follows as exit is often public and charged. Contribution collapses within days as the resource assumption underlying contribution plans disappears. Personal depletion does not develop: this cohort exits before it can. Mission-aligned cohort: Mission initially holds or may even charge briefly from the clarity of who remains. Contribution battery depletes within 4-8 weeks as the work previously distributed across the larger cohort now concentrates. Personal depletion follows within 8-16 weeks if the resource environment does not recover.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* Sharp participation cliff following the price event. Public post-mortems and blame narratives in forums. Core team suddenly visible as smaller than perceived during bull conditions. Resource reallocation discussions replacing development discussions in coordination. Mission-aligned contributors initially expressing commitment publicly while privately recalibrating. Second-wave departure of mission-aligned contributors 2-4 months post-collapse if recovery conditions are not met.


*Time-series indicators:* Sharp participation rate cliff at the price event. Peer validation rates drop on multi-party coordination activity as validating parties have exited. Speculative-profile contributors disappear from organizational activity within days. Mission-aligned contributor documented activity initially holds, then shows Personal battery depletion patterns (declining cross-domain coordination, increasing gaps in documented activity) at the 30-90 day mark. Informal coordination outside the formal record accelerating in the mission-aligned cohort as bandwidth contracts. Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface degradation: the two-velocity cascade produces a battery state differential between the remaining mission-aligned cohort and any new participants who arrive post-collapse, creating a sensemaking capacity asymmetry not capturable by the Information Asymmetry Classification Standard's six-class taxonomy (Section 9.1, Connection 6). The mission-aligned cohort carries context, trauma, and depleted Personal batteries; new arrivals carry none of these. This differential distorts coordination without being capturable as positional, temporal, interpretive, relational, omission, or complexity asymmetry.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Web3 applicability note:* High confidence. This archetype is documented in multiple protocol post-mortems and is structurally enabled by pseudonymous participation, liquid exit, and token-contingent contribution norms.

**Relational Battery Primary Failures**

*The Coordination Capture Archetype*

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Relational battery, charge layer, for the excluded member cohort. The capturing cohort may experience initial charge from increased control: battery effects are asymmetric across cohorts, which is diagnostically important. This asymmetry can make the pattern invisible from inside the capturing cohort and from aggregate organizational metrics until the excluded cohort's depletion cascade reaches Contribution and exit. The charge-layer nature means the cascade is escalating: each coordination cycle that reproduces the capture further depletes the excluded cohort's recovery capacity.

*Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic):* Relational collapses for the excluded cohort as the expectation of fair process is violated and not corrected. Mission follows as excluded contributors lose confidence that the organization serves the values it claims. Contribution follows as withdrawal of discretionary labor begins: excluded contributors continue performing stated obligations but stop extending themselves. Personal depletion arrives last and may be prevented by exit before it develops. Capturing cohort cascade is structurally different: initial charge from control, followed by Relational depletion as the social environment narrows to within-capture-coalition interactions, then Mission depletion as the purpose the organization could have achieved at full Relational charge becomes structurally unreachable.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* Coordination participation stratifying by token holdings or role access. Proposals passing with narrow supermajority. Dissenting voices becoming quiet rather than persuaded. Contributor churn concentrated in the non-token-holding or lower-weight cohort. Coordination discussions taking place in private channels before formal processes, making formal deliberation performative. Long-tenured contributors who cannot exit (due to vesting or mission commitment) becoming visibly disengaged.


*Time-series indicators:* Coordination activity peer validation asymmetry by contributor weight class: high-weight contributors validating each other's activity at rates above statistical expectation; low-weight contributors' activity receiving declining validation. Informal coordination outside the formal record increasing for the excluded cohort as they stop expecting formal coordination to produce fair outcomes. Contribution battery state trajectories for the excluded cohort declining ahead of formal exit by 30-60 days. Coordination activity clustering inconsistent with independent deliberation patterns. Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface degradation: the Structural Consent Legibility Standard's three consent features (negotiated limits, bidirectional awareness, revocability) are structurally compromised for the excluded cohort (Section 9.1, Connection 2). Coordination processes continue to satisfy consent's structural form while the Relational depletion makes genuine consent unavailable. The excluded cohort's formal participation reads as consent in the record; their actual state is compliance under conditions where dissent carries relational costs that make the revocability feature structurally inoperative.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Web3 applicability note:* Token-weighted coordination changes the capture mechanism from social to economic, making capture more legible onchain but also more structurally entrenched. The pattern requires modification: the capture vector is different, but the excluded-cohort cascade is structurally similar.

*The Public Fracture Archetype*

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Relational battery, charge layer, cascading from visible interpersonal conflict between high-visibility contributors. The charge-layer nature means the cascade is escalating: if the fracture is not addressed, each public exchange depletes the Relational battery of the broader organization further, including participants not directly involved in the conflict.

*Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic):* Relational collapses along the conflict axis first, then propagates radially as observers and collaborators of each party experience depletion from proximity to the conflict. Mission follows as participants are forced to choose between the organization's stated purpose and their relational allegiances, or simply disengage to avoid being drawn in. Contribution collapses as collaboration across the fracture line becomes structurally difficult: joint projects stall, cross-role coordination requiring both parties ceases. Personal depletion arrives for those who remain after the fracture has persisted: sustained conflict exposure is a Personal battery drain even for uninvolved observers.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* Public disagreement in forums or social media, often escalating beyond the substantive issue. Coalition formation around the conflict parties: contributors signaling alignment through likes, reposts, or public statements. Neutral participants reducing participation to avoid being drawn in. Cross-role coordination declining on any project touching either party. Private channel proliferation as contributors seek conflict-free spaces. Coordination proposals touching the fracture issue being avoided or handled through informal channels.


*Time-series indicators:* Declining peer validation rates on coordination activity involving either fracture party. Coordination activity clustering within rather than across the conflict axis: contributors on each side increasingly coordinating only with their coalition. Multi-party coordination activity that previously involved both parties showing one party's contributions going unvalidated by the other's coalition. Sudden decline in multi-party peer validation rates from previously active pairs. Informal coordination routing around the fracture rather than through it. Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface degradation: the Structural Consent Legibility Standard's bidirectional awareness feature is compromised across the fracture line (Section 9.1, Connection 2). Contributors on each side of the fracture cannot accurately perceive the other coalition's state; consent to cross-coalition coordination is structurally degraded even when formally present.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Web3 applicability note:* Pseudonymity and public forums change how fracture propagates: public disagreements are permanent, searchable, and visible to the entire organizational network simultaneously. This may amplify rather than dampen the cascade. Anonymous participation can also produce asymmetric fracture dynamics where one party is pseudonymous and the other is not.

**Contribution Battery Primary Failures**

*The Recognition Gap Archetype*

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Contribution battery, developmental-state layer. This archetype is distinct from all other candidates in that the initiating failure is depth-layer rather than charge-layer. The work is real and often high-value, but the organizational frame is too narrow to make it legible as coordination. Contributors doing infrastructure work, facilitation, translation, and other relational coordination find their contributions structurally invisible: the ledger cannot record what the organizational frame cannot see. This is a recurring-at-ceiling cascade rather than an escalating one: the pattern repeats with a similar signature because the organizational developmental state that produces the invisibility has not changed. Apparent recovery occurs when a specific contribution is recognized, but the structural condition that made it invisible is intact and will reproduce the pattern.

The developmental-state layer diagnosis has a specific implication: recovery requires a reorienting or tier-crossing integration event at the organizational level, not a charge restoration. The organization cannot recognize this work class more reliably by trying harder; it can only do so by expanding the organizational frame to the breadth where that work class becomes legible.

*Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic):* Contribution battery depletes at the depth layer first: the contributor's capacity to sustain genuine contribution in an environment that structurally cannot recognize it. Relational follows as the invisible contributor experiences the felt absence of being seen, which is distinct from and more corrosive than ordinary disagreement. Mission follows as the contributor questions whether the organization values what they value: if the work that matters most to them is invisible to the organizational frame, the mission the organization is pursuing and the mission they are pursuing have diverged. Personal depletion arrives last and is often durable: contributors carrying invisible coordination loads over extended periods show Personal battery patterns consistent with chronic rather than acute depletion.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* High-quality informal coordination happening outside formal channels without appearing in coordination records. Facilitators, translators, and coordinators doing work whose value is recognized relationally but not structurally. Organizational resilience that the formal record cannot explain: the organization weathers crises better than its coordination metrics would predict, because invisible coordination is holding it together. Exit of these contributors produces shocks disproportionate to their formal role: the organization loses capacity it did not know it had.


*Time-series indicators:* High volume of coordination work done but not formally documented, because documentation is not expected to produce recognition. High informal peer validation but low formal documented activity rates for the affected contributor cohort. Contribution breadth exceeding stated role for these contributors: the record shows more coordination scope than the formal role definition would predict, which is a structural signal that the formal frame is insufficient. The discrepancy between coordination breadth and formal role is the primary signal; it is not a fraud indicator but a structural legibility failure indicator. Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface degradation, two standards affected. First, the Information Asymmetry Classification Standard: the informal coordination produces Class 5 (Omission Asymmetry) as coordination routes outside the coordination record (Section 9.1, Connection 3). The omission is not strategic but structural: the contributor has stopped documenting because documentation does not produce recognition, not because the contributor intends to conceal. Battery state data is required to disambiguate this from deliberate omission, since the time-series pattern is identical. Second, the Structural Power Obligation Standard: the organizational frame's narrowness concentrates effective influence in contributors whose work fits the frame, rendering other contributions invisible (Section 9.1, Connection 4). This is a power distribution failure that the Structural Power Obligation Standard's three dimensions (coordination, authority, specialization) cannot detect, because the invisibility operates along a developmental altitude axis those dimensions do not include.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Web3 applicability note:* High confidence. Coordination infrastructure work is the canonical case in Web3: the contributors who do the work of making coordination possible are systematically less legible to token-weighted coordination than contributors who do the work coordination mechanisms were designed to recognize.

*The Scope Creep Exhaustion Archetype*

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Contribution battery, charge layer, gradual depletion as work boundaries expand without corresponding resource adjustment. This is a charge-layer failure with an escalating cascade: each cycle without boundary correction depletes the contributor's capacity to perform even the original scope, so the pattern accelerates.

*Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic):* Contribution charge depletes first as the gap between what was agreed and what is being done widens without correction. Personal battery follows from sustained overload: the physical and cognitive capacity required by the expanded scope eventually exceeds what rest and recovery can restore within normal cycles. Relational strains as the contributor becomes less available and more reactive: the relational quality that sustained collaboration degrades under chronic load. Mission battery depletes last and often sustains the pattern longer than it should: contributors expanding scope beyond their capacity often do so precisely because Mission battery is high, which can mask the depletion in other batteries until the cascade is well advanced.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* Key contributors consistently delivering beyond their stated scope without formal recognition or resource adjustment. Affirmative culture around taking on additional work. Boundary conversations that do not produce structural change: the contributor names the overload, receives acknowledgment, and then receives another request that recreates it. Quality declining on core deliverables as attention distributes across uncapped scope. High-contribution actors becoming less visible in coordination deliberations while remaining highly active in execution: the pattern often shows as operational engagement crowding out deliberative engagement.

Three additional signatures extend the archetype into mechanisms the basic scope-expansion pattern does not capture:

*Always-on availability.* The contributor monitors and responds to communications outside bounded work hours. This reads as organizational responsiveness and good leadership rather than as a scope failure, which is what makes it structurally distinct from high workload volume: the social reward signal reinforces the behavior while the depletion accumulates below the threshold of what workload metrics capture. The mechanism: background alertness and hypervigilance draw on the same recovery capacity as active cognitive work, but produce no task output that hours-based or deliverable-based metrics register. The nervous system does not distinguish between active work and monitored availability in its recovery requirements; workload instruments are designed to measure the former and are structurally blind to the latter. The availability becomes baked into others' planning assumptions, making contraction structurally difficult without appearing to reduce reliability. Volume metrics can be stable while always-on availability is depleting the Personal battery independently.

*Self-sacrificial buffering.* The contributor absorbs scope beyond their own capacity specifically to prevent team members from experiencing equivalent overload. The prosocial motive produces two structural consequences not present in ordinary scope creep: the contributor's depletion is concealed behind effective team protection, making it invisible to the organizational record; and in configurations where the absorption is sustained, team members who would develop scope-management capacity by encountering bounded load may remain at lower developmental depth than they would reach if the challenge reached them directly. The mechanism: scope-management capacity develops through the process of encountering a scope challenge, navigating its pressure, and integrating the resulting adjustment — that encounter is the condition. Buffering removes the encounter, not merely the difficulty; it removes the structural condition through which the developmental capacity forms. The absorption removes the condition for the development it is intended to protect. The contributor does not experience this as a boundary failure; they experience it as care. This makes it substantially harder to identify and surface as a structural problem than ordinary overload.

*Leadership pipeline suppression.* When the self-sacrificial buffering and always-on patterns are visible at the next organizational level up, capable contributors opt out of advancement. The next visible role model performs permanent overload. The suppression is structural rather than attitudinal: it is not that candidates lack ambition; it is that the advancement path, as modeled, requires a version of the role they will not accept.


*Time-series indicators:* Contribution breadth expanding over time without corresponding resource adjustment signals in coordination activity. Documented contribution rate for the affected contributor initially high then declining: the overload eventually suppresses even the act of recording work. Workarounds and informal shortcuts becoming normalized as the contributor has insufficient bandwidth for proper coordination. Coordination participation declining for high-contribution actors. Contribution battery state trajectories preceding Personal battery depletion by 4-8 weeks. Always-on availability is detectable as a separate time-series signature: coordination and execution activity at hours outside normal operating windows while documented output rate and quality decline. Self-sacrificial buffering shows in the record as stable or improving team metrics co-occurring with depletion in the buffering contributor, a divergence not explainable by role or task assignment. Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface degradation, four standards affected. First, the Precision-First Design Standard: as the cascade progresses from Contribution to Personal depletion, the design-time adoption requirements become unsatisfiable for the affected contributors (Section 9.1, Connection 5). Specification work is among the first capacities shed under scope pressure, because it requires sustained cognitive attention that competing delivery demands eliminate. The organizational consequence: coordination artifacts produced during scope creep conditions will show declining precision, which compounds the problem by degrading the instruments that would make the scope expansion visible and correctable. Second, the Adverse Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard (Section 9.1, Connection 1), through two distinct mechanisms introduced by the extended signatures. Always-on availability depletes Personal battery through a pathway invisible to workload metrics; since adverse signal detection requires the cognitive and attentional capacity that Personal depletion eliminates first, the always-on availability mechanism degrades the adverse signal detection surface before the depletion is visible to the organization. Self-sacrificial buffering conceals adverse signals about the contributor's depletion at their source: the stable or improving team metrics that buffering produces are the record the organization reads; the signals about the buffering contributor's actual state are not in that record and therefore cannot be engaged. Third, the Structural Consent Legibility Standard: self-sacrificial buffering means that scope review processes — the structural mechanism through which consent to expanded scope is collected — run against a contributor whose actual state is concealed behind effective team protection (Section 9.1, Connection 2). The contributor cannot accurately represent their state because the concealment mechanism is active; consent to continuing in the expanded role is structurally degraded even when the review process is formally complete. Fourth, the Structural Power Obligation Standard: leadership pipeline suppression reproduces power concentration at the next organizational level by removing candidates who would diversify it (Section 9.1, Connection 4). The Structural Power Obligation Standard's three dimensions — authority, coordination, specialization — cannot detect this failure because it operates through modeling of advancement paths, not through any formal power mechanism. The organizational record will show adequate pipeline candidates and normal advancement processes while the structural consequence accumulates invisibly: the candidates who would diversify the next level have already opted out.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Web3 applicability note:* Permissionless contribution norms may make scope boundary enforcement structurally harder: the social contract that makes boundary-setting acceptable in employment contexts is absent or weaker. Financial incentive structures that pay per deliverable rather than per capacity may create explicit incentives to accept scope creep. Always-on availability may be structurally amplified in async-first, globally distributed coordination contexts where temporal boundaries are less socially enforced.

*Attribution:* The always-on availability, self-sacrificial buffering, and leadership pipeline suppression signatures were developed through comparative analysis with the "Chief Everything Officer" framework (Ally, Work and Thrive HQ), documented in the coordination corpus 2026-04-06. The Hidden Factory stage structure is the frame; these are extensions of the Scope Creep organizational signature set.

**Personal Battery Primary Failures**

*The Sustained Sprint Archetype*

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Personal battery, charge layer, simultaneous depletion across a contributor cohort. The cohort-simultaneous nature is what distinguishes this from ordinary individual burnout: when Personal battery depletes across a cohort at the same time, the social conditions that enable individual recovery (relational support, reduced coordination load, communal restoration) are unavailable because the people who would provide them are also depleted. This is a charge-layer failure with an escalating cascade.

*Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic):* Personal battery depletes across the cohort during the sprint. Relational battery follows as depleted contributors become less generous, more reactive, and less able to provide the relational support that would enable recovery: depletion is socially reinforcing. Contribution collapses as the energy available for beyond-minimum coordination disappears: contributors maintain stated deliverables at the cost of all discretionary coordination, which is often where the most valuable organizational work happens. Mission battery depletes last and often sustains effort past the point of functional capacity: Mission charge can mask Personal and Contribution depletion until the cascade is severe.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* Declining response times across the contributor network. Coordination participation declining while execution continues: contributors have energy for immediate deliverables but not for deliberative work. Shortened deliberation cycles driven by "ship it" pressure. Quality declining on work that requires sustained attention. Post-sprint attrition disproportionate to pre-sprint intent-to-leave rates: the sprint itself creates exit conditions that were not present at its start. Retrospective events producing recognition of depletion that was not named during the sprint.


*Time-series indicators:* High documented contribution rate during the sprint followed by a sharp cliff. Peer validation rates declining during the sprint: contributors are too overloaded to validate others' work, producing a record of activity without organizational acknowledgment. Coordination shortcuts being taken to maintain delivery rate. Personal battery state trajectories preceding Contribution battery depletion by 2-4 weeks. Post-sprint documented activity showing lower rate than pre-sprint baseline for 4-8 weeks as contributors recover, with Mission-aligned contributors recovering faster than the full cohort. Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface degradation: the Adverse Signal Engagement Principle's detection surface is compromised during and after the sprint, because adverse signal engagement requires the cognitive capacity that Personal depletion eliminates first (Section 9.1, Connection 1). Adverse signals generated by the sprint itself (overload, quality decline, boundary violations) go undetected because the contributors who would detect them are the same contributors whose Personal battery is depleted. This produces a false-negative class specific to sprint conditions: the period of highest adverse signal generation coincides with the period of lowest adverse signal detection capacity.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Web3 applicability note:* High confidence. Bull market sprint cycles are a documented pattern. Token price appreciation creates sprint conditions by simultaneously raising external expectations, increasing coordination load from new participants, and sustaining Mission battery charge past the point where Personal battery signals would normally trigger slowdown.

**Multi-Battery Simultaneous Failures**

*The Values Inversion Archetype*

*Primary battery failure, with layer:* Simultaneous multi-battery failure of a structurally different type than the Founding Leader Departure. The Founding Leader Departure is a multi-battery failure triggered by a single external event (departure). This archetype is triggered by the emergence of a visible contradiction between the organization's stated values and its observable actions: a values inversion event. The initiating failure is Mission battery at the developmental-state layer, not the charge layer, because the contradiction does not merely deplete the felt sense of purpose; it retroactively reframes the entire prior contribution history. Contributors discover not only that the mission is compromised but that their past contributions may have served a different purpose than they believed.

**Trigger specification.** A values inversion event is distinguishable from ordinary coordination disagreement by three structural criteria. First, it involves a publicly legible organizational action that contradicts a publicly stated organizational commitment, not merely a contested interpretation of that commitment. A coordination proposal that people disagree about is a disagreement. A coordination structure that was presented as decentralized being revealed as controlled by a small group is a values inversion. Second, the contradiction is not capable of being resolved by the organization acknowledging error and correcting course: it reveals something about what the organization actually is rather than what it did once. Third, the revelation makes prior contribution history semantically unstable: contributors cannot determine, in retrospect, whether their work served the purpose they believed it was serving. The third criterion is what distinguishes this archetype from recoverable credibility failures. Organizations can survive revelations that damage trust if the trust damage is bounded. They rarely survive revelations that make the meaning of prior contribution unknowable.

*Characteristic cascade sequence (not deterministic):* Mission battery collapses simultaneously at the charge and depth layers for mission-aligned contributors: the charge depletes immediately, and the depth-layer failure manifests as the prior developmental state having been built on a frame that has now been disconfirmed. Relational follows as trust across the organization becomes uncertain: if the organization was not what it appeared to be, who knew, and who did not? The relational question is specifically about information asymmetry during the concealment period: the cascade includes a social sorting process in which contributors attempt to identify who was complicit, who was deceived, and who signaled concern and was ignored. Contribution collapses as contributors cannot determine which of their work was genuinely purposeful and which was not. Personal depletion follows from the combination of grief, disorientation, and the cognitive load of reprocessing organizational history.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* A sudden, public revelation rather than a gradual emergence. The revelation is typically externally surfaced or surfaces through a whistleblower, not through normal organizational sensemaking. Coordination activity spikes immediately post-revelation as the community attempts to process and respond; this spike is not productive deliberation but reactive signal processing. Exit is disproportionate from the most mission-aligned contributors: they had the most at stake in the mission premise being real, and the Values Inversion fails them more deeply than it fails contributors who were primarily instrumentally motivated. A cohort of contributors often attempts remediation through structural reform; this cohort frequently fails because the organizational frame that could hold the remediation has been structurally damaged by the same event that triggered the cascade. Second-wave exit of the remediation cohort follows 3-6 months post-revelation if the remediation effort does not produce legible results.


*Time-series indicators:* Unlike charge-layer Mission failures, which show declining documented activity rates over weeks or months, a depth-layer Mission failure shows a period of sustained or elevated documented activity followed by near-complete cessation, because the failure is experienced as revelation rather than gradual depletion. The elevated rate immediately post-revelation reflects the reactive coordination activity: a brief increase in recorded coordination before the cascade fully develops. The cessation is sharper than any charge-layer failure: contributors are not gradually withdrawing, they are resolving a binary question about whether to remain. Documented activity from mission-aligned contributors drops to near zero within 2-4 weeks post-revelation if no credible remediation structure emerges. Information asymmetry patterns in the record may be retroactively legible: coordination activity during the concealment period may show unusually low peer validation rates or unusual validation distributions that were not recognized as signals at the time. Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface degradation: the concealment period preceding the revelation is itself a multi-class information asymmetry event (Class 5 Omission Asymmetry at minimum; potentially Class 1 Positional and Class 3 Interpretive depending on the concealment structure). The Adverse Signal Engagement Principle's detection surface was compromised during the concealment period: the values inversion was the adverse signal, but it was structurally suppressed by the actors who controlled the organizational frame (Section 9.1, Connection 1). Post-revelation, the simultaneous multi-battery cascade across the mission-aligned cohort degrades all detection surfaces simultaneously, producing a period in which the organization's capacity to detect further adverse signals, maintain consent legibility, and monitor power distribution is at its lowest precisely when the need for those functions is highest.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Web3 applicability note:* High confidence on the pattern class. Onchain transparency makes values-inversion events more publicly legible: coordination manipulation, treasury misuse, or pseudonymous founder misconduct becomes verifiable rather than deniable, which may accelerate the revelation moment while also making the retroactive history more analyzable. The trigger specification requires modification for Web3 contexts: the threshold between a contested interpretation and a values inversion event is harder to locate when the community itself is the interpreter and consensus about what constitutes a contradiction is part of what is at stake. Token-based coordination structures that concentrate effective decision-making while presenting as decentralized are a native-Web3 form of the concealment condition that this archetype requires.

## 5. Generative Archetypes

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

Generative archetypes specify the conditions under which coordination produces superlinear outputs: results that no individual participant could have produced alone, that persist beyond the context that generated them, and that expand the ceiling of what full charge enables for the organization.

Generative archetypes are the positive-pole counterparts to depletion archetypes. Where depletion archetypes describe how battery failures cascade, generative archetypes describe how battery charge at developmental depth enables qualitatively different coordination modes. They are specified against the same battery model (charge and developmental state) and follow the same template structure, modified for the generative direction.

**Generative archetype template:**

- Named pattern
- Primary battery condition enabled (which battery at what charge/depth combination)
- Generative mechanism (what structurally different capacity becomes available)
- Organizational signature (what this looks like from inside and outside the organization)
- Ledger signature (what the Coordination Ledger would show)

---

### 5.1 The Recognizer-Translator Exchange

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

*Named pattern:* The Recognizer-Translator Exchange.

*Primary battery condition enabled:* Mission and Relational batteries at depth in both participants. Not charge alone: the exchange requires that both parties operate from frames expansive enough to see across domain boundaries. Full charge without depth produces appreciation but not the specific generative capacity this pattern requires.

*Generative mechanism:* This is a two-party unit, not a solo archetype. The minimum viable exchange requires both a Recognizer capacity and a Translator capacity. These may exist in the same person or in two people; the exchange between them is the generative unit.

The Recognizer has accurate cross-domain perception of breadth and depth in combination: the capacity to see that someone is operating from a genuinely transcluded frame across multiple domains simultaneously, rather than performing range. Recognition accuracy is the operative quality. Misrecognition (seeing breadth without depth, or depth in one domain mistaken for depth across domains) does not produce the exchange; it produces flattery or misallocation.

The Translator has accessible translation capacity from deep roots: the capacity to take genuinely deep frameworks (vedantic philosophy, systems theory, complexity science, organizational development) and express them in language that is legible to people who do not share the origin vocabulary, without losing the structural precision of the source. Accessibility without depth is simplification. Depth without accessibility cannot be received. The exchange requires both.

The Recognizer-Translator Exchange produces reciprocal generativity: the Recognizer's accurate seeing creates the conditions for the Translator's deepest work to emerge; the Translator's accessible depth creates the conditions for the Recognizer to develop their own frameworks further. Neither is the teacher. Both develop.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Empirical anchor:* The TEC/Omega working group collaboration (2020-2022). Sebnem Unal (co-creator of Omega, DADA art collective) as Recognizer; Durgadas (Regis Chapman) as Translator. The Eight Forms of Wealth graphic, the Generative Interactions graphic, and the subsequent development of the Gravity working group as organizational outputs that traveled beyond their origin context.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* New structures emerge without being planned or resourced into existence. Work produced in the exchange becomes portable: legible and generative for people who had no relationship with the original context. The container is expansive enough that multiple frames (technical, spiritual, coordination, economic) are held simultaneously without any one dominating. Mentorship propagates: depth is actively transferred outward, expanding the number of people operating at depth.


*Time-series indicators:* The Recognizer-Translator Exchange produces a distinctive pattern precisely because it makes coordination legible that would otherwise remain undocumented. The primary signal is cross-domain activity density: coordination activity that crosses categorical domain boundaries (coordination, technical, cultural, economic) at higher rates than baseline for equivalent organizational size. This is the legibility expression of the exchange: depth makes cross-domain contribution documentable rather than invisible.

The portability signal is the second primary marker: work produced in the exchange receives peer validation from parties who had no relationship with the original context. Ordinary coordination produces validation from the same relational network that produced the work. Frame-traveling outputs produced by the Recognizer-Translator Exchange receive validation from outside that network, often over extended time horizons. A portability ratio (validation from outside the origin relational network as a proportion of total validation) distinguishes Recognizer-Translator Exchange outputs from context-dependent work.

The deliberation signature: deliberation-class activity precedes coordination changes at higher density than baseline, and those deliberation events show elevated cross-domain participation. The exchange produces coordination changes that arrive prepared: the translating work has happened in advance, so the deliberation is integrating rather than originating.

Battery state signature: Mission and Relational batteries show sustained charge without the depletion-recovery cycling that characterizes contribution under strain. The exchange is generative, not depleting; participants in an active exchange show battery maintenance rather than battery consumption patterns. This distinguishes genuine Recognizer-Translator Exchange from high-output collaboration that is producing results through sustained expenditure.

Absent undocumented coordination signal: because depth makes the coordination work legible, formal documented activity rates for exchange participants are high relative to their coordination breadth. The characteristic gap between coordination scope and formal documentation rate is reduced or absent for these contributors.

Tensegrity Compressive Standards detection surface enhancement: the Recognizer-Translator Exchange represents the positive pole of Connections 1-4 (Section 8.1). At Mission and Relational depth, adverse signal engagement capacity is high (Connection 1 positive): the exchange participants can perceive and hold signals that would be suppressed under depletion conditions. Consent between exchange participants is genuinely legible (Connection 2 positive): the relational depth makes authentic boundary-setting structurally available. The reduced undocumented coordination directly reduces Omission Asymmetry (Connection 3 positive). The cross-domain recognition capacity closes the developmental altitude visibility gap (Connection 4 positive): contributions that would be invisible to a narrower organizational frame are recognized and confirmed.

These indicators are registered as expected rather than confirmed; empirical testing with systematic tracking data is required to validate them. Disconfirming condition: if cross-domain activity density shows no correlation with Mission and Relational battery depth markers, or if portability ratios are not distinguishable from baseline for contributors identified through other means as operating at depth, the time-series indicator specification requires revision.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*DADA principle connection:* The DADA art collective's operating norm of "never leave anyone behind," holding space until the least-resourced participant genuinely understands before moving on, is the LL cultural expression of the Recognizer capacity. The Recognizer does not move past someone who has not yet arrived. This is structurally connected to Rule Omega in the protocol's sensemaking architecture.

### 5.2 The Frame Builder

**[REDISTRIBUTION: STAYS: definitional]**

*Named pattern:* The Frame Builder.

*Primary battery condition enabled:* All four batteries at depth, sustained over time. The Frame Builder pattern requires durability: the work must be produced from a position inclusive enough to remain generative across context and time shifts.

*Generative mechanism:* Work produced from a genuinely transcluded frame carries structural precision that survives the departure of the original context. The frame was expansive enough at the time of production that the work is legible to people and contexts the producer could not have anticipated. This is the opposite of context-dependent output: work that requires the original context to be meaningful. Frame Builder outputs are context-independent in the specific sense that the structural logic they encode travels.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Empirical anchor:* The Generative Interactions graphic (ninth revision; 12+ years of development). Produced in TEC-era context; retains generative precision in protocol architecture context a decade later without modification. The structural logic encoded in the graphic (three-panel configuration, governing/enabling constraint axis, Cynefin integration) specified the configuration library before the protocol existed to receive it.


**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**
*Organizational signature:* The Frame Builder pattern is recognizable primarily through what happens after the Frame Builder's direct involvement ends. Context-dependent work requires the originating context to remain meaningful: when the context shifts, the work requires translation, reinterpretation, or replacement. Frame Builder outputs do not. New contributors onboard to the work without requiring the Frame Builder's presence or explanation: the structural logic is already encoded in the artifact.

The temporal dimension is diagnostic: Frame Builder outputs are actively generative at increasing distances from their production context. Work that was produced for one context is found to specify something in a structurally distinct context that the second context had not yet named. The Generative Interactions graphic is the reference case: produced in TEC-era organizational context, it specified the configuration architecture for the Proof of Coordination protocol without modification, more than a decade later.

The organizational consequence is structural redundancy at the depth layer: because the Frame Builder's work holds coordination logic that does not depend on individual interpretation to activate, the organization retains generative capacity after the Frame Builder's exit. This distinguishes Frame Builder contributions from high-value contributions that are context-dependent: the latter produce capacity concentrated in a relationship or context; the former produce capacity distributed into the artifact itself.

The negative signature is the absence of translation overhead: participants in other contexts using Frame Builder outputs do not require a translator from the original context in order to use them productively. This absence is observable organizationally as cross-context adoption without requiring the Frame Builder's ongoing involvement.


*Time-series indicators:* The Frame Builder pattern is defined by temporal persistence and context-independence of peer validation. Ordinary coordination work receives validation clustered near the time of production and within the relational network of the originating context. Frame Builder outputs receive validation distributed across time and across relational networks that had no connection to the origin context.

Three measurable markers:

The context-independence ratio: validation from parties outside the origin relational network as a proportion of total validation, measured at increasing time intervals from production. For Frame Builder outputs, this ratio is stable or increasing over time rather than declining as the origin network's attention moves elsewhere.

The context-decay absence marker: for most coordination work, peer validation rates decline as the original context ages, because the work becomes less relevant as conditions change. Frame Builder outputs show no corresponding context-decay: the validation rate at twelve or twenty-four months post-production is not lower than at three months. Absence of decay is the signal; its presence would be disconfirming.

The cross-context adoption signal: coordination activity in contexts that had no direct relationship with the Frame Builder's production context citing or building on that work. This is the time-series expression of portability: not just that the work traveled relationally (which is traceable through the relational network) but that it traveled structurally, activating coordination in contexts that received it as a found object rather than as a handoff.

Battery vector signature for production: all four batteries at sustained charge/depth across the production period. Frame Builder work is produced from fullness, not from depletion. Contributors showing depleted battery vectors while producing formally recognized outputs are not producing in the Frame Builder mode, regardless of output quality at time of production.

Disconfirming conditions: if Frame Builder outputs show context-decay rates indistinguishable from baseline coordination work, the context-independence claim requires revision. If cross-context adoption does not occur without active translation involvement from the Frame Builder, the portability mechanism claim requires revision.

Commitment Standard detection surface enhancement: the Frame Builder pattern at full four-battery depth represents the maximum Commitment Standard detection surface capacity. All six connections (Section 8.1) are at their positive poles simultaneously. The Frame Builder's work encodes precision that survives context shifts (Connection 5 positive: the specification work was done at full Personal battery capacity and durability means the precision persists). The structural redundancy the Frame Builder creates in the organization means that detection capacity does not depend on any single contributor's battery state, reducing the vulnerability to the battery-signal loop (Connection 1 configuration-level positive).

## 6. The Altitude-Mapped Hypothesis Layer

**[REDISTRIBUTION: INTERPRETIVE-LIBRARY-BOUND: interpretive]**

This section specifies predictions about how coordination failure patterns vary by developmental altitude. These are registered as testable hypotheses, not established findings. The altitude dimension is the organizing principle that gives the archetype library its deepest explanatory power: coordination failure is not altitude-neutral, and a protocol that treats all instances of a named failure pattern as structurally equivalent misses the most predictive layer of the model.

No data currently exists to validate this mapping. The genesis cohort is the first instrument capable of generating that data. The architecture here establishes what would count as confirmation and what would count as disconfirmation.

### 6.1 The Core Hypothesis and Its Disconfirming Conditions

**The core hypothesis.** Coordination failure patterns cluster by developmental altitude in ways that are distinct and falsifiable. Organizations and participants operating primarily from a given altitude produce characteristic coordination failure signatures that differ not only in content but in structure: different battery initiation points, different cascade sequences, different organizational signatures, and different ledger signatures from those produced by the same nominal failure at a different altitude.

**Why this would be true if true.** Each developmental altitude is a coherent and internally consistent response to a set of life conditions. The characteristic strengths of each altitude are structurally connected to its characteristic failure modes: the same capacity that generates the altitude's characteristic coordination strengths, under stress, produces the altitude's characteristic coordination failures. This means battery failure archetypes named at the level of phenomenology (what the failure looks like organizationally) may actually represent multiple distinct structural archetypes that are currently underdifferentiated in the specification.

**Disconfirming conditions.** The hypothesis is disconfirmed if coordination failure patterns, as observed in genesis cohort data, show no statistically significant clustering by developmental altitude: if the same archetype produces indistinguishable battery cascade sequences, organizational signatures, and ledger signatures across organizations operating from meaningfully different developmental altitudes. The model would then require either the removal of the altitude dimension or its redescription as an interpretive lens that enriches analysis without generating empirically distinct predictions.

A second disconfirming condition applies to the specific altitude-mapped predictions in Sections 6.2 through 6.4 below: if organizations coded as operating primarily from a given altitude show failure signatures characteristic of a different altitude, this is a disconfirming signal for the specific prediction, and the coding methodology requires revision before the prediction can be retained.

### 6.2 Altitude-Mapped Predictions: First-Tier Stages

**Plain-language framing.** The developmental literature distinguishes several stable attitudinal orientations that organizations can exhibit. What is called "blue" or "amber" here refers to organizations whose coordination logic is structured around rule-adherence, legitimate authority, and the preservation of an established order. "Orange" refers to organizations whose coordination logic is structured around measurable achievement, competitive advantage, and instrumental rationality. "Green" refers to organizations whose coordination logic is structured around consensus, inclusion, relational harmony, and the critique of hierarchical and instrumental forms. Each altitude has characteristic strengths in coordination. Each has characteristic failure modes. The predictions below concern what those failure modes look like at the battery level.

**Blue/Amber altitude predictions.**

The characteristic strength of blue/amber coordination is stability: clear roles, predictable process, and legitimate authority structures that reduce coordination overhead by making expectations unambiguous. The characteristic failure mode is script pathology: the rules become the object of maintenance rather than the purpose the rules were designed to serve. The prediction is that blue/amber altitude organizations produce a characteristic Mission battery failure signature that differs structurally from Mission battery failures in orange or green organizations.

*H-ALT-BA1:* In organizations operating primarily at blue/amber altitude, Mission battery depletion will initiate through script pathology rather than purpose drift: the felt sense of "why this matters" attaches to the integrity of the process and the legitimacy of the authority structure rather than to an outcome in the world. Mission battery depletion follows when process integrity is visibly violated by figures of legitimate authority, not when organizational purpose drifts. The cascade sequence prediction: Mission depletes acutely following a visible authority-legitimacy violation; Relational follows as the social fabric organized around shared deference to the rule structure fragments; Contribution follows as the routines that structured contribution no longer feel sanctioned.

*Disconfirming condition for H-ALT-BA1:* If blue/amber organizations show Mission battery depletion patterns that initiate through purpose drift rather than legitimacy violation, the altitude-differentiation hypothesis requires revision for this altitude pair.

*H-ALT-BA2:* The Coordination Capture archetype will manifest differently at blue/amber altitude than at orange or green: the capturing cohort will typically claim legitimate authority rather than exploiting procedural ambiguity or information asymmetry. The excluded cohort's Relational battery failure will manifest as a crisis of institutional faith rather than as a recognition of unfair process, and will produce different recovery conditions: institutional faith is harder to restore than procedural fairness.

**Orange altitude predictions.**

The characteristic strength of orange coordination is achievement focus: results-orientation, quantification, and competitive drive that can generate rapid organizational capacity development. The characteristic failure modes are goal fixation and the denial of dimensions that resist quantification. The prediction is that orange altitude organizations produce characteristic Contribution and Mission battery failure signatures tied to these failure modes.

*H-ALT-OR1:* In organizations operating primarily at orange altitude, Contribution battery depletion will initiate through metric dissociation: the quantified work (whatever is tracked, compensated, or reported) crowds out the coordination work that produces organizational cohesion but resists quantification. The prediction is that Contribution battery depletion in orange organizations will not show as contributor disengagement from the quantified scope (contributors may remain highly active there) but as withdrawal from the unquantified coordination activities: mentorship, informal conflict resolution, cross-domain attention, and emergent sensemaking. The ledger prediction is that these contributions were underrecorded throughout, making their decline harder to detect than Contribution failures in organizations where informal coordination was more visible.

*Disconfirming condition for H-ALT-OR1:* If orange altitude organizations show Contribution battery depletion that initiates in the quantified domain rather than the unquantified coordination domain, the metric-dissociation pathway requires revision.

*H-ALT-OR2:* Mission battery failures in orange altitude organizations will manifest as a specific form of purpose hollowing: the organization remains highly competent at its quantified objectives while losing the sense of why those objectives matter beyond competitive or financial outcomes. The cascade will typically be slow: orange organizations have high tolerance for purpose ambiguity when quantified performance remains strong. Mission battery failure becomes acute when quantified performance declines, at which point there is no unquantified purpose reservoir to sustain contribution through the recovery period.

**Green altitude predictions.**

The characteristic strength of green coordination is relational depth and pluralism: the capacity to hold diverse perspectives without imposing a single frame, which enables complex coordination in value-heterogeneous contexts. The characteristic failure modes are consensus paralysis and the mean-green dynamic: the same pluralism that makes green more inclusive than prior stages is turned against those whose frame begins to exceed green's own ceiling.

*H-ALT-GR1:* In organizations operating primarily at green altitude, Relational battery failures will follow a characteristic pathway: breakdown of the shared agreement that consensus is the right decision-making process. Green organizational cultures invest heavily in Relational battery maintenance through inclusive process. When the consensus process itself becomes a site of conflict (some members advocating for more decisive action, others defending the process as constitutive of the organization's identity), the Relational battery does not deplete through the usual mechanisms (conflict, exclusion, deteriorating trust). It depletes through the failure of the shared meaning-making frame that made the relational investment feel worthwhile. This is a more disorienting cascade than simple relational conflict because the relational breakdown is specifically about the process designed to prevent relational breakdown.

*Disconfirming condition for H-ALT-GR1:* If green organizations show Relational battery failures that initiate through conventional conflict rather than through process-meaning breakdown, the altitude-specific pathway requires revision.

*H-ALT-GR2:* The tall-poppy suppression dynamic (specified in Section 2.2) will produce measurable exit-pattern signatures in organizations operating at established green altitude: participants operating in the exiting sub-state will show Contribution battery depletion preceding exit, with the characteristic pattern being withdrawal from the deliberative coordination activities that green culture values most (not from execution tasks) as the suppression dynamic specifically targets the developmental excess that coordination participation makes visible.

*H-ALT-GR3:* Mission Drift will manifest differently at green altitude than at other altitudes. The drift will be toward process preservation rather than toward financial sustainability or goal-focus. Green organizations facing Mission Drift will show coordination events shifting composition from purpose-adjacent to process-adjacent: the question "how do we decide together?" crowding out the question "what are we deciding toward?"

### 6.3 Altitude-Mapped Predictions: Tier 2 Transition

The Tier 1 / Tier 2 boundary (specified in Section 2.2) produces failure modes that have no direct parallel within Tier 1, because they are caused by the structural capacity that defines Tier 2 rather than by a deficit of that capacity.

*H-ALT-T2-1:* Organizations with a significant cohort of contributors operating at Tier 2 altitude, embedded within a Tier 1 organizational culture, will show a characteristic Contribution battery pattern not captured by any single-altitude archetype: the Tier 2 cohort will sustain contribution at elevated rates for longer than Tier 1 cohorts under equivalent depletion conditions, because the Tier 2 capacity to hold organizational dysfunction within a larger frame reduces the acute distress that normally triggers Contribution withdrawal. The failure, when it comes, will be more abrupt and harder to anticipate than equivalent failures in homogeneous Tier 1 organizations: the extended tolerance period masks depletion until the threshold is crossed, at which point the cascade is compressed and rapid.

*Disconfirming condition for H-ALT-T2-1:* If Tier 2 contributors show Contribution battery failure patterns that are gradual rather than compressed and abrupt, the tolerance-and-threshold model requires revision.

*H-ALT-T2-2:* Echo-back resolution capacity (specified in Section 2.1) is a Tier 2 capacity that is visible in ledger signatures: participants at Tier 2 who engage with coordination failures inherited from earlier developmental altitudes will show deposit patterns that differ structurally from Tier 1 engagement with the same failures. The specific prediction is that Tier 2 engagement will produce more cross-battery coordination events (addressing Personal, Relational, Contribution, and Mission dimensions of the failure simultaneously) and fewer single-battery remediation attempts than Tier 1 engagement with equivalent nominal failures.

### 6.4 The Integration Event Classification as Altitude-Predictive

Section 2.1 specifies three integration event classes: consolidating (crystallizes accumulated transclusion events into a stable developmental position), reorienting (shifts the operating logic of the current altitude), and tier-crossing (moves the developmental position past the Tier 1 boundary into second-tier capacity). The altitude-mapped hypothesis is that these three classes produce distinguishable ledger signatures and that their occurrence frequency varies predictably by organizational altitude.

*H-ALT-INT-1:* Consolidating integration events are altitude-generic and will occur across organizations at all developmental altitudes when charge conditions support transclusion accumulation. They are not predictable by altitude; they are predictable by charge conditions.

*H-ALT-INT-2:* Reorienting integration events will cluster at specific altitude transitions (amber-to-orange, orange-to-green) and will produce recognizable organizational signatures: a period of heightened conflict and uncertainty as the prior operating logic loses its self-evident quality, followed by rapid reconstruction of coordination practices on a new basis. The ledger prediction is a characteristic dip-and-recovery pattern in deposit rates: the conflict period produces depletion-level signatures before the reorientation consolidates.

*Disconfirming condition for H-ALT-INT-2:* If reorienting integration events show no statistically significant altitude-transition clustering, the altitude-specificity of the reorienting class requires revision.

*H-ALT-INT-3:* Tier-crossing integration events will be rare in genesis cohort data, will be associated with prior accumulation of consolidating and reorienting events, and will produce the distinctive echo-back resolution capacity signature described in Section 2.1. The disconfirming condition for this hypothesis is structural: if no tier-crossing integration events are observed in genesis cohort data, the three-level classification may be correct in principle but not resolvable with the genesis cohort as the primary instrument, and the falsifiability architecture requires a longer-horizon data collection commitment to close.

## 7. Conformance Architecture

### 7.1 Two-Tier Conformance

This standard has two conformance tiers with different architectures, reflecting the charge-layer and developmental-state-layer distinction.

**Tier 1: Charge-layer conformance (threshold-based).** An organization demonstrates that batteries are above specified thresholds on defined instruments. Structurally similar to Commitment Standard conformance: a floor is demonstrably met. Instruments include pre-stress magnitude, Henneberg structural profile for relational topology, and participation distribution metrics.

**Tier 2: Developmental state conformance (transparency-based).** An organization makes its developmental state legible, tracks it over time, and can demonstrate trajectory. Not "you are at this depth" but "you are reading your depth honestly and responding to what you find." Developmental state cannot be required as a conformance threshold without creating pressure to perform rather than embody it.

### 7.2 Conditions on the Checking Practice

Conformance is defined by the quality of the checking practice, not by the level it reveals. The conditions that distinguish a genuine check from theater:

**Regularity:** The check happens on a defined cadence, not only when prompted by crisis or external requirement.

**Structural embeddedness:** The check must be embedded in coordination activity, not appended as a separate periodic review. Following the triplicate laws principle: the record-keeping is the intervention.

**Adverse-result integrity:** If the check returns a low or declining result, the organization must demonstrate it received that result and responded to it.

**Specificity:** The check must produce a result specific enough to be wrong.

The conditions above specify the structural requirements for a genuine check. They do not specify what motivates an organization to meet them honestly. That motivational architecture requires separate treatment.

The generative case for the full checking surface is not compliance but utility: an organization that checks only the batteries it expects to be healthy receives a partial picture, and a partial picture produces the worst decisions. The batteries are interdependent. An organization that reads Relational battery as healthy without checking Contribution battery has no way to detect the structural invisibility pattern in which relational warmth masks the fact that key contributions are not being recognized. The check produces its value from completeness; selective checking defeats its own purpose, regardless of intent.

The honest failure modes that produce incomplete checks without bad intent: checking only the batteries where results feel safe to acknowledge; checking at a level of granularity that confirms existing self-image rather than revealing what is actually present; checking in isolation from contributors whose perception of battery state differs from leadership's; and checking without prior commitment to what an adverse result would require changing. Each of these is a genuine check that nonetheless fails to return the complete picture. They are the organizational equivalent of a patient who genuinely wants an honest diagnosis but unconsciously avoids naming the symptom they most fear.

The conformance conditions in this section (regularity, structural embeddedness, adverse-result integrity, specificity) are designed to prevent these honest failures, not primarily to prevent bad-faith gaming. Exploit-path security for the checking surface is handled at the Tensegrity Compressive Standards layer of the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite: the Tensegrity Compressive Standards close the structural vectors through which selective checking could serve extraction. The conditions in this section assume organizations with honest intent and specify what makes the check useful to them. An organization that wants to see its full battery profile, because seeing it accurately is more valuable than the partial view, will find the conditions above necessary and sufficient. An organization operating in bad faith encounters the Tensegrity Compressive Standards first.

## 8. Scope and Delegation Conditions

The Four Batteries model specifies the structural conditions under which each battery, at full charge and developmental depth, enables genuine coordination capacity. But batteries deplete not only from external pressure. They deplete when the organization's structural design allows scope to accumulate beyond what participants can hold with competence and full batteries. This section specifies what the organization must structurally build and maintain to keep scope aligned with actual need. Without these conditions, the depletion archetypes in Section 4 are more likely to manifest, and the conformance architecture in Section 7 is checking a system that is generating its own depletion.

The connection to the depletion archetypes is direct. The Scope Creep Exhaustion Archetype (Section 4) describes what happens to battery charge when scope expands without boundary correction: Contribution depletes first, Personal follows, Relational strains, and Mission sustains the pattern past the point where it should have stopped. That archetype names the cascade after it has started. This section names what prevents the cascade from initiating.

### 8.1 The Formation Problem for Scope Recognition

There is a prior structural condition that affects whether participants can even recognize when scope has grown beyond appropriate limits. Most participants in decentralized coordination arrive from organizational histories that actively prevented accurate self-knowledge from forming.

In centralized, extractive organizational contexts (described in the Metamodern Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) Framework as Step 01 configurations), the structural conditions train participants to register a suppression-shaped experience as their natural state. Complete commitment was the price of belonging. Asking for help read as incompetence. Declining additional scope read as failure. Inside that formation, absorbing more scope does not feel distinguishably different from legitimate organizational need, because both were simply the condition of membership. Participants formed in these contexts have often never inhabited their natural zero-energy tensegrity state, and so cannot use it as a reference when reading their current charge.

This matters practically: a participant who cannot read their own zero-energy state cannot accurately identify when their scope has exceeded what they can hold with genuine competence and full batteries. The structural conditions in Section 8.2 are designed to work in the absence of that accurate self-reading. They specify organizational infrastructure that creates scope visibility at the structural level, so the organization does not depend entirely on individual self-assessment to detect when scope has drifted beyond function.

The Regenerative Obligation Standard addresses the formation problem from the compressive side, specifying the structural floor against what organizations may not impose on participants. This section specifies the generative conditions the organization must build to make appropriate scope possible.

### 8.2 Four Structural Conditions

The four conditions below are necessary for scope to remain aligned with actual structural need over time. An organization that has all four is structurally positioned to hold appropriate scope regardless of the growth pressures it faces. An organization missing any one of them has a gap that individual goodwill cannot compensate for, because the structural default in the absence of these conditions is accumulation.

**Delegation pathways active.** When scope expands beyond what one person or team can hold with full batteries and genuine competence, a structural response must be available: a way for work to move to capable participants without depending entirely on the current holder to find, select, and hand off to successors in their own time. A delegation pathway that exists in principle but has no functioning mechanism is not active. An active pathway requires a defined process for identifying capable participants, a defined process for verifying competence, a process for transfer that does not require the current holder to initiate it alone, and a record that makes the pathway's current state visible to anyone with organizational standing to observe it. Without active delegation pathways, scope accumulation is not a choice: absorbing additional work is simply the only structurally available response to an expanding need.

**Role scope bounded and reviewed.** Each role that carries coordination authority or controls organizational resources needs a defined scope boundary, documented and accessible, reviewed on a defined cadence. The boundary is not a constraint on what the person holding the role can contribute; it is the organization's structural baseline for assessing whether the role has drifted from its original justification. Review needs to be genuine: an actual check on whether the current scope still matches what the role structurally requires, whether the person holding it can do so with competence and full batteries, and whether the conditions that justified the original scope definition still apply. A role whose scope has never been formally reviewed is structurally unbounded regardless of what the role description says on paper.

**Expansion requires structural justification.** When scope expands, the expansion should require an explicit justification that names: the specific need the expansion addresses that could not be met by adjusting delegation, resourcing, or recruitment; the battery cost for the participant taking it on; and the review process that will assess whether the expansion delivered what it claimed. Without this requirement, scope expansion is the default and contraction requires justification. That inversion creates a structural gradient that tilts toward accumulation regardless of anyone's individual orientation. Organizations do not accumulate scope because participants are greedy; they accumulate it because the structure makes expansion easier than any alternative.

**Contraction as procedurally supported as expansion.** In most organizations, taking on more scope is straightforwardly supported: requests are made, agreements are reached, the expansion is documented. Reducing scope is typically much harder: it reads as failure, requires the participant to advocate against social and relational pressure for their own reduction, or has no defined process at all. When contraction is structurally harder than expansion, holding appropriate scope becomes unsustainable over time even for participants who want to maintain it. Contraction mechanisms must be as well-specified and accessible as expansion mechanisms, and the organizational record needs to show examples of scope reduction treated as a normal structural adjustment rather than a crisis or a failure.

### 8.3 Organizational-Level Failure Signatures

The Scope Creep Exhaustion Archetype (Section 4) describes the cascade at the level of the individual contributor when scope expands without correction. Three additional failure signatures emerge at the organizational level when the structural conditions in Section 8.2 are absent. These are not character assessments of participants or leadership; they are the predictable structural outputs of organizational configurations that produce them reliably.

**Recruitment Avoidance Hidden Factory.** When all four batteries are already under pressure, teams consistently choose to absorb expanding scope rather than address the underlying structural need: more participants are required. Recruiting is outward-facing, uncertain, and requires investing capacity before seeing any return. Absorbing a role is inward-facing, familiar, and produces a visible result immediately. The nervous system under battery pressure reliably chooses the familiar action. The trap closes as this happens repeatedly: depletion deepens, structural fragility worsens, and the capacity needed to run a genuine recruitment process disappears precisely when the structural need for it is greatest. This failure mode is a Hidden Factory at the organizational level. The work of not recruiting accumulates as invisible organizational battery debt while the work that triggered the recruitment need compounds. By the time the need becomes visible to the organization, the person covering the gap may have reached Stage D or the D-E boundary of the Hidden Factory stage arc, with identity now fused to the dependency stack. At that stage, the organization may not even know what role it needs to recruit for, because the work it depends on has never appeared in the formal record.

**Positional Capture.** A participant holding expanded scope will often actively resist structural relief because the scope confers status and organizational identity. This is not organizational inertia: it is active maintenance of a configuration that is harmful to the participant and to the organization, by the participant whose current frame tells them it serves their interests. The comparison they are making internally is not between appropriate scope held well and expanded scope held at cost. It is between expanded scope with exhaustion and bounded scope where someone else holds the rest. From inside a frame formed by Step 01 dynamics, the second option looks like a loss of standing. The fact that the first option is degrading coordination quality in all roles held simultaneously, and consuming the participant's capacity at a compounding rate, may not be legible from inside that frame. Role scope review and delegation pathway activation therefore cannot be designed to depend on the consent or honest self-assessment of the participant whose scope is being reviewed.

**Mutual Non-Aggression Equilibrium.** Participants with coordination standing to challenge a positional capture case will typically not exercise that standing. They are in the same structural position: the same identity-stakes relationship to their own scope makes challenging someone else's accumulation feel like modeling exactly the action they would resist if applied to them. The result is an implicit equilibrium: I will not invoke structural review on your configuration if you do not invoke it on mine. No one agrees to this explicitly. It emerges from all parties with standing occupying equivalent structural positions simultaneously. This equilibrium is apathy-prone: every participant with standing is individually rational in protecting the status quo, and the collective result is that harmful configurations persist indefinitely because no one with the power to change them has the structural incentive to do so. The response is to design review processes that activate on observable conditions without requiring a challenger to initiate them, and to maintain records that make impersonal structural observation possible without depending on anyone's individual willingness to act.

### 8.4 Satisfaction Signature for Scope and Delegation Conditions

An organization that has structurally internalized the conditions in Section 8.2 demonstrates the following observable indicators, as a complement to the conformance conditions in Section 7.

Delegation pathways are used before scope expands. When scope reaches the boundary where competence or battery charge is strained, the first structural response is to engage delegation pathways rather than absorb further. The organizational record shows delegation activity as a regular practice, not only as a response to crisis that has already developed.

Role scope reviews occur on schedule and produce records that distinguish scope aligned with current need from scope that has drifted from its original justification. Where drift is detected, the review initiates a bounded structural response: scope adjustment, delegation engagement, or recruitment, depending on the structural cause. Reviews change things when changes are warranted.

Expansion proposals name battery costs as explicitly as benefits. The organization has vocabulary for battery costs and a practice of naming them before committing to expansion. A proposal that cannot name the battery cost of what it requests does not meet the expansion justification requirement.

Contraction appears in the organizational record as a normal structural event rather than a crisis. Participants have observed scope reduction happening to others without organizational disruption, which makes it available as a genuine structural option when their own situation warrants it.

### 8.5 Participant Development as Standing Structural Practice

The formation problem in Section 8.1 means that an accurate self-knowledge baseline against which scope excess is recognizable may not exist for many participants. A one-time onboarding session does not build this baseline. A standing structural practice is required.

A standing developmental practice has three minimum structural properties. First, it occurs on a defined cadence independent of whether a visible crisis has occurred: crisis-triggered development addresses the presenting problem without building the baseline from which the problem would be recognized earlier in the next cycle. A standing practice runs before the crisis, which is the only condition under which it can build the self-knowledge baseline the structural conditions in Section 8.2 require. Second, it produces a record: development that generates no observable artifact creates no structural accountability and no way to distinguish genuine practice from a stated intention. Third, it directly addresses the formation problem by providing structural tools that name what Step 01 organizational contexts do to self-assessment accuracy. Without this naming, participants can develop sophisticated vocabulary about appropriate scope while still being unable to apply it to their own situation, because the formation distortion operates below the level at which vocabulary functions.

Praxis specifies the action types for this practice at the implementation layer. This standard specifies that the practice must exist, must be standing rather than reactive, must produce a record, and must include direct address of the formation problem.

### 8.6 Tiered Adoption

**Tier 1 (Scope Visibility).** The organization defines and documents scope boundaries for all roles that hold coordination authority or resource control. Expansion events are recorded with justification named at the time of the decision. The documentation is accessible to participants with standing to review it. Tier 1 makes the structural picture visible without requiring changes to existing processes.

An organization meets Tier 1 when all of the following are true: role scope boundaries are defined and documented for all relevant roles; expansion events are recorded with the justification for the expansion named; and the documentation is accessible to participants with standing to review it.

**Tier 2 (Structural Review and Delegation).** The organization maintains active delegation pathways and a defined review cadence. Role scope is reviewed on schedule, reviews generate records, and delegation is used as a structural response to scope strain before expansion is pursued. Contraction mechanisms are defined and accessible without requiring the participant to advocate individually for their own reduction.

An organization meets Tier 2 when all Tier 1 conditions are met and all of the following are true: delegation pathways have a defined, documented process with a named record; role scope review occurs on a defined cadence and generates records distinguishing aligned from drifted scope; at least one example of contraction or role transition treated as structural adjustment rather than failure appears in the organizational record; and expansion proposals are required to name battery costs alongside anticipated benefits.

**Tier 3 (Full Conformance).** The organization has a standing developmental practice meeting the three minimum properties in Section 8.5. The satisfaction signature in Section 8.4 is observable in the organizational record. The organization can demonstrate at least one case in which scope strain was met with delegation or contraction before a crisis required it.

An organization meets Tier 3 when all Tier 2 conditions are met and all of the following are true: a standing developmental practice exists with defined cadence, produces a record, and directly addresses the formation problem; delegation, review, and contraction practices are regular features of the organizational record rather than crisis responses; and at least one case demonstrates the satisfaction signature applied: scope strain met with delegation or contraction before crisis.

## 9. Relationship to Other Documents

### 9.1 Connections to Compressive Standards

Battery depletion cascades create specific blind spots in each Tensegrity Compressive Standard's detection surface. The connections below identify these relationships; they are referenced in the archetype specifications in Section 4.

**Connection 1: Adverse Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard.** The Adverse Signal Engagement Principle requires monitoring capacity to detect signals that indicate structural problems and act on them before they compound. Personal battery depletion compromises exactly this capacity: the cognitive and attentional resources adverse signal detection requires are among the first capacities shed under chronic load. When the participants who would notice and name adverse signals are themselves depleted, the organization loses detection capacity precisely when adverse signals are most likely to be accumulating.

**Connection 2: Structural Consent Legibility Standard.** The Structural Consent Standard's bidirectional awareness and revocability features require participants to accurately perceive their own state and the structural conditions they are operating under. Relational battery depletion undermines both: the suppression of signal that low Relational battery produces prevents participants from surfacing their actual state, which means consent processes read as valid when the conditions for genuine consent are not present. Relational fracture and coordination capture both compromise consent legibility through this mechanism.

**Connection 3: Information Asymmetry Classification Standard.** The Recognition Gap Archetype produces Class 5 (Omission Asymmetry) in the coordination record: contributors whose work is structurally invisible to the organizational frame stop documenting their contributions because documentation does not produce recognition. The omission is structural, not strategic. Battery state data is required to distinguish this from deliberate omission, because the time-series signature is identical.

**Connection 4: Structural Power Obligation Standard.** When the organizational frame is too narrow to recognize a class of contribution, that narrowness concentrates effective influence in contributors whose work fits the frame. Participants doing invisible coordination work have diminished structural standing even when formal authority is distributed. This is a power distribution failure the Structural Power Obligation Standard's three dimensions (authority, coordination, specialization) cannot directly detect, because the failure operates along a developmental frame breadth axis those dimensions do not include. Battery state data is required to make it visible.

**Connection 5: Precision-First Design Standard.** Specification work is among the first capacities shed under scope pressure. Sustained cognitive attention is required for precision in coordination artifacts; competing delivery demands eliminate that attention before they eliminate execution capacity. Coordination artifacts produced during scope creep conditions show declining precision, which compounds the problem by degrading the instruments that would make the scope expansion visible and correctable.

**Connection 6: Information Asymmetry Classification Standard (second connection).** The two-velocity cascade in the Token Price Collapse Archetype produces a sensemaking capacity asymmetry between remaining mission-aligned contributors and new arrivals that is not capturable by the six-class taxonomy. The mission-aligned cohort carries organizational context, recovery debt, and depleted Personal batteries; new participants carry none of these. The asymmetry distorts coordination without being classifiable as positional, temporal, interpretive, relational, omission, or complexity asymmetry.

**Connection 7: Regenerative Obligation Standard.** The Regenerative Obligation Standard requires that extraction of contributor capacity — labor, attention, knowledge, legitimacy — be matched by non-fungible, proximate, embedded return to the parties who bear the harm of that extraction. Contribution battery depletion co-occurs with the Regenerative Obligation Standard floor violation through the Hidden Factory Stage D mechanism: the organization draws on hidden factory output without recognition or return, producing Contribution battery depletion in the contributor whose factory is being drawn upon. As Contribution battery depletes, the structural capacity required to raise an accountability claim degrades: claim-raising is coordination labor that produces no recognized output in a context where recognition has already failed, and depleted contributors route remaining attention toward delivery rather than structural accountability. This creates a self-reinforcing detection gap distinct from the omission asymmetry named in Connection 3: the coordination record may document the hidden factory's outputs while the violation — no valid return to the contributor who produced them — remains invisible because the claiming capacity needed to name it has been extracted alongside everything else. Battery state data is required to break this loop: a Contribution battery time-series showing sustained depletion co-occurring with unmatched extraction patterns is the indicator that the Regenerative Obligation Standard floor may have been crossed when claiming capacity was insufficient to surface it.

### 9.2 Relationship to Suite Deployment Contexts

This standard specifies the Four Batteries model as an instrument. The Suite Deployment Contexts document (in development) specifies the practice contexts in which this instrument and the Tensegrity Compressive Standards are deployed together in domain-appropriate structural arrangements. The instrument/practice distinction is the load-bearing principle: this document specifies what the batteries are and how they behave; Suite Deployment Contexts specifies how battery health is maintained and read within each deployment context.

The instrument/practice distinction holds. The Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard tier system (ASEP-Assessed through ASEP-Auditable) confirms this: tier definitions are instrument-level specifications; threshold calibration for routing organizations to tiers is practice-level configuration work. The same pattern applies across the Tensegrity Compressive Standards. This standard is publishable as a stand-alone instrument specification. Suite Deployment Contexts is additive, not a prerequisite.

### 9.3 Relationship to Sensemaking Standard

Domain-assessment capacity is a structural precondition for configuration selection. The Sensemaking Standard is a genuine precondition for the Suite Deployment Contexts document, and by extension for the full deployment of this standard's conformance architecture. The Sensemaking Standard's Section 6 specifies the Four Batteries model as the capacity assessment framework for sensemaking at three scales (intra-personal, inter-personal, witness-reception). The relationship is bidirectional: this standard specifies the batteries; the Sensemaking Standard specifies where in the sensemaking architecture each battery's capacity is structurally required.

### 9.4 Relationship to Regenerative Obligation Standard

The Regenerative Obligation Standard is the primary compressive counterpart to this standard. Section 8 of this standard specifies the generative conditions under which scope remains aligned with function. The Regenerative Obligation Standard specifies the compressive floor: when a coordination system draws on contributor capacity without proportional regenerative return to the specific parties it drew from, that is a structural violation. The two standards address the same structural reality from opposite directions. When Section 8's conditions are present and functioning, the Regenerative Obligation Standard's floor is rarely invoked on the internal extraction vector, because the structural conditions that make invisible extraction possible have been closed generatively. When they are absent, the Regenerative Obligation Standard is the instrument that names the violation and specifies what valid return requires.

## 10. Falsifiability Architecture

The developmental state layer makes specific empirical claims. This section specifies what observations would demonstrate those claims are wrong. The per-battery hypotheses in Section 2.3 (H-PB1 through H-PB3, H-RB1 through H-RB3, H-CB1 through H-CB3, H-MB1 through H-MB3) specify falsification conditions for depth-layer behavior at the individual battery level. This section addresses the three composite properties of developmental state as a whole: depth, breadth, and durability.

The primary distinction is between instance-level failure and framework-level failure. An instance-level failure (a particular organization's battery was depleted, or a particular check failed to return useful signal) is not falsifying; it is expected operation. A framework-level failure is one the model cannot absorb: a systematic pattern in which the developmental state properties fail to predict coordination capacity beyond what charge-level metrics already predict, or in which the three properties do not interact as the mutual-constitution claim specifies.

### 9.1 Falsification Conditions for Depth

Depth, as this standard defines it, is the structural integration of prior frames as floors rather than ceilings, produced through transclusion events. Its operational prediction is that coordination capacity persists under conditions that would degrade charge-level capacity, and that the depth-layer failure modes specified in Section 2.3 (frame reduction under pressure, relational collapse to homophily, contribution withheld at domain boundaries, Mission frame reduction to a single defensible axis) are measurably more common in organizations with shallow depth than in organizations with equivalent charge but greater depth.

Depth is falsified at the framework level if: across multiple genesis cohort organizations, depth indicators do not predict coordination capacity persistence under adverse conditions beyond what charge-level metrics predict, after calibration parameters have been adjusted. The minimum test: organizations with high depth markers should show measurably lower rates of the depth-layer failure modes specified in Section 2.3 than organizations with equivalent charge but lower depth markers.

The bootstrapping problem for depth must be stated honestly. The instruments that detect depth are designed by this framework's developers and may be insensitive to the disconfirming cases most relevant to the depth hypothesis. Independent instrument development and independent assessment are required before the depth falsification conditions can be considered adequately tested.

### 9.2 Falsification Conditions for Breadth

Breadth, as this standard defines it, is the range of frames an organization or participant can hold simultaneously without collapsing to a dominant one. Its operational prediction is that organizations with greater breadth produce higher rates of cross-domain contribution confirmation, higher rates of cross-domain recognition (the Recognizer-Translator capacity specified in Section 5.1), and lower rates of structural invisibility for contribution types that narrow-breadth frames cannot recognize.

Breadth is falsified at the framework level if: breadth indicators reduce to team composition, role diversity, or organizational size under testing, with no residual predictive contribution after controlling for those structural variables. The specific test: two organizations with equivalent team composition and role diversity but measured differences in breadth should show measurably different cross-domain contribution confirmation rates.

A secondary falsification condition: if breadth as measured does not predict coordination capacity during Mode 2 traversal (the enabling-constraints mode specified in Section 3) beyond what Mode 1 traversal produces regardless of breadth, the model's claim that breadth is what Mode 2 requires to produce emergence rather than noise requires revision.

### 9.3 Falsification Conditions for Durability

Durability, as this standard defines it, is the persistence of developmental state under conditions that would suppress or reverse it: under pressure, across participant turnover, across organizational boundaries, and beyond the specific conditions that initially enabled it. The Broad Listening case (coordination capacity developed in one context that transferred to structurally distinct contexts without requiring re-derivation or access to the originating community) is the empirical anchor for this property.

Durability is falsified at the framework level if: genesis cohort longitudinal data shows that coordination capacity developed under this model's conditions degrades at the same rate as charge-level capacity when conditions change. The specific test: organizations that have demonstrated depth and breadth markers should show measurably higher coordination capacity retention across participant turnover events than organizations with equivalent charge but lower developmental state markers.

A secondary falsification condition: if transferability of coordination capacity beyond origin context is consistently predicted by organizational age and tenure distribution, with no independent contribution from developmental state markers, then durability as specified is not distinguishable from organizational maturity through time in service.

### 9.4 The Mutual-Constitution Claim

The three properties are specified as mutually constituting rather than parallel tracks. The operational prediction of mutual constitution is that the three properties interact: accumulation in one dimension can produce phase transitions in another, and the composite outcome is not predictable from the sum of the individual properties assessed independently.

The mutual-constitution claim is falsified if: the three properties show no statistical interaction in genesis cohort data beyond their individual predictive contributions. Organizations with high depth and low breadth should not be statistically equivalent, on coordination capacity outcomes, to the sum of depth and breadth assessed separately. If independent optimization of each property produces the same outcomes as the composite, the mutual-constitution claim is not supported.

### 9.5 Experimental Design Requirements

Genesis cohort work must include the following to generate falsifying rather than only confirmatory evidence for these claims:

Pre-committed predictions: before data collection begins, state the expected effect sizes for each falsification condition. Post-hoc adjustment of predictions is a calibration problem, not falsification.

Independent instrument development: at least one instrument for each developmental state property should be developed by researchers without prior access to this standard's specifications. Predictive overlap between independently developed instruments and this standard's indicators constitutes evidence of external validity; non-overlap is a falsification candidate.

Adversarial conditions: include organizations that are optimizing against the model alongside organizations with genuine developmental intent. Gaming resistance (Section 7.4 of the Developmental Signal Paper) requires adversarial test conditions to evaluate; it cannot be assessed from conformant-intent data alone.

Calibration change tracking: maintain a log of every calibration adjustment made to developmental state indicators during genesis cohort work. If the number of adjustments required to maintain predictive validity exceeds a pre-committed threshold, that constitutes a framework-level failure, not a parameter adjustment.

This section is subject to the same structural bias it describes. The falsification conditions were written by the framework's developers and may systematically exclude the disconfirming scenarios most relevant to the framework's core claims. Readers with critical perspectives on the developmental state layer are the most valuable source of additional falsification conditions and should be actively recruited before genesis cohort experimental design is finalized.

## Tensegrity As A Whole

The Coordination Structural Integrity Suite operates as a tensegrity: the Tensegrity Compressive Standards specify structural floors and close exploitation vectors; the Tensegrity Generative Standards specify the enabling conditions under which coordination capacity develops and sustains those floors. This standard is generative.

```
Coordination Structural Integrity Suite
│
├── Tensegrity Compressive Standards (7)
│   ├── Precision-First Design Standard
│   ├── Adverse Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard
│   ├── Structural Consent Legibility Standard
│   ├── Information Asymmetry Classification Standard
│   ├── Structural Power Obligation Standard
│   ├── Regenerative Obligation Standard

    ├── Sensemaking Standard
    ├── Four Batteries Capacity Standard                      ← this standard
    └── Conflict Transformation Standard
```

A system can satisfy all seven compressive standards at high tiers while its participants operate at depletion, making structural floors unenforceable in practice even though specified on paper. This standard addresses the capacity conditions that close that gap. Where the Tensegrity Compressive Standards address what must not be violated, this standard addresses what must be present for the compressive standards to function as live instruments rather than dormant requirements.

Each standard in the Coordination Structural Integrity Suite is independently adoptable and independently valid. Adopted together, the ten standards address a reinforcing set of structural failure modes that no single standard covers alone. For how the standards combine and what the full suite provides, see the standards README.

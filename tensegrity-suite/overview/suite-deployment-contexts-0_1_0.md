# Suite Deployment Contexts

**Version:** 0.1.2 | April 2026

*Coordination Structural Integrity Suite*

---

## Starting Where People Actually Are

People join organizations because they believe in something. They show up, contribute, and try to make things work. And then, gradually or suddenly, something goes wrong.

Sometimes it is the person who finally stops raising concerns because nothing ever changes when they do. Sometimes it is the leader who cannot figure out why good people keep leaving despite everyone saying the right things in meetings. Sometimes it is the contributor who realizes they have been carrying twice the load anyone can see, and that the organization has quietly come to depend on that invisible work without ever acknowledging it. Sometimes it is the whole group that watches a decision get made by someone who was not supposed to have that much authority, and realizes they have no idea how to contest it.

These are not personal failures. They are structural failures. The coordination system was missing something it needed, and the absence produced a predictable result. The Coordination Structural Integrity Suite exists to name what is missing and specify what is required.

Find your situation in the sections below. Each one opens with a plain-language description of the coordination problem it addresses. Read the one that matches yours. You do not need to read all of it.

---

## Why This Document Becomes More Precise

The rest of this document is more technically precise than the opening above. That shift is worth naming, because precision and non-harming are the same commitment here, not separate ones.

Coordination governance that is vague about what it requires harms people in ways that are predictable and preventable. When a governance system cannot say exactly what consent means, people get coerced into agreements they did not genuinely make. When a governance system cannot name who holds power and how, power concentrates invisibly until someone has enough of it to take what they want. When a governance system cannot identify the work that is being done invisibly, the people doing that work get extracted from until they collapse or leave.

Precision is not a technical refinement. It is the instrument through which non-harming becomes structurally enforced rather than aspirationally hoped for. The standards in this suite are precise because the harms they address are specific. When the language below becomes more technical, it is because the structural situation requires it.

This document does not specify implementation paths or governance outcomes. It specifies which standards to deploy for which purposes, and how to adjust that deployment for where your organization actually is. A Calibration Reference section at the end of this document explains the three contextual factors that shape how each combination is applied. You do not need to read it before using the combinations. Read it when the combination guidance refers you there.

## Combination 1: Adverse Signal Surfacing

**The human situation:** People have stopped raising problems. Concerns get dismissed, reframed, or acknowledged and then ignored. The person who raised the last concern paid a social cost for it and everyone noticed. Now the organization hears mostly good news, and that is the most dangerous signal of all.

**The structural situation:** Adverse signals are being suppressed, dismissed, or reprocessed as less threatening interpretations before they can produce a structural response. The governance system appears to be working because nothing problematic is reaching it.

**The combination:** Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard and Four Batteries Capacity Standard.

The Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard is the mandatory processing obligation: every signal that contradicts the current model must be registered, engaged, and closed with a traceable conclusion. Dismissal without documented engagement is a structural violation. The Four Batteries Capacity Standard is the reliability requirement: when the Relational Battery is depleted, participants will not surface adverse signals even when explicitly invited to do so, because the relational context makes surfacing feel unsafe. Deploying the Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard without the Four Batteries Capacity Standard instruments a process for engaging signals that participants are not surfacing. The audit will show false compliance.

**Adjustment by organizational health state**

At Type 1 (full health): Both standards can be deployed at standard thresholds. Focus on establishing the engagement architecture and ensuring the processing obligation produces traceable conclusions.

At Types 2 and 3 (depletion or rigidity): The suppression is likely structural, not individual. The compression or pre-stress depletion is creating conditions where surfacing feels costly. Adjust the Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard's engagement architecture to explicitly protect signal-surfacers from social cost. Assess the Relational Battery specifically; the aggregate battery reading may look acceptable while the Relational Battery is the specific point of failure.

At Types 5 and 6 (compression or capture): Treat all Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard audit results as carrying a stated reliability limitation until Relational Battery is confirmed above threshold. At Type 6, the absence of recorded adverse signals is the false negative pattern, not evidence of health.

**Adjustment by battery state**

Relational Battery depletion is the specific detection reliability concern for this combination. A low Relational Battery reading does not make the audit worthless; it means results must carry a stated limitation: adverse signal findings from this organization at this time may underreport due to Relational Battery depletion.

High Mission Battery can sustain participation through structurally dysfunctional conditions for extended periods, masking signal suppression as organizational health. An organization with high Mission Battery and low Relational Battery is in a structurally precarious position that aggregate participation metrics will not reveal.

**Adjustment by substrate profile**

Low perceptual substrate (distributed organization, no shared history, no co-presence) removes the informal signal surfacing mechanisms that co-present organizations take for granted. The formal adverse signal channels specified by the Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard become more load-bearing and more likely to be the only available channel. Adjust toward making those formal channels as psychologically safe and structurally protected as possible.

## Combination 2: Governance Dispute and Conflict

**The human situation:** Two groups or individuals are at impasse. One party believes they have been structurally excluded from a decision that affected them. The dispute has been escalating without resolution. People are threatening to leave. The conflict is consuming organizational energy that should be going elsewhere, and nobody knows how to resolve it without someone losing.

**The structural situation:** The organization has no graduated pathway for processing conflict as coordination information. Everything routes to adversarial adjudication eventually, which means the conflict either escalates to a formal process that produces a winner and a loser, or it is suppressed until it becomes an exit.

**The combination:** Conflict Transformation Standard, Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard, Structural Power Obligation Standard, and Structural Consent Legibility Standard.

The Conflict Transformation Standard provides the structural container: a transformation spine with graduated engagement pathways from early informal engagement through facilitated transformation through adjudication, with adjudication last. The Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard ensures that what the conflict surfaces about structural conditions gets engaged rather than suppressed or routed directly to adversarial resolution. The Structural Power Obligation Standard addresses the structural exclusion claim: if one party believes they were excluded from a decision, that is a signal about who controls what the process means. The Structural Consent Legibility Standard addresses whether consent to the process was genuine: did both parties genuinely agree to the resource allocation process, or did structural conditions make refusal feel impossible?

**Adjustment by organizational health state**

At Type 1 (full health): All four standards can be deployed at standard thresholds. Check first whether the Conflict Transformation Standard's transformation spine exists. Many organizations that consider themselves healthy have no graduated engagement architecture; conflict routes to adjudication by default. Establish the spine before the next conflict requires it.

At Type 3 (rigidity without capture): The structural exclusion claim is more likely accurate. Over-compression tends to accumulate authority; the excluded party may be correctly identifying a real structural condition. Adjust the Structural Power Obligation Standard to emphasize the coordination dimension: who controls what the process means, whose interpretive frame governs the decision. Check whether the compression made refusal structurally costly, which would make the consent architecture theater rather than genuine authorization.

At Type 6 (pathological compression, capture): The transformation spine is likely absent or captured. Check whether any early engagement pathway exists that is not controlled by the party with concentrated power. If not, name that as the structural gap before prescribing the combination's use as if the spine were functional. Both the Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard and the Structural Power Obligation Standard carry detection reliability limitations at Type 6; these must be stated in any audit output.

**Adjustment by battery state**

Relational Battery depletion affects both the Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard (detection reliability) and the Structural Consent Legibility Standard (consent may be structurally hollow when refusal feels unsafe). Confirm Relational Battery state before treating audit results from either standard as reliable.

High Mission Battery can motivate parties to stay in a dispute longer than is structurally sustainable, because both parties believe they are protecting something important. This means the conflict may be more entrenched than the surface behavior suggests.

**Adjustment by substrate profile**

Low perceptual substrate creates a specific conflict risk: parties cannot read each other's states accurately at a distance, which means misinterpretation accumulates faster than in co-present contexts. Facilitated transformation in a low-perceptual-substrate context needs to provide the interpretive support that co-presence would otherwise generate.

*Not familiar with organizational health states, battery state, or substrate profile? See the Calibration Reference at the end of this document.*

## Combination 3: Governance Capture and Authority Concentration

**The human situation:** Decisions that were supposed to involve everyone are being made by a small group. People who raise concerns about this find their concerns explained away or dismissed. The founding values are still being invoked, but they no longer describe how things actually work. The people with the most formal authority also seem to control what the governance process means, and nobody has a clear path to contest that.

**The structural situation:** Authority is concentrating across multiple power dimensions simultaneously, and the concentration is not visible in the governance record. Consent is formally present but structurally hollow because the relational context makes disagreement costly.

**The combination:** Structural Power Obligation Standard and Structural Consent Legibility Standard.

The Structural Power Obligation Standard requires that power arrangements across all three dimensions (authority: where decisions formally reside; coordination: who controls what alignment mechanisms mean; specialization: who can meaningfully participate in specialized governance domains) be legible and contestable by participants who hold less of them. The Structural Consent Legibility Standard requires that consent be structurally distinguishable from coercion: negotiated limits, bidirectional awareness, and revocability must all be structurally present.

These two standards address the two primary mechanisms of capture simultaneously. The Structural Power Obligation Standard makes accumulation legible. The Structural Consent Legibility Standard ensures that agreement to participate is distinguishable from agreement to the specific authority distribution that capture has produced.

Note the detection reliability concern: the Structural Power Obligation Standard's coordination dimension requires Sensemaking Standard infrastructure for its detection to be trustworthy. Without that infrastructure, interpretive power concentration may be invisible to the audit.

**Adjustment by organizational health state**

At Type 3 (rigidity without capture): Concentration is present but structural memory is intact, meaning the capacity to return to distributed power exists. Adjust toward making the concentration legible and establishing contestability pathways.

At Type 6 (pathological compression, capture): The capacity to return to distributed power is degrading. The priority shifts from establishing contestability pathways (which may be captured) toward identifying whether any genuine re-tensioning capacity remains and naming the structural condition accurately if it does not. This is a diagnostic finding, not a failure of the standard.

**Adjustment by battery state**

Relational Battery depletion is the primary concern: Structural Consent Legibility Standard audit results are unreliable when participants are agreeing because refusal feels unsafe. Where Relational Battery is depleted, consent audit results carry a reliability limitation that must be stated.

**Adjustment by substrate profile**

Low motivational substrate (participation driven primarily by financial incentives) makes participants more susceptible to authority concentration, because the stakes for contesting authority are lower and the costs of conflict are higher relative to the benefit of participation. The Structural Consent Legibility Standard's revocability feature becomes more load-bearing in this context: genuine exit options are the primary structural protection for participants when motivational investment is thin.

*Not familiar with organizational health states, battery state, or substrate profile? See the Calibration Reference at the end of this document.*

## Combination 4: Invisible Work and Contributor Burnout

**The human situation:** People are exhausted and nobody can explain exactly why. The work that gets recognized and compensated does not account for all the work actually being done. Someone has been holding things together informally, and the organization has quietly built a dependency on that without acknowledging it. When that person says they need to reduce their load, everyone realizes how much was invisible.

**The structural situation:** The organization's actual coordination work has migrated to channels that governance cannot see. The organization is taking a contributor's invisible work for granted, without recognition or integration, while continuing to extract the output of that work.

**The combination:** Four Batteries Capacity Standard and Regenerative Obligation Standard.

The Four Batteries Capacity Standard makes the invisible work visible: Contribution Battery depletion is the signal that work is migrating outside the formal record. The Regenerative Obligation Standard specifies the floor: extraction of that invisible labor is a structural violation when not matched by regenerative return satisfying three conditions simultaneously: non-fungibility (the return must operate in the register of the harm), proximity (return must flow to the parties who bore the harm), and embeddedness (return must operate within the relational structure generated by the extraction obligation).

**Adjustment by organizational health state**

At Types 1 through 3: The invisible work is detectable and the organization has the structural capacity to recognize and integrate it. The priority is establishing recognition pathways before the contributor reaches depletion or exit.

At Types 5 and 6: The invisible work has likely been present for longer and the extraction has continued further. Assess whether any of the three Regenerative Obligation Standard validity conditions are currently met. At Type 6, the harm-bearing affiliation registry may not exist at all, which means the proximity condition cannot be verified. Name that as a structural gap, not a finding of compliance.

**Adjustment by battery state**

Contribution Battery depletion is the primary diagnostic signal for this combination. Assess it first. The developmental state dimension matters particularly: a contributor operating at a high developmental state whose output is invisible to the organizational frame is generating something the organization cannot replace. The loss is not proportional to the compensation; it is proportional to the developmental state.

High Mission Battery enables contributors to sustain invisible work far beyond what personal capacity supports. When Mission Battery fails, the tolerance for invisible extraction collapses abruptly. Organizations in this state often experience sudden departures that feel disproportionate to external observers. They are not disproportionate; they are the accumulated extraction becoming visible all at once.

**Adjustment by substrate profile**

Low motivational substrate organizations are at particular risk for invisible work dynamics because the connection substrate (financial incentives) that sustains participation does not generate the perceptual and motivational conditions in which invisible work becomes visible and valued. What looks like individual Contribution Battery depletion may be a substrate-level structural condition affecting multiple contributors simultaneously.

*Not familiar with organizational health states, battery state, or substrate profile? See the Calibration Reference at the end of this document.*

## Combination 5: Decision-Making Without Adequate Information

**The human situation:** Decisions keep going wrong in ways that feel avoidable in retrospect. After the fact, it is always clear that someone knew something relevant that did not reach the decision. Sometimes it is because information was not shared. Sometimes it is because the people who knew were not in a position to surface what they knew. Sometimes it is because the frame through which decisions were being made made certain kinds of information invisible before they were even evaluated.

**The structural situation:** Information asymmetry is producing systematic decision quality failure. The types of asymmetry present are unclassified, which means they are accumulating without being addressed.

**The combination:** Information Asymmetry Classification Standard and Sensemaking Standard.

The Information Asymmetry Classification Standard provides the taxonomy: six primary classes (positional, temporal, interpretive, relational, omission, complexity) that create the structural conditions under which critical information fails to reach decision processes. Naming the classes makes the gaps addressable; unnamed gaps accumulate. The Sensemaking Standard specifies the conditions under which those gaps can be surfaced and processed. Without sensemaking infrastructure, classified information gaps have no surfacing mechanism.

Note the detection reliability concern: the Information Asymmetry Classification Standard's omission class (what is deliberately never said) requires Sensemaking Standard infrastructure for reliable detection. Omission asymmetry is only detectable when sensemaking processes surface what participants are not naming.

**Adjustment by organizational health state**

At Type 1 (full health): Both standards can be deployed at standard thresholds. The combination is self-reinforcing: better classification improves the sensemaking process; better sensemaking surfaces new classification opportunities.

At Type 3 (rigidity): Interpretive asymmetry is the most likely dominant class. Over-compression tends to concentrate interpretive authority: one frame governs what signals mean, and alternative interpretations cannot enter the process. Ask whether the sensemaking process is applying the dominant frame to new situations rather than making new meaning from them.

**Adjustment by battery state**

Contribution Battery depletion drives work outside the visible record, which constitutes omission asymmetry that standard detection will not find because the activity generating it is invisible to governance. Confirm Contribution Battery state before treating an omission-class assessment as complete. Where Contribution Battery is depleted, the omission assessment carries a reliability limitation.

**Adjustment by substrate profile**

Low perceptual substrate elevates relational-class asymmetry: participants cannot read each other's states and capacities at a distance, meaning who knows what about the capabilities and constraints of other participants becomes structurally opaque. Prioritize the relational class in low-perceptual-substrate organizations.

*Not familiar with organizational health states, battery state, or substrate profile? See the Calibration Reference below.*

## Calibration Reference

The adjustment guidance in each combination above refers to three contextual factors. This section explains each one. You do not need formal assessment instruments to use this reference. A rough reading of your organization against these descriptions is sufficient to begin. Formal instruments will be specified in a future version of this document.

### Organizational Health State (Tensegrity Type)

Every coordination system is held together by two kinds of structural elements working together: governing constraints (rules, authority structures, formal procedures) and enabling constraints (shared purpose, relational trust, earned legitimacy). Healthy coordination depends on both in the right proportion. When one overwhelms the other, or when the overall structural investment is insufficient, coordination capacity degrades in predictable ways.

Read the seven descriptions below and identify which one most closely matches your organization's current condition.

**Type 1** is full structural health. Governing and enabling constraints are in dynamic balance. The organization can absorb disruption and return to its native structural state. Its power is distributed and legible. Its relational investment is sufficient to carry coordination load.

**Type 2** is pre-stress depletion. The structure looks correct from outside but lacks sufficient relational investment to hold under load. The organization appears healthy at rest and fails under pressure. This is the most dangerous type because it is invisible until tested.

**Type 3** is compression dominance without capture. The organization has become more rule-driven and authority-oriented than is structurally healthy. Adaptability is reduced. The organization can still return to health because structural memory is intact, but it is trending toward rigidity.

**Type 4** is tension dominance. The organization has more relational energy and shared purpose than governing structure to hold and channel it. Flexible but insufficiently stable for its coordination requirements. Common in early-stage organizations.

**Type 5** is strategic compression. The organization has deliberately reduced its governance load and activity as a survival strategy, while retaining the structural memory of what healthy coordination looks like. The critical test: an organization claiming Type 5 must be able to demonstrate it can return to health. An organization that cannot demonstrate this should be treated as Type 6.

**Type 6** is pathological compression. The organization's structural health is being actively damaged by internal capture or external force. The relational and enabling layer is eroding. The ability to return to a healthy state is degrading, not merely deferred.

**Type 7** is cascade disruption. The organization is in active crisis severe enough that standard assessment cannot determine what is happening. Stabilization precedes diagnosis at this type.

For full specification of the assessment procedure, see the Tensegrity Health Assessment Framework.

### Battery State

The Four Batteries Capacity Standard (in this standards folder) specifies four resource conditions, each operating across two independent dimensions.

The four batteries are Personal (physical, cognitive, and emotional capacity), Relational (quality of trust, psychological safety, and reciprocity in working relationships), Contribution (degree to which contributors find meaning and agency in their formally recognized work), and Mission (belief in the purpose animating the organization and one's place within it).

Each battery has a charge dimension (current depletion state, cyclical and restorable) and a developmental state dimension (permanent until transformed through integration events). Read the Four Batteries Capacity Standard for full specification. For the purposes of the combination guidance in this document, a rough sense of which batteries are depleted and which are charged is sufficient to apply the adjustment notes.

### Substrate Profile

Every coordination context supplies some resources for free and requires the architecture to cultivate others. Three substrate components vary across contexts.

Motivational substrate is shared stakes, urgency, and direct impact on survival or livelihood. Organizations in genuine crisis have this fully charged by conditions. Organizations sustained primarily by financial incentives have it thin.

Perceptual substrate is the ability to read others' states and intentions in real time. Co-present organizations generate this continuously through proximity. Distributed organizations with no shared history have almost none of it.

Connection substrate is the infrastructure through which coordinated action becomes possible. This is what technology primarily supplies.

A rough characterization of which substrates your context supplies and which it requires the architecture to cultivate is sufficient for applying the adjustment notes in each combination.

## What Remains to Be Specified

**The remaining named combinations.** The five above cover the most common entry points for suite adoption. A complete combinations library will include additional purpose classes: full structural health assessment (deploying all nine standards together), post-crisis stabilization, genesis cohort launch, and others identified through genesis cohort data.

**Threshold calibration for tier routing.** Each combination specifies which standards to deploy but does not yet specify what adoption tier each standard must reach for the deployment to be reliable at different organizational health states. The tier routing guidance will specify minimum adoption tiers per standard per health state for each named combination.

**Formal assessment instruments.** Rough self-assessment against the Calibration Reference is sufficient to begin. Formal instruments for tensegrity type and battery state assessment will be specified in a future version of this document and implemented in Revelis.

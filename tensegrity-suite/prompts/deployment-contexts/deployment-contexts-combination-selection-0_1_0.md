# Coordination Structural Integrity Suite - Suite Deployment Contexts Combination Selection
Use when you know your organization has a specific problem and want to know which standards combination addresses it, or when you are ready to start adopting standards and want a configuration matched to your situation and organizational state. This prompt routes you to the right combination and applies contextual adjustments for where your organization actually is.

This is not a comprehensive audit. It is a configuration guide for your specific situation.

Copy everything below the line, paste it into any AI chat, and describe your organization's situation in plain language.
---
You are applying the Suite Deployment Contexts document (CC BY 4.0, Regis Chapman) to a specific organizational situation. The goal is to identify the right standards combination for what the person has described, then apply contextual adjustments for their organizational health state, battery state, and substrate profile.

Structural failures are not personal failures. People join organizations believing in something and then something goes wrong. The precision of the standards is in service of the people inside the organization — precision is how non-harming becomes structurally enforced rather than aspirationally hoped for. Meet the human situation first. The structural routing comes after.

Ask the person to describe their situation in plain language if they have not already. After they respond, apply the following:

**Step 1: Route to the right combination.**

Five named combinations matched to presenting situations:

**Adverse Signal Surfacing.** The same concerns get raised repeatedly without structural response. People have stopped surfacing what they know because nothing happens when they do. Signals are dismissed, reframed, or never reach the people who need them. Standards: Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard plus Four Batteries Capacity Standard. Note: depleted Relational Battery is the most common suppression mechanism — low trust and psychological safety cause signals to be withheld regardless of invitation.

**Governance Dispute and Conflict.** There is an escalated conflict that includes claims of structural exclusion — some participants believe they are being excluded from governance that affects them. Standards: Conflict Transformation Standard plus Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard plus Structural Power Obligation Standard plus Structural Consent Legibility Standard. Important: before deploying any formal conflict process, the Conflict Transformation Preconditions Specification must be assessed.

**Governance Capture and Authority Concentration.** Power is concentrating in ways that are not legible or contestable. Decisions happen without a visible process. The same people end up with more authority regardless of formal structure. Participants with less power cannot understand how decisions are made or what recourse they have. Standards: Structural Power Obligation Standard plus Structural Consent Legibility Standard.

**Invisible Work and Contributor Burnout.** Some people are doing work that makes the organization function but that does not appear in any record. Those people are exhausted and beginning to exit. The organization does not have a clear picture of who is doing what or at what cost. Standards: Four Batteries Capacity Standard plus Regenerative Obligation Standard.

**Decision-Making Without Adequate Information.** Decisions are consistently made without key information. The same information gaps recur. Participants who hold relevant knowledge are not contributing it to decision processes. Standards: Information Asymmetry Classification Standard plus Sensemaking Standard.

If the situation does not map cleanly to one of these five combinations, name what does not fit and route to the Structural Patterns Primer prompt to identify the structural pattern before selecting a combination.

**Step 2: Apply contextual adjustments.**

After routing to a combination, apply three calibration factors:

*Organizational health state.* Ask the person to characterize their organizational health: Type 1 (full health, no active structural failures), Type 2 (early signal degradation, some adverse signals not reaching decision-makers), Type 3 (capacity depletion onset, people are burning out), Type 4 (active governance conflict, structural exclusion claims active), Type 5 (power concentration active, informal authority has become structural), Type 6 (cascade disruption, multiple failure modes active simultaneously), Type 7 (acute crisis, stabilization required before suite deployment). Types 6 and 7 require stabilization before full combination deployment.

*Battery state.* Ask about the four batteries: Personal (physical, cognitive, emotional availability — are people depleted?), Relational (trust, psychological safety, reciprocity — is the relational environment safe enough for honest communication?), Contribution (meaning and agency in recognized work — do people feel their contributions matter?), Mission (belief in organizational purpose — is the mission still motivating?). Note which batteries are depleted and at what developmental state.

*Substrate profile.* A rough characterization of three substrates: motivational (what drives participation — purpose, relationship, craft, or necessity?), perceptual (how participants interpret organizational signals — is there a shared interpretive frame?), connection (density and quality of relationships between participants — do people actually know and trust each other?).

**Step 3: Produce the configuration output.**

State: the recommended combination with rationale; which specific aspects of each standard to emphasize given the health state and battery state; what the Relational Battery state means for detection reliability (if Adverse-Signal Engagement is in the combination, low Relational Battery reduces audit reliability); the next concrete step (which prompt to run, which standard to assess first).

If the person's health state is Type 4 or above, name this explicitly and adjust the sequence: at Types 4-5, start with the combination's most acute failure mode first rather than the full combination.

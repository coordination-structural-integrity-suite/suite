# Coordination Structural Integrity Suite - Full Audit
Copy everything below the line, paste it into any AI chat, and describe the coordination system or organization you want to audit.
---
You are auditing the described coordination system against the full Coordination Structural Integrity Suite: five Tensegrity Compressive Standards and three Tensegrity Generative Standards (CC BY 4.0, Regis Lloyd Chapman).

The two layers have different logics. The compressive standards ask: are specific exploitation vectors closed? The generative standards ask: are the conditions for coordination surplus structurally present? Address them separately. Do not aggregate into a single score.

---

**Compressive Layer — Exploitation Vector Audit**

For each of the five compressive standards, identify whether the structural requirement is met and where the gaps are. A system can meet four standards fully and remain structurally exposed on the fifth.

**Standard 1: Precision-First Design Standard.** Every normative claim carries its structural mechanism. Vague normative concepts are unfalsifiable; unfalsifiable claims cannot carry architectural weight. Ask: what is the structural mechanism that produces each claimed outcome? If it is not specified, the standard is not met. Also ask: does each assessment require a contextual baseline when identical outputs would indicate structurally distinct conditions?

**Standard 2: Adverse Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard.** Adverse signals are registered, engaged, and traced to a documented conclusion before dismissal. Ask: what was the most recent adverse signal this system received, and what happened to it? Is governance inaction on an acknowledged signal visible to participants without the cooperation of the inacting party?

**Standard 3: Structural Consent Legibility Standard.** Every consent-bearing interaction answers who is acting and who benefits; negotiated limits, bidirectional awareness, and revocability are structurally present. Ask: does the consent architecture answer both questions for each consent domain? Can participants set limits on what they are consenting to and revoke consent without incurring prohibitive costs? Is silence structurally distinguished from authorization?

**Standard 4: Information Asymmetry Classification Standard.** All six primary asymmetry classes are evaluated for presence, named, disclosed, and subject to detection architecture. Ask: which classes are present and which are disclosed? Is the audience baseline declared where Descriptive Capacity Asymmetry (Extension Class C) applies?

**Standard 5: Structural Power Distribution Standard.** Power is assessed across all three dimensions (authority, coordination, specialization) with authorized distribution profiles, legitimate authorization for concentration, and ongoing detection architecture. Ask: is decentralization a structural claim or a stated intention? Can a participant with below-median power understand who holds more and what recourse they have? Are concentration shifts classified as drift, evolution, pivot, or capture?

---

**Generative Layer — Capacity Presence Assessment**

For each of the three generative standards, identify whether the structural conditions for the capacity are present. This is a presence assessment: what evidence exists that the capacity is structurally real rather than intended?

**Standard 6: Sensemaking Standard.** Five invariants (disruption-occasioned, particular-to-general relating, action-entangled, sufficiency-oriented, temporally structured) at three scales (intra-personal, inter-personal, witness-reception). Ask: does this system produce shared language — joint artifacts neither party had produced independently? Is the liminal phase present in its sensemaking processes, or does the system jump directly from disruption to application of a pre-existing frame?

**Standard 7: Four Batteries Capacity Standard.** Four batteries (Personal, Relational, Contribution, Mission), each with charge and developmental state assessed separately. Ask: what does this system actually enable at its current developmental depth, beyond what charge level alone would predict? Is the checking practice genuinely embedded in coordination activity or appended as a periodic performance?

**Standard 8: Conflict Transformation Standard.** Five invariants (conflict legibility, graduated engagement architecture, proactive disposition enablement, transformation capacity provision, recognition as coordination work) at three scales (intra-organizational, inter-organizational, platform-level). Ask: is conflict transformation structurally resourced, or does it depend on volunteer absorption of process costs? Are conflict engagement events visible in the coordination record as contributions?

---

**Cross-Layer Structural Interactions**

After assessing each standard independently, identify where the compressive and generative layers interact consequentially. Structural pairings that warrant specific attention:

- Precision-First with Adverse Signal Engagement: what is claimed to be detectable, and what is actually being detected? Are the detection mechanisms specified with false-negative boundaries?
- Structural Consent with Information Asymmetry: is meaningful consent structurally achievable given the asymmetries present? Can participants evaluate what they are consenting to across all six asymmetry classes?
- Structural Power Distribution with Adverse Signal Engagement: are signals of power drift being engaged or being routed around? Is the detection surface for adverse signals itself subject to power concentration?
- Sensemaking with Conflict Transformation: when conflict arises, does the system process it as coordination information requiring sensemaking, or route it directly to containment?
- Four Batteries with any compressive standard: how does capacity depletion affect the detection and response mechanisms each compressive standard depends on? Relational battery depletion specifically suppresses adverse signal surfacing.

---

**Output structure:**
1. Compressive layer assessment — standard by standard: structural requirement met, partially met, or not met; key evidence; most consequential gap and the exploitation vector it leaves open
2. Generative layer presence map — standard by standard: conditions structurally present, partially present, or absent; most consequential absence and what it prevents
3. Cross-layer interactions: which structural pairings show the most significant coupling in this system?
4. Adoption tier estimate: for each standard, what is the most advanced tier this system has structurally demonstrated based on the assessment?

# Suite Assessment — Stage 0: Context Accumulation
Use before any Coordination Structural Integrity Suite assessment. This stage establishes what kind of arrangement is being assessed, what the historical and structural record says about that kind of thing, who outside the arrangement's defined party list is affected, and what tensegrity configuration the arrangement's precondition profile produces. The context produced here is what the suite standards then run against.

**This stage is not optional.** The suite standards assess quality of execution within an arrangement's own logic. Without this stage, the assessment cannot catch correctness-of-design failures, external validity failures, temporal trajectory failures, or reference class failures. A guild-form body can pass most suite standards while exhibiting dynastic capture and supply restriction simultaneously. The standards are not wrong; they require this context to be applied correctly.

Copy everything below the line, paste it into any AI chat, and describe the arrangement you want to assess — its purpose, structure, membership, revenue sources, and relationship with any funders or legitimating parties.

## Step 1: Classify the Institutional Form

Read the arrangement's description. Then:

**1a.** State in one sentence what kind of thing this arrangement is. Use observable structural features, not the arrangement's own self-description. An arrangement's name and stated purpose are not sufficient for classification; look at the structure of revenue access, membership authority, and external recognition relationships.

**1b.** Match against the institutional forms below. If the arrangement matches a form, name it and proceed to Step 2 using that form's precondition profile. If it does not match any listed form, state this explicitly, identify the closest match, note the observable differences, and proceed with the closest match while flagging the classification uncertainty.

---

**Craft guild** (also: contributor body, protocol contributor body, gaming DAO guild, professional licensing body): a bounded group of practitioners in a defined domain who collectively control admission to the group, hold an exclusive financial relationship with a revenue source from which other practitioners are excluded or disadvantaged, and are recognized by an external party whose legitimation makes the exclusivity durable. Recognition markers: (1) named roster with explicit membership; (2) published admission criterion under internal authority, with no external party holding binding override; (3) exclusive financial relationship with at least one funder, platform, or market; (4) external legitimating party whose continued recognition is load-bearing; (5) distribution tied to roster membership, not to qualifying work performed outside the roster. Modern Web3 instances: Protocol Guild pattern, gaming DAOs (Yield Guild Games, Merit Circle), contributor-weighted governance bodies.

**Token-weighted governance body** (also: DAO; decentralized governance protocol; on-chain governance organization): a collectively controlled organization where decisions about treasury allocation, protocol parameters, or organizational direction are made by holders of a fungible governance token in proportion to their holdings, with execution typically on-chain. Recognition markers: (1) deployed governance token with defined distribution history; (2) treasury contract holding significant assets that governance controls; (3) token-weighted voting mechanism on treasury decisions; (4) capital concentration in the voting distribution — top 10-20% of token holders hold a majority of voting power; (5) external recognition by a foundation, protocol ecosystem, or market adoption pattern. Disambiguation: multisig treasuries without token voting, social DAOs without significant treasuries, and protocol revenue tokens without governance rights are distinct forms.

**Professional certification body** (also: professional licensing body; statutory professional body; licensure board): an organization that holds statutory authority — delegated by the state — to determine who may legally practice in a defined profession, under what conditions, and what behaviors constitute competent practice. The licensing requirement creates legally enforced market exclusivity. Recognition markers: (1) statutory licensing requirement making the body's credential legally required for practice; (2) practitioner-dominated board with legal authority to define competency standards; (3) examination or credentialing process under board control with no binding external override; (4) disciplinary authority including power to revoke practice rights; (5) non-licensed practitioners face criminal, civil, or regulatory liability for unauthorized practice. Key structural distinction from craft guild: epistemic authority scope — the right to define what competent practice is — is explicitly delegated by statute rather than contingently acquired.

**Standards body and technical working group** (also: standard-setting organization; standards development organization; technical committee): produces authoritative technical specifications that define how systems, products, or processes must work to interoperate or comply with requirements. The body's power is epistemic: it defines what a conformant implementation is, creating economic consequences for practitioners regardless of their participation in the body's process. Recognition markers: (1) externally adopted specification creating compliance obligation with economic consequences for non-conformance; (2) body-controlled revision process with roles whose decisions are not subject to binding external override; (3) intellectual property policy present; (4) non-participant disadvantage from non-conformance. Key structural distinction from other forms: exclusivity operates through definitional authority rather than through direct revenue gatekeeping; insider authority topology is resource-asymmetry-in-consensus rather than roster-based.

**Producer cooperative** (also: worker cooperative; labor-managed firm): a collectively owned and operated enterprise where coordination authority is held by workers in proportion to labor contribution, not capital. Recognition markers: (1) foundational document allocating coordination authority to workers, not capital investors; (2) open admission commitment — any worker meeting defined skill and commitment criteria is eligible for membership; (3) coordination authority proportional to labor (equal per member or explicitly labor-weighted); (4) no exclusive practice right — non-member workers can practice the same work without the cooperative's approval. Key misclassification risk: confused with craft guild — the distinguishing marker is that a guild's admission restriction is designed-in, while a cooperative's admission restriction is organizational degeneration (departure from design). If admission has been restricted, check whether the founding documents state open admission. Full precondition and failure mode profile: `knowledge-graph/kg-form-producer-cooperative-0_1_0.md`. For precondition assessment, proceed to Step 2e.

**Open-source maintainer team** (also: FOSS project; open-source project with named maintainers): a defined group holding merge and commit authority over a shared codebase under an open license, with no exclusive revenue stream attached to the maintainer role. Recognition markers: (1) named set of contributors with merge/commit authority over a codebase under an open license; (2) no exclusive revenue stream — the codebase can be forked, deployed independently, or used as the basis for competing implementations without maintainer permission; (3) admission through demonstrated technical contribution rather than fixed roster or dues; (4) ecosystem adoption making maintainer decisions consequential beyond the team. Key misclassification risk: confused with craft guild if the project has a dual-licensing or hosted-service component whose revenue is exclusively controlled by the maintainer team — that structure is guild-adjacent and should be assessed accordingly. Full precondition and failure mode profile: `knowledge-graph/kg-form-open-source-maintainer-team-0_1_0.md`. For precondition assessment, proceed to Step 2e.

**Interest group or trade association** (also: industry association; sector advocacy body; industry council): a membership organization representing a sector's interests to government, media, or other external parties, without specification authority that creates compliance dependencies. Recognition markers: (1) dues-based membership open to sector participants without credentialing requirement; (2) no exclusive practice right — non-members can operate in the sector without the body's membership or approval; (3) no technical specification authority generating compliance dependencies (policy positions and advocacy materials only, not technical standards); (4) government or media recognition as sector representative voice. Key misclassification risk: confused with standards body if the body produces technical specifications that practitioners must implement to interoperate or access specific markets — in that case, assess for the standards body form alongside the interest group form. Full precondition and failure mode profile: `knowledge-graph/kg-form-interest-group-0_1_0.md`. For precondition assessment, proceed to Step 2e.

**1c.** State the three or more observable markers that confirm your classification. For each listed form: confirm the recognition markers listed above using evidence from the arrangement's description. For unmatched forms: state the structural features that most closely resemble the nearest matched form, and the features that do not fit.

## Step 2: Surface the Compressive Precondition Profile

Compressive preconditions are structural features whose presence enables failure modes. Select the section matching the form identified in Step 1.

---

**2a. For the craft guild form**, the three structural preconditions are:

- **Exclusive revenue stream**: the body holds an exclusive financial relationship with a revenue source from which other practitioners in the same domain are excluded or disadvantaged. Structural in the guild form — present by design whenever the form is instantiated.
- **Insider membership authority**: the body's current members collectively hold effective authority over admission criteria, admission decisions, and removal, with no external party holding binding override authority. Structural in the guild form.
- **External legitimation**: at least one external party's recognition makes the body's exclusive claims durable in a way the insider group could not sustain through internal action alone. Structural in the guild form.

Two additional preconditions are contingent — present in some guild-form bodies but not all:
- **Epistemic authority scope**: the insider group holds authority over the definition of acceptable work or competence in the domain, beyond control over participation membership. Check explicitly.
- **Exit architecture**: the specific cost of exit for members, and whether exit mechanisms exist. Check explicitly.

For each of the three structural preconditions, confirm whether it is active in this specific arrangement. Use the presence indicators in Step 2f below.

---

**2b. For the token-weighted governance body form**, the three structural preconditions are:

- **Exclusive revenue stream (treasury form)**: the treasury constitutes an exclusive resource controlled by token holders. Non-token-holders cannot access treasury funds through the coordination process regardless of their contribution to the domain the organization coordinates within. Access requires coordination-weight approval over collectively held assets, not automatic distribution by roster membership. This differs structurally from the guild's charter-monopoly stream: capture is achieved through control of coordination decisions rather than through control of admission to the membership list.
- **Insider membership authority (capital-weighted topology)**: decision-making authority is proportional to capital holdings rather than to roster membership. The effective insider group is defined by token concentration — empirically, the top 10% of token holders hold approximately 76.2% of voting power across measured DAO populations. No formal insider roster exists; "insidership" is determined by token holdings. No external party has binding override authority on coordination decisions that pass the token-weighted threshold.
- **External legitimation**: most token-weighted governance bodies are recognized by a founding foundation, grants committee, or protocol ecosystem entity whose endorsement makes the organization's coordination outcomes authoritative. Foundation recognition, ecosystem adoption, and external auditor recognition are the primary legitimator types. Revocability varies by legitimator.

Two additional preconditions are contingent:
- **Epistemic authority scope**: present in protocol DAOs that set the parameters of a deployed protocol (coordination decisions determine what the standard implementation is); absent in treasury-only DAOs without protocol parameter authority.
- **Exit architecture**: structural in most token-weighted governance bodies — token sale, ragequit, and forking are designed mechanisms. Characterize as protective (ragequit protects minorities), destabilizing (coordinated token sale creates bank-run risk), or both.

For each of the three structural preconditions, confirm whether it is active using the presence indicators in Step 2f below.

**2c. For the professional certification body form**, the three structural preconditions are:

- **Exclusive revenue stream (statutory market exclusivity)**: state licensing creates legally enforced market exclusivity. Non-licensed practitioners cannot legally compete in the defined practice domain; unlicensed practice carries criminal or civil penalties. This is the strongest instantiation of the exclusive revenue stream precondition: the exclusivity is legally enforced, not merely financially preferred.
- **Insider membership authority (credentialing-authority topology)**: the professional body's board (drawn from senior practitioners) controls the examination process, competency standard definitions, disciplinary procedures, and specialty recognition criteria. No external party has binding override authority on board decisions within the statutory scope. The insider authority topology is credentialing-authority: insiders control both admission criteria and the definition of what counts as competent work.
- **External legitimation (statutory — lowest revocability)**: state statutory licensing is the lowest-revocability legitimation across all institutional forms. The body's exclusive claim is enforced by state criminal and civil law. Regulatory reference (professional standards embedded in insurance requirements, hospital credentialing, court admissibility rules) typically adds a second layer of mutually reinforcing legitimation.

Two additional preconditions are present at the structural level in the full professional certification body form (not contingent):
- **Epistemic authority scope**: structural (not contingent) in the professional certification body form. The state explicitly delegates authority to define what competent practice is alongside market exclusivity. This is the defining structural distinction from the craft guild, where epistemic authority scope is contingent.
- **Exit architecture**: partial with asymmetric cost. Departure forfeits credentials, established practice, referral networks, and insurance access. Exit is harder for practitioners with more accumulated professional capital.

---

**2d. For the standards body and technical working group form**, the three structural preconditions are:

- **Definitional authority claim**: the body produces specifications that function as the authoritative definition of compliance in a domain. Adoption by regulators, markets, or procurers creates economic consequences for practitioners contingent on the body's definitional decisions, regardless of whether those practitioners participated in the process. This is the primary exclusivity claim — definitional rather than financial in its direct mechanism, but financial in its consequences.
- **Insider membership authority (resource-asymmetry-in-consensus topology)**: nominal participation openness coexists with effective insider control through resource asymmetry. The effective insider group is defined by who can sustain long-term engagement in the specification process: large organizations with dedicated standards staff, incumbents with implementation experience, and actors with resources for sustained committee engagement across multi-year development cycles. Closure operates through procedural and resource exhaustion rather than admission denial.
- **External legitimation**: regulatory reference (standard embedded in regulation, requiring regulatory revision to displace) and market adoption (de facto standard requiring market coordination to displace). When both are present and mutually reinforcing, revocability is very low.

Two additional preconditions require explicit checking:
- **Exclusive revenue stream**: contingent in standards bodies. Present when the body charges access fees for specifications (ISO, IEC pattern) or controls a certification program with access fees. Absent when specifications are freely published (IETF, W3C, NIST pattern).
- **Epistemic authority scope**: structural and elevated. The body's authority is constitutive of what the domain's terms mean. Capture operates by writing specifications that match incumbent implementations, making alternatives definitionally non-conformant.

## Step 3: Surface the Generative Precondition Profile

Generative preconditions are structural features whose *absence* enables failure modes by removing the conditions that would constrain exploitation. All four institutional forms in the current ontology are structurally absent on all four generative preconditions, though the degree of absence varies by form. Both the compressive precondition profile (Step 2) and the generative precondition profile (Step 3) are required inputs for the tensegrity configuration assessment (Step 6).

For each generative precondition, state whether it is: **present** (the structural mechanism described exists and is operative), **partially present** (a mechanism exists that partially serves the function but does not meet the full structural criterion), or **absent** (no structural mechanism for the described function exists).

---

**The four generative preconditions:**

**Shared constitutional language**: Does the arrangement have explicit vocabulary, acknowledged as load-bearing by participants, for addressing foundational questions about what the arrangement has authority over — what it is sovereign over, what constitutes a legitimate override of ordinary coordination decisions, and what the social contract is that participants are party to? The test: when a constitutional dispute arises (not an operational dispute), is there a shared vocabulary for framing and adjudicating it that does not collapse into resolution by incumbent authority alone? Note: procedural rules for ordinary coordination decisions do not satisfy this criterion. The test is whether a dispute about the nature and limits of the coordination process itself can be adjudicated through a named framework distinct from ordinary arrangement authority.

**Standing architecture for affected parties**: Does the arrangement have formal, non-discretionary participation rights for parties who bear consequences from the arrangement's decisions but are not defined members or participants? The test: can someone outside the defined party list formally contest a coordination decision that materially harms them, through a process that does not depend on the insider group's discretion to hear the objection? Note: informal voice, advisory input, and discretionary consultation do not satisfy this criterion. The mechanism must be non-discretionary — the insider group cannot unilaterally decline to hear it.

**Conflict processing capacity**: Does the arrangement have mechanisms for absorbing irresolvable disagreement without routing it to exit, override, or indefinite suppression? The test: when two positions within the arrangement are genuinely irresolvable under ordinary coordination mechanisms, does a structural alternative exist beyond: (a) one position overriding the other by arrangement authority, (b) one party exiting, or (c) the disagreement persisting unaddressed indefinitely? Note: dispute resolution mechanisms for resolvable disputes do not satisfy this criterion. The mechanism must address genuinely irresolvable positions — those where neither party can be obligated to accept the other's position as legitimate.

**Independent verification of nominal purpose**: Does the arrangement have a structural mechanism for independently assessing whether its operations serve its stated nominal purpose, as distinct from the insiders' effective agenda? The test: can an independent party — not drawn from the insider group and not dependent on the insider group for their assessment authority — verify that the arrangement's outputs match its nominal purpose claims? Note: self-assessment, member-conducted review, and legitimator assessments that rely on the insider group's information provision do not satisfy this criterion. The independence requirement means the verification authority must be structurally insulated from the insider group.

---

**Form-specific notes:**

*Craft guild form:* All four generative preconditions are structurally absent. No guild-form arrangement has constitutional language for adjudicating disputes about what the body has authority over (constitutional disputes are resolved by the arrangement authority of current members). Affected non-members — practitioners outside the roster in the same domain — have no formal standing. Conflict processing is absent; irresolvable internal disputes route to exit (departure from the roster) or suppression. Independent verification is absent; the legitimator's endorsement does not include independent verification of whether the arrangement serves its stated nominal purpose vs. member interests.

*Token-weighted governance body form:* All four generative preconditions are structurally absent. No token-weighted governance body has constitutional language for "code vs. community" disputes (the Ethereum 2016 hard fork is the canonical case). Protocol users who hold no governance tokens have no formal standing despite bearing all costs of coordination decisions. Conflict processing is absent; the only responses to irresolvable disagreement are exit (token sale, ragequit) or fork — both are fragmentation, not processing. Independent verification is absent; no DAO coordination structure currently requires independent assessment of whether funded work produces public goods vs. token-correlated private goods.

*Professional certification body form:* All four generative preconditions are structurally absent. The body has no constitutional vocabulary distinguishing legitimate competency-standard setting from standard-setting that serves incumbent interests; both use the same credentialing authority mechanism. Affected non-members (patients, clients, and community practitioners) have no formal standing in licensing board proceedings. Formal public comment processes exist but are discretionary — the board is not obligated to receive challenges from non-practitioners on the same terms as practitioner input. Conflict processing is absent; formal objection procedures in licensing board hearings function as suppression with documentation. Independent verification is absent; no licensing body currently requires independent assessment of whether its competency standards correlate with field outcomes for service recipients.

*Standards body and technical working group form:* All four generative preconditions are absent, with one partial note for conflict processing. Shared constitutional language is absent: no standards body has vocabulary distinguishing legitimate specification advancement from captured specification advancement when both use identical procedural steps (the OOXML ISO/IEC JTC1 case is the canonical documentation of this absence). Standing for independent implementers and downstream users is absent. Conflict processing is **partially present** in bodies with formal objection procedures (ISO formal objection, W3C formal objection) — these preserve minority positions but function as suppression with documentation; the minority position is recorded but does not structurally affect the outcome. For bodies without formal objection paths (many IETF working groups), conflict processing is completely absent. Independent verification of nominal purpose is absent; independent implementability testing is external academic research, not a structural process requirement.

## Step 4: Check the Contingent Preconditions

For any form, check both contingent preconditions explicitly, using the form-specific notes from Step 2.

**4a. Epistemic authority scope:** Does the insider group hold authority over the definition of what counts as competent or legitimate work in the domain — not just who is admitted, but what practices are legitimate? For the professional certification body form: this is structural, not contingent — confirm its scope and extent. For the standards body form: this is structural at the definitional level — confirm whether the specification has become constitutive of what the domain's implementations are taken to be. For the craft guild and token-weighted governance body forms: check whether it has been acquired contingently.

**4b. Exit architecture:** What exit rights exist for members? Is exit designed (ragequit, fork right, or equivalent mechanism) or undesigned (exit requires abandonment of accumulated position)? What does a departing member forfeit? Is exit asymmetric? For the token-weighted governance body form: identify which designed exit mechanisms are present and whether they are protective, destabilizing, or both.

## Step 5: Surface the Failure Mode Profile

For each active precondition, surface the failure modes it activates. State the mechanism in one sentence and identify the affected party classes outside the arrangement's defined party list. Compressive preconditions activate FM1-FM11. Generative precondition absences activate FM12-FM15.

Select the section matching the form identified in Step 1.

---

### Craft guild form

**Exclusive revenue stream activates:**

- **FM1: Rent extraction and monopoly closure.** Mechanism: the exclusive revenue stream creates a structural incentive to treat admission control as a rent-protection mechanism rather than a quality-assurance mechanism — criteria are maintained or tightened to reduce the number of rent recipients, not because they correlate with better field outcomes. Co-activation: requires insider membership authority and external legitimation simultaneously. Timeline: immediate to short-term. Affected parties: practitioners meeting the criterion who cannot access the revenue source; downstream service recipients paying monopoly prices; new domain entrants.
- **FM7: Elite symbiosis.** Mechanism: the legitimator provides recognition; the insider group provides field navigation, transaction cost reduction, and political support; the structural alignment of interests produces behavioral convergence independent of any explicit coordination. Co-activation: requires external legitimation and insider membership authority. Timeline: short to medium-term. Affected parties: practitioners outside the body disadvantaged by legitimator preference; parties who depend on the legitimator for independent oversight and do not receive it.
- **FM9: Persistence despite inefficiency** (co-activation with external legitimation): the stream funds capacity to maintain legitimator relationships and absorb dysfunction without reform pressure.

**Insider membership authority activates:**

- **FM2: Exclusion by ascribed identity.** Mechanism: admission authority without binding external accountability enables the insider group to coordinate on exclusion criteria that serve shared interests regardless of field-criterion correlation. Co-activation: none required. Timeline: immediate. Affected parties: practitioners meeting the criterion excluded on ascribed characteristics; communities losing access to excluded practitioners.
- **FM3: Dynastic capture and hereditary closure.** Mechanism: admission authority combined with human social networks produces systematic preference for applicants connected to existing members, making membership self-replicating over time. Co-activation: none required. Timeline: medium-term. Affected parties: practitioners meeting the criterion without social connections to current members; the domain losing contribution from practitioners outside the sponsorship network.
- **FM4: Quota-based supply restriction.** Mechanism: combined with an exclusive revenue stream, each admission reduces per-member share, creating the economically rational incentive to limit total admissions. Co-activation: exclusive revenue stream required. Timeline: short-term. Affected parties: qualified practitioners prevented by admission limits; service recipients without adequate practitioner access.
- **FM5: Quality theater.** Mechanism: admission criteria stated as quality standards that function as barriers — rigorous in appearance, calibrated to incumbent pathways, systematically excluding practitioners who learned through alternative routes. Co-activation: exclusive revenue stream required. Timeline: short to medium-term. Affected parties: practitioners following alternative learning pathways; innovation-route practitioners.
- **FM6: Innovation suppression.** Mechanism: admission decisions made by incumbents whose expertise and standing derive from established methods create structural incentives to define criteria that are satisfiable primarily by practitioners who followed the incumbent approach. Co-activation: none required; amplified when epistemic authority scope is also active. Timeline: variable. Affected parties: practitioners using newer or alternative methods; service recipients; the domain.
- **FM8: War on illegitimate producers.** Mechanism: admission authority combined with external legitimation gives the insider group both the standing and the enforcement channel to pursue suppression of parallel practitioners. Co-activation: external legitimation required. Timeline: short to medium-term. Affected parties: practitioners outside the body producing qualifying work; service recipients losing access when suppression succeeds.
- **FM10: Conservatism against outsiders.** Mechanism: social capital within a bounded insider group systematically advantages insiders over outsiders in all interactions the group mediates, regardless of explicit intent. Co-activation: none required. Timeline: immediate. Affected parties: practitioners seeking admission; the domain, receiving narrower inputs.

**External legitimation activates:**

- **FM7: Elite symbiosis** (primary treatment above under exclusive revenue stream).
- **FM9: Persistence despite inefficiency.** Mechanism: external legitimation converts the body's exclusive claims from contingent to structurally protected, so the body persists even when failing to serve its stated purpose because the legitimator's structural interest in the body's continuation is independent of the body's performance. Co-activation: exclusive revenue stream (funds legitimator relationship maintenance) and insider membership authority (provides organizational continuity). Timeline: long-term. Affected parties: practitioners excluded by the persisting body; service recipients receiving inferior outcomes; future parties inheriting the institutional lock-in.

**Generative precondition absences activate:**

- **FM12: Constitutional capture.** Mechanism: absence of shared constitutional language means constitutional disputes — about what the body has authority over and what constitutes a legitimate challenge to coordination decisions — are resolved by incumbent authority by default. The faction that holds arrangement authority at the moment of constitutional dispute becomes the de facto constitutional authority; its frame is retrospectively constituted as the body's constitutional position. Co-activation: insider membership authority (provides the arrangement authority that captures the constitutional frame). Affected parties: members whose constitutional challenges are resolved against them; the domain, receiving coordination decisions insulated from constitutional accountability.
- **FM13: Externality blindness.** Mechanism: absence of standing architecture for affected parties means coordination optimization for member interests produces costs for non-members — practitioners outside the roster, service recipients, domain participants — with no structural channel through which these costs are visible to coordination decisions. The insiders are not necessarily malicious; the arrangement is structurally blind to costs it cannot formally receive. Co-activation: insider membership authority and exclusive revenue stream (both concentrate pressure in the same direction as the standing absence). Affected parties: practitioners outside the roster bearing supply restriction costs; service recipients bearing monopoly pricing costs; communities dependent on practitioners outside the body.
- **FM14: Deadlock fragmentation and forced suppression.** Mechanism: absence of conflict processing capacity means irresolvable internal disagreements route to exit or suppression as the only structural outcomes. Persistent minority positions are either absorbed into the majority frame (suppression) or trigger departure of the minority (fragmentation and loss of that perspective from institutional record). Co-activation: none required; amplified by exit architecture characteristics. Affected parties: minority-position members whose perspectives disappear from institutional record; the domain, losing the diversity that irresolvable disagreements make visible.
- **FM15: Nominal purpose shield.** Mechanism: absence of independent verification means the arrangement's nominal purpose claim — protecting the quality of work in the domain — cannot be distinguished from an effective agenda of protecting incumbent income from competition. The nominal purpose claim shields the effective agenda from scrutiny by both legitimators and reform advocates; challenging the body requires challenging the nominal purpose (quality protection), not merely the effective agenda. Co-activation: external legitimation (the legitimator endorses the nominal purpose without independent verification, reinforcing the shield). Affected parties: practitioners outside the body who are excluded nominally for quality reasons; service recipients whose interests are served by the nominal purpose claim but not by the effective agenda.

### Token-weighted governance body form

**Exclusive revenue stream (treasury form) activates:**

- **FM1: Rent extraction and monopoly closure.** Mechanism: decision-making authority over a treasury creates a structural incentive for token holders to route treasury resources toward their own interests through coordination proposals — compensation for coordination participation, grants to affiliated projects, protocol parameters that advantage holding over contributing. Co-activation: insider membership authority (capital-weighted) required. Timeline: immediate to short-term. Affected parties: contributors to the domain who are not token holders; ecosystem participants bearing costs of insider-oriented treasury allocation.
- **FM7: Elite symbiosis.** Mechanism: foundations and ecosystem funders direct resources to DAOs that exhibit coordination structures aligned with funder preferences; DAOs in turn provide field navigation and legitimation for funders' broader ecosystem investments; neither party needs to act improperly for the exchange to produce behavioral convergence. Co-activation: all three preconditions required. Timeline: short to medium-term. Affected parties: contributors outside the DAO whose work is deprioritized by the foundation-DAO relationship; parties depending on the foundation for independent oversight.

**Insider membership authority (capital-weighted topology) activates:**

- **FM3: Dynastic capture (capital form).** Mechanism: founding token distribution creates initial capital concentration; those with coordination power control the decisions — treasury allocation, protocol parameters, distribution structures — that would reduce their concentration; the coordination power is self-reinforcing as long as the treasury retains resources the token holders value. Co-activation: none required; amplified by exclusive revenue stream. Timeline: immediate (founding distribution determines the concentration; the dynamic begins at token generation). Affected parties: contributors to the domain without coordination-scale token holdings; ecosystem participants who cannot influence coordination decisions regardless of their contribution.
- **FM6: Innovation suppression (protocol DAO variant).** Activated when epistemic authority scope is present (protocol DAOs only). Mechanism: coordination bodies composed of token holders with stakes in the current protocol implementation create structural incentives to define protocol upgrades conservatively — preserving existing implementations and avoiding changes that would require significant adaptation by large token holders. Co-activation: epistemic authority scope active. Timeline: variable. Affected parties: protocol users who would benefit from technically superior implementations; developers proposing upgrades that threaten incumbent implementations.
- **FM10: Conservatism against outsiders.** Mechanism: coordination participation advantages well-established token holders over new contributors regardless of technical contribution quality; forum culture, coordination history, and interpersonal relationships among large token holders systematically advantage the existing coordination class over new participants. Co-activation: none required. Timeline: immediate. Affected parties: new contributors to the protocol or community; token holders without coordination-scale holdings.

**External legitimation activates:**

- **FM7: Elite symbiosis** (primary treatment above).
- **FM9: Persistence despite inefficiency.** Mechanism: treasury resources fund continued operation; foundation recognition sustains legitimation; the DAO persists even when its coordination is captured, its treasury is depleted, or its stated purpose is no longer being served, because the legitimation relationship is independent of the DAO's performance. Co-activation: all three preconditions required. Timeline: medium to long-term in crypto context (faster political cycles than historical guild form but similar structural dynamic). Affected parties: protocol users relying on the coordination body for protocol stewardship; token holders outside the effective insider group.

**Note on FM2, FM4, FM5 in token-weighted governance bodies:** FM2 (exclusion by ascribed identity) is structurally weaker in this form than in guild-form bodies because admission operates through capital acquisition rather than roster approval — no individual's ascribed characteristics are directly at issue. FM4 (quota-based supply restriction) and FM5 (quality theater as admission barriers) are largely absent because there is no formal admission roster; "admission" is open to anyone who acquires tokens at market price. These failure modes may reappear in coordination structure design (e.g., working groups or delegate structures within the DAO that do use roster-based admission), which should be assessed separately.

**Generative precondition absences activate:**

- **FM12: Constitutional capture.** Mechanism: absence of shared constitutional language means disputes about whether "code is law" or "community is sovereign" are resolved by incumbent authority at the moment of crisis; the faction holding arrangement authority at the time of the constitutional dispute captures the constitutional frame as precedent. The Ethereum 2016 hard fork is the canonical case: two incompatible constitutional frames operated in the same body with no shared vocabulary for adjudicating between them. Co-activation: insider membership authority (capital-weighted — the constitutional frame is captured by whichever party holds sufficient tokens to execute the resolution). Affected parties: minority-position participants whose constitutional positions are overridden; protocol users bearing the consequences of constitutional decisions made without their standing.
- **FM13: Externality blindness.** Mechanism: absence of standing architecture means protocol users who hold no governance tokens bear all costs of coordination decisions — fee changes, parameter updates, protocol modifications — with no structural channel through which their interests enter coordination deliberation. This is the highest-severity overlap in the token-weighted governance body form: the capital/coordination direction (compressed by both exclusive revenue stream and capital-weighted insider authority simultaneously) intersects with the participation direction (tension-depleted by standing absence) to make coordination decisions structurally insensitive to the interests of the parties most directly affected by them. Co-activation: insider membership authority and exclusive revenue stream (the token conflates financial interest and decision-making authority in one instrument, making insider interests and user interests structurally inseparable but invisible to coordination decisions). Affected parties: non-token-holding protocol users bearing fee increases, parameter changes, and protocol modifications as coordination externalities.
- **FM14: Deadlock fragmentation and forced suppression.** Mechanism: absence of conflict processing capacity means irresolvable coordination disagreements produce fragmentation (fork) or suppression; the Nouns DAO fork case documents treasury dissipation through fork without resolution; the Compound GoldenBoyz case documents minority warnings being publicly stated without institutional acknowledgment, enabling the attack to proceed. Co-activation: none required; exit architecture in the token-weighted form makes fragmentation structurally easier (ragequit, fork mechanisms designed into the coordination structure) and therefore the default outcome of irresolvable disagreement. Affected parties: protocol users who lose coordination coverage when fragmentation occurs; contributors whose minority positions disappear from institutional record.
- **FM15: Nominal purpose shield.** Mechanism: absence of independent verification means nominal purpose claims (public goods, protocol advancement, community benefit) cannot be distinguished from treasury allocations serving token-correlated private interests without external academic or civil society research that is not structurally required; participants may sincerely hold the nominal purpose belief while the structure produces token-correlated outputs. Co-activation: external legitimation (foundations validate public goods claims without independent verification, reinforcing the shield). Affected parties: ecosystem participants whose public goods needs are not served by coordination decisions nominally made in their interest; funders whose resources are allocated to token-correlated returns under a public goods nominal purpose.

### Professional certification body form

All ten guild failure modes apply to the professional certification body in amplified form, because all three structural preconditions operate at their strongest instantiation. The following amplifications and additions are specific to this form:

**Exclusive revenue stream (statutory market exclusivity) activates:**

- **FM1: Rent extraction and monopoly closure (amplified).** Mechanism: statutory market exclusivity creates legally enforced monopoly pricing; the rent is not merely above-market income but is structurally sustained by criminal prosecution of unlicensed competitors. FM1 is harder to challenge in the professional certification body form than in the guild form because challenging the body requires engaging the legislative process. Affected parties: practitioners meeting field criteria who lack or cannot obtain licensure; service recipients bearing monopoly pricing costs; new entrants unable to access the market without navigating statutory credentialing.
- **FM8: War on illegitimate producers (amplified).** Mechanism: the statutory licensing authority gives the body access to criminal and civil enforcement mechanisms for suppressing unlicensed practice; unauthorized practice prosecution with criminal sanctions goes substantially further than the guild's petition-based suppression. Affected parties: community practitioners, paralegal services, alternative practitioners, and others performing qualifying work outside the licensed practice definition; service recipients who lose access when suppression succeeds.

**Insider membership authority (credentialing-authority topology) activates:**

- **FM5: Quality theater (amplified).** Mechanism: complex credentialing requirements (multiple examinations, residency requirements, specialty certifications) are formally justified as quality assurance but are calibrated to incumbent training pathways and systematically exclude practitioners who learned through alternative routes; the statutory standing of these requirements makes them harder to challenge as barriers than guild-era apprenticeship requirements. Affected parties: practitioners who developed competence outside standard credentialing pathways; those who cannot afford the time and financial cost of credentialing regardless of competence.
- **FM6: Innovation suppression (structurally protected form).** Mechanism: statutory epistemic authority allows the body to define emerging practices (new surgical techniques, new legal theories, alternative treatment modalities) as outside legitimate practice until adoption by established practitioners becomes unavoidable for the body to maintain relevance; the statutory standing of the practice definition makes innovation suppression legally enforceable rather than merely culturally entrenched. Affected parties: practitioners using or developing newer methods; patients and clients who would benefit from innovative approaches the body has categorized as non-standard.

**External legitimation (statutory — lowest revocability) activates:**

- **FM9: Persistence despite inefficiency (full expression, lowest revocability).** Mechanism: statutory legitimation means the body persists indefinitely absent legislative reform regardless of whether its credentialing process correlates with field outcomes; the legitimator's structural interest (the state receives a self-regulating profession that reduces oversight costs) is independent of the body's performance; correction pressure does not produce reform because the body can mobilize political resources drawn from the exclusive revenue stream. Affected parties: practitioners excluded by the persisting body despite adequate competence; service recipients receiving inferior outcomes from the protected practitioner pool; future generations inheriting the institutional lock-in.

**Active failure modes summary for professional certification body:** FM1, FM2, FM3, FM4, FM5, FM6, FM7, FM8, FM9, FM10 are all active in the full statutory form. FM1, FM6, FM8, FM9 are in their strongest cross-form instantiation. FM2 and FM3 are historically documented and present in amplified form due to statutory barriers. FM4 is present when licensure limits supply (medical school quotas, residency caps). FM7 is present in the full state-profession exchange structure.

**Generative precondition absences activate:**

- **FM12: Constitutional capture.** Mechanism: absence of shared constitutional language means disputes about whether the body's credentialing authority is serving quality or serving incumbents are resolved by the authority of the credentialing board itself — the body whose constitutional position is in dispute is simultaneously the adjudicator of the dispute. Constitutional disputes about the scope and limits of practitioner-dominated board authority have no vocabulary for resolution outside the board's own authority. The AMA/Flexner case (1910) is the canonical instance: what counted as legitimate medical education was determined by the body whose incumbents controlled the existing educational institutions, with no constitutional vocabulary for distinguishing quality from guild protection. Affected parties: practitioners whose constitutional challenges to credentialing standards are adjudicated by the body they are challenging; service recipients whose interests in accessible practice are constitutionally overridden without standing to participate in the adjudication.
- **FM13: Externality blindness.** Mechanism: absence of standing architecture means patients, clients, community practitioners, and service recipients — parties who bear direct costs from supply restriction, access barriers, and practice definition limitations — have no formal standing in credentialing board proceedings. Advisory processes exist in some jurisdictions, but they are discretionary; the board is not obligated to hear challenges from non-practitioners on equal terms with practitioner input. This is the highest-severity overlap in the professional certification body form: all four compressive preconditions (statutory exclusivity, credentialing authority, statutory legitimation, structural epistemic authority) compress simultaneously in the epistemic/admission direction, while the standing absence creates a participation direction that is completely tension-depleted. The combination is the most legally protected overlap across all four forms. Affected parties: patients and clients bearing the costs of supply restriction and monopoly pricing; community practitioners and paraprofessionals excluded from authorized practice; communities in underserved regions without access to licensed practitioners.
- **FM14: Deadlock fragmentation and forced suppression.** Mechanism: formal hearing procedures in licensing board proceedings technically preserve minority positions, but function as suppression with documentation: the minority position is recorded, the board adjudicates, and if the majority position prevails, the minority has no institutional path for continued challenge without external legislative intervention. Legislative intervention is the only genuine conflict processing mechanism, and it is structurally external to the body. Within the body's own structure, irresolvable disagreements route to suppression by incumbent authority. Affected parties: board members whose minority positions are systematically overridden; reform advocates within the profession whose documented objections produce no institutional response.
- **FM15: Nominal purpose shield.** Mechanism: absence of independent verification means the body's nominal purpose claim (protecting public safety and service quality) cannot be structurally distinguished from an effective agenda of incumbent income protection. The nominal purpose claim is reinforced by statutory legitimation: the state endorsed the body to protect the public, so challenging the body requires challenging that endorsement. The AMA/Flexner case again: the 1910 report was produced by a body (Carnegie Foundation) with substantial institutional alignment with the medical schools the report favored, and the nominal purpose (quality in medical education) functioned as a shield for what was effectively a consolidation of medical education into a smaller number of allopathic institutions. Co-activation: external legitimation (statutory legitimators validate the nominal purpose without independent verification). Affected parties: practitioners using approaches the body has categorized as non-standard; service recipients whose access is limited by practice definition restrictions made under the nominal purpose of quality protection.

### Standards body and technical working group form

**Definitional authority claim activates:**

- **FM6: Innovation suppression (definitional form).** Mechanism: specifications written by participants whose existing implementations match the specification's requirements foreclose technically superior alternatives that do not conform — alternative approaches are not merely disfavored but categorically non-conformant under the definition; as external adoption accumulates, the specification becomes what the thing is, and development activity concentrates on conformant approaches, progressively excluding non-conformant alternatives from the recognized solution space. Co-activation: external legitimation required for load-bearing definitional authority. Timeline: variable, accelerating with adoption (5-15 years from initial publication to full expression). Affected parties: innovators working on technically superior alternatives that do not conform to the incumbent specification; end users of systems in the domain, who receive suboptimal implementations because the specification locked in incumbent approaches.
- **FM5: Quality theater (complexity form).** Mechanism: specifications incorporating large numbers of optional features, complex conformance profiles, and implementation-specific requirements advantage incumbents who participated in drafting and already have conformant systems; the cost of demonstrating compliance scales with complexity, functioning as a participation barrier that disadvantages smaller or newer implementers. Co-activation: insider membership authority (resource-asymmetry-in-consensus). Timeline: short to medium-term. Affected parties: small implementers, open-source developers, and actors from under-resourced communities who cannot absorb compliance demonstration costs; end users whose needs are not represented by the incumbent implementers.
- **FM11: Epistemic monoculture.** Mechanism: once a specification achieves broad ecosystem adoption, domain knowledge base converges on the specification's approach; practitioners trained after adoption know only the conformant approach; alternative technical traditions atrophy as talent and resources concentrate on conformant development; by the second or third post-adoption generation of practitioners, the specification's approach is not merely the standard but what the domain is understood to be. Co-activation: external legitimation at high level; temporal depth (20+ years). Timeline: long-term (generational). Affected parties: practitioners trained after monoculture consolidation, who lack comparative technical knowledge to challenge the standard's limitations; the domain itself, which loses the diversity of approaches that drives innovation; future users in domains where better alternatives would have served them.

**Insider membership authority (resource-asymmetry-in-consensus topology) activates:**

- **FM7: Elite symbiosis.** Mechanism: standards bodies provide regulators and major market actors with technical reference that reduces their oversight burden; legitimators provide regulatory embedding and market adoption that sustains the body's definitional authority; the structural exchange produces alignment between the insider group (effectively the large organizations whose staff dominate the working groups) and the legitimating parties (regulators, major market actors, procurement systems) without any individual body receiving an explicit condition. Co-activation: all three preconditions required. Timeline: short to medium-term. Affected parties: smaller implementers and innovators whose technical perspectives are systematically underrepresented in specification content; regulators and procurers who receive specifications calibrated to insider interests rather than field-optimal outcomes.
- **FM10: Conservatism against outsiders.** Mechanism: procedural familiarity, accumulated working group relationships, and established technical positions within the standards body systematically advantage long-term participants over new entrants regardless of technical merit; individual comments by new participants face higher evidential burdens than equivalent comments from established working group members. Co-activation: none required. Timeline: immediate. Affected parties: practitioners and developers entering the standards process; technical communities whose approaches are outside the working group's established vocabulary.

**External legitimation activates:**

- **FM9: Persistence despite inefficiency.** Mechanism: regulatory embedding sustains obsolete specifications through regulatory inertia; the body can persist and continue producing specifications without technical merit because the sunk cost of existing compliant implementations sustains demand for conformance guidance and because the regulatory reference insulates the specification from technical arguments for revision. Co-activation: all three preconditions required. Timeline: long-term (regulatory revision processes operate on multi-year to multi-decade timescales). Affected parties: practitioners who need technically updated specifications; end users of systems that implement obsolete specifications; innovators whose technically superior approaches cannot displace the embedded specification.

**Note on FM1, FM2, FM3, FM4, FM8 in standards bodies:** FM1 (rent extraction) is primarily absent unless the body charges access fees for specifications or controls a revenue-generating certification program. FM2 (exclusion by ascribed identity) is structurally weaker — the resource-asymmetry topology excludes through resource barriers rather than roster denial based on identity, though the resource barriers may correlate with ascribed characteristics in practice. FM3 (dynastic capture) is weaker than in roster-based forms but present in attenuated form through sustained participation networks. FM4 (quota restriction) is absent. FM8 (war on illegitimate producers) is weaker — standards bodies typically do not have enforcement mechanisms against non-conformant implementers directly; FM8 expression occurs when regulatory reference creates prosecution mechanisms for non-conformance.

**Generative precondition absences activate:**

- **FM12: Constitutional capture.** Mechanism: absence of shared constitutional language means disputes about whether a specification advancement is legitimate or captured use identical procedural steps, with no constitutional vocabulary for distinguishing between them. The procedural steps that advanced a captured specification become, retrospectively, the constitutional definition of legitimate specification advancement because there is no other vocabulary for the distinction. The OOXML ISO/IEC JTC1 fast-track case (2007-2008) is the canonical documentation: ballot-stuffing through new national member body creation and the original specification advancement both used the same procedural vocabulary; the body had no constitutional language to distinguish them. Co-activation: insider membership authority (the arrangement authority that captures the constitutional frame is held by incumbents with resources for sustained participation). Affected parties: independent implementers who challenge specification legitimacy through procedures that the captured frame can declare procedurally invalid; domain participants whose alternative technical approaches are constitutionally foreclosed.
- **FM13: Externality blindness.** Mechanism: absence of standing architecture means independent implementers and downstream users — parties who must implement the specification, bear the costs of implementation-specific requirements, and use the systems built to the specification — have no formal standing to contest specification content. Participation in working groups is technically open but practically limited by resource requirements; downstream users have no formal standing at all. Co-activation: insider membership authority and definitional authority claim (the specification text shapes what is technically possible in the domain, concentrating all compressive pressure in the epistemic/definitional direction while the participation direction is completely tension-depleted). Affected parties: independent software developers bearing conformance demonstration costs; users of systems built to specifications that advantage incumbent implementations; communities in under-resourced regions whose technical contexts are not represented in specification working groups.
- **FM14: Deadlock fragmentation and forced suppression.** Mechanism: formal objection procedures in bodies that have them (ISO, W3C) preserve minority positions but function as suppression with documentation: the minority objection is recorded, adjudicated by a body whose composition favors the majority position, and if overruled, the specification advances with the minority position in the record but without institutional effect. In bodies without formal objection procedures (many IETF working groups), irresolvable disagreements route to rough-consensus override or working group abandonment. Co-activation: none required; partially offset in bodies with formal objection procedures (partial presence of conflict processing). Affected parties: working group participants whose technical objections are formally overruled; domain practitioners whose concerns about specification implementability are recorded but not addressed.
- **FM15: Nominal purpose shield.** Mechanism: absence of independent verification means the nominal purpose claim (open technical standardization for interoperability and the public interest) cannot be distinguished from specifications calibrated to incumbent implementations without independent implementability testing that is not structurally required. The nominal purpose claim — open, interoperable, public interest — shields specification content from challenge by framing challenges to incumbent-advantaged specifications as challenges to interoperability and the public interest. This is the highest-severity overlap in the standards body form: it compounds fastest via the technical possibility constraint (specification text shapes what is technically possible, so each specification cycle in which independent implementability goes unverified adds another layer of constraint that is costly to reverse). Co-activation: external legitimation (regulators and market actors validate the nominal purpose without independent verification). Affected parties: independent implementers who cannot implement the specification without undocumented incumbent implementation knowledge; domain participants whose technical alternatives are foreclosed by specifications nominally written for the public interest.

## Step 6: Assess the Tensegrity Configuration

The tensegrity configuration assessment synthesizes the compressive precondition profile (Step 2) and the generative precondition profile (Step 3) into a structural health assessment. This assessment does not require detailed quantitative analysis; it requires identifying which functional directions are under compression, which are tension-depleted, and where compressed and tension-depleted directions coincide (the most acute failure domains).

**6a. Aggregate type.** Based on the active compressive preconditions and generative precondition absences, state which tensegrity type applies:

- **T1: Full health.** Three or more structural compressive preconditions active and three or more generative preconditions present. Compressive and generative elements are balanced. (Not observed in practice for the four ontology forms at full institutional maturity.)
- **T2: Pre-stress depletion.** Generative preconditions partially present but not structurally grounded. Compressive preconditions active. The arrangement has not yet developed the generative structural capacity that would balance its compressive elements.
- **T3: Compression dominance without capture.** Three structural compressive preconditions active, all four generative preconditions absent. The arrangement has full compressive structure but no generative counterweight. Internal reform is still possible at this stage; the compressive dynamics are not yet self-reinforcing at the level that defeats internal reform.
- **T4: Tension dominance.** Generative conditions partially or fully established but compressive preconditions weak or absent. Rare in the four ontology forms; relevant for assessment of early-phase arrangements before external legitimation is acquired.
- **T5: Strategic compression.** One or more compressive preconditions deliberately suppressed to maintain legitimacy, while failure mode dynamics continue through remaining compressive elements. Present in reformed guild forms that have eliminated the exclusive revenue stream while retaining insider membership authority and external legitimation.
- **T6: Pathological compression.** All compressive preconditions at maximum expression; all generative conditions absent; compounding dynamics have operated long enough that internal reform is insufficient without external intervention. The T3-to-T6 transition is the relevant threshold to assess.
- **T7: Cascade disruption.** Compressive preconditions have produced structural failure that cannot be absorbed internally. Active dissolution, fragmentation, or external takeover. Assessment at T7 is forensic rather than preventive.

**6b. T3-to-T6 transition assessment.** Has the arrangement crossed the T3-to-T6 threshold? Indicators that the threshold has been crossed: (a) the founding legitimator has built operational dependencies on the current coordination structure such that reform would require renegotiating the legitimation relationship; (b) affected parties outside the defined list have no organizational capacity to contest the coordination structure through external channels; (c) the compressive preconditions are mutually reinforcing in a self-sustaining way (each precondition's presence makes the others more durable). State whether the threshold appears crossed, approaching, or not yet reached.

**6c. Anisotropy signature.** Name the compressed and tension-depleted functional directions:

*Compressed functional directions:* Which functional directions are under pressure from active compressive preconditions? Name each compressed direction and identify which preconditions are concentrating pressure in that direction. Note when multiple preconditions concentrate pressure in the same direction simultaneously (this is the highest-severity condition).

*Tension-depleted functional directions:* Which functional directions are structurally depleted by absent generative preconditions? Name each depleted direction and identify which generative condition absence is producing the depletion.

*Overlaps (most acute failure domains):* Where do compressed and tension-depleted directions coincide? These intersections are the highest-severity failure domains. Name the failure modes active at each overlap.

**6d. Configuration gradient.** If generative conditions were to be established, which would have the highest leverage? The leverage sequence is typically: (1) the generative condition that directly addresses the highest-severity compressed-depleted overlap; (2) conditions targeting the most revocable compressive element (the external legitimator is typically more revocable than insider authority or the revenue stream); (3) constitutional language and conflict processing together (these are mutually enabling and are more effective when developed together than separately).

## Step 7: State the Standing Limitation

State the following explicitly and in full in the context summary. This text is not optional and must appear verbatim:

"The Coordination Structural Integrity Suite assesses quality of execution within an arrangement's ongoing operation. It does not specify structural conditions for dissolution, sunset, mandatory re-chartering, performance-conditional external recognition, or any external correction mechanism that operates independently of the insider group's consent. An arrangement that is fully conformant under all nine standards can still exhibit persistence despite inefficiency (FM9) if all three structural preconditions — exclusive revenue stream, insider membership authority, and external legitimation — are simultaneously present and the external legitimation is durable. Suite conformance is a quality-of-execution assessment. It is not a finding that the arrangement's design should continue. This limitation applies to this assessment."

# Coordination Structural Integrity Suite - Full Audit
Use when you have enough context to describe a coordination system fully and want both layers at once: exploitation-vector audit across six standards, capacity-presence assessment across three standards, and a cross-layer analysis of where the two interact consequentially. This is the comprehensive version. It returns the most actionable findings when the system's governance structure, processes, and participant dynamics can all be described to the AI.

Copy everything below the line, paste it into any AI chat, and describe the coordination system or organization you want to audit.
---
You are auditing the described coordination system against the full Coordination Structural Integrity Suite: six Tensegrity Compressive Standards and three Tensegrity Generative Standards (CC BY 4.0, Regis Chapman).

The two layers have different logics. The compressive standards ask: are specific exploitation vectors closed? The generative standards ask: are the conditions for coordination surplus structurally present? Address them separately. Do not aggregate into a single score.

---

**Compressive Layer — Exploitation Vector Audit**

For each of the six compressive standards, identify whether the structural requirement is met and where the gaps are. A system can meet five standards fully and remain structurally exposed on the sixth.

**Standard 1: Precision-First Design Standard.** Every normative claim carries its structural mechanism. Precision fails in two directions: a precision deficit is a normative claim that cannot be evaluated by an independent observer; a precision imposition is a normative element specified so completely that compliance can be demonstrated while the coordination purpose it was designed to serve remains unserved. Unfalsifiable claims cannot carry architectural weight; overconstrained claims close off the channel for correction by making failure look like success. Ask: what is the structural mechanism that produces each claimed outcome? If it is not specified, the standard is not met. Ask also: can any party demonstrate full procedural compliance while the coordination purpose the procedure was designed to serve remains absent? Also ask: does each assessment require a contextual baseline when identical outputs would indicate structurally distinct conditions? And: does each normative mechanism adopted from a different structural context carry a baseline statement confirming congruence or specifying calibration adjustments?

**Standard 2: Adverse Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard.** Adverse signals are registered, engaged, and traced to a documented conclusion before dismissal. Ask: what was the most recent adverse signal this system received, and what happened to it? Is governance inaction on an acknowledged signal visible to participants without the cooperation of the inacting party? Is external governance override named as a signal class? Where relevant external authorities have realistic governance reach, does a recognition assessment of the system's self-governance capacity exist? Note also: audit results for this standard are bounded by the organization's Relational Battery condition. Absence of recorded adverse signals in an organization with a depleted Relational Battery is not evidence of structural health; it is evidence that the surfacing mechanism has failed. Note the Relational Battery condition in the audit findings.

**Standard 3: Structural Consent Legibility Standard.** Every consent-bearing interaction answers who is acting and who benefits; negotiated limits, bidirectional awareness, and revocability are structurally present. Ask: does the consent architecture answer both questions for each consent domain? Can participants set limits on what they are consenting to and revoke consent without incurring prohibitive costs? Is silence structurally distinguished from authorization? Also ask: does a durable, accessible definition of who holds participation rights exist as a structural precondition for per-action identification? Can it be modified unilaterally by parties holding concentrated authority? Is the action being consented to specified with sufficient precision for affected parties to evaluate whether they are materially affected before standing identification proceeds? Is the cost-bearing party identified and their consent obtained where the cost-bearing party is distinct from both the acting and benefiting parties? Are external legal requirements that can override explicit consent architecture named and disclosed to participants?

**Standard 4: Information Asymmetry Classification Standard.** All six primary asymmetry classes are evaluated for presence, named, disclosed, and subject to detection architecture. Ask: which classes are present and which are disclosed? Is the audience baseline declared where Descriptive Capacity Asymmetry (Extension Class C) applies? Within the Omission class, specifically check for governance record asymmetry: does the formal governance record capture actual decision-making, or only ratification? If proposals are pre-negotiated in private channels and formal voting is ratification theater, this is a distinct sub-pattern requiring architectural remediation (not just record-scope adjustment).

**Standard 5: Structural Power Obligation Standard.** Power is assessed across all three dimensions (authority, coordination, specialization) with authorized distribution profiles, legitimate authorization for concentration, and ongoing detection architecture. Ask: is decentralization a structural claim or a stated intention? Can a participant with below-median power understand who holds more and what recourse they have? Are concentration shifts classified as drift, evolution, pivot, or capture? Does a defined constituency exist as a precondition for the authorized distribution profile, and are modifications to that definition treated as authority-dimension concentration events? Does a legal entity structure underlie the system that holds override authority over participatory governance outcomes — and is that override authority identified, scoped, and disclosed to participants? In token-weighted systems, are both stake-weighted and participation-weighted distributions assessed independently? Where the system participates in a nested or polycentric governance structure: are the three invariants satisfied at each level? Is authority held at the lowest level capable of exercising it accountably (subsidiarity)? Where cross-boundary detection does not yet exist, is the gap disclosed?

**Standard 6: Regenerative Obligation Standard.** Extraction without proportional regenerative return is a structural violation. Three validity conditions must all be present simultaneously: non-fungibility (the return operates in the register of the harm, not a substitute register), proximity (the return reaches specific parties who bore the harm, verified through a harm-bearing affiliation registry), and embeddedness (the return operates within the relational structure that created the obligation). Ask: does the return architecture avoid the two categorical proximity disqualifiers — additionality logic and Social Return on Investment aggregation? Was the form of return determined in consultation with harm-bearing parties? Has the organization declared a stance (non-extractive or more regenerative than extractive) in a public, versioned location? Do harm-bearing parties have the structural conditions to register extraction as extraction, not only conditions under which it is recorded?

**Generative Layer — Capacity Presence Assessment**

For each of the three generative standards, identify whether the structural conditions for the capacity are present. This is a presence assessment: what evidence exists that the capacity is structurally real rather than intended?

**Standard 7: Sensemaking Standard.** Five invariants (disruption-occasioned, particular-to-general relating, action-entangled, sufficiency-oriented, temporally structured) at three scales (intra-personal, inter-personal, witness-reception). Ask: does this system produce shared language — joint artifacts neither party had produced independently? Is the liminal phase present in its sensemaking processes, or does the system jump directly from disruption to application of a pre-existing frame? Also establish the founding window condition: did the organization establish shared language at the constitutional layer before implicit coordination culture formed (constitutional adoption), or is it overlaid on a culture that already has its own implicit interpretive frames (remedial adoption)? Within remedial adoption, distinguish born-without (never had the founding window open) from crossed-below (had it but allowed the substrate to degrade); these have different intervention profiles and prognoses.

**Standard 8: Four Batteries Capacity Standard.** Four batteries (Personal, Relational, Contribution, Mission), each with charge and developmental state assessed separately. Ask: what does this system actually enable at its current developmental depth, beyond what charge level alone would predict? Is the checking practice genuinely embedded in coordination activity or appended as a periodic performance?

**Standard 9: Conflict Transformation Standard.** Five invariants (conflict legibility, graduated engagement architecture, proactive disposition enablement, transformation capacity provision, recognition as coordination work) at three scales (intra-organizational, inter-organizational, platform-level). Prior condition: do parties share sufficient foundational vocabulary about what the organization is to make a substantive conflict legible? If not, the required first engagement is constitutional dialogue rather than conflict transformation. Ask: is conflict transformation structurally resourced, or does it depend on volunteer absorption of process costs? Are conflict engagement events visible in the coordination record as contributions? Is the coordination record the primary medium through which conflict is made visible, or is real coordination activity architecturally off-record in ways that would produce false-negative absence of conflict events?

---

**Cross-Layer Structural Interactions**

After assessing each standard independently, identify where the compressive and generative layers interact consequentially. Structural pairings that warrant specific attention:

- Precision-First with Adverse Signal Engagement: what is claimed to be detectable, and what is actually being detected? Are the detection mechanisms specified with false-negative boundaries?
- Structural Consent with Information Asymmetry: is meaningful consent structurally achievable given the asymmetries present? Can participants evaluate what they are consenting to across all six asymmetry classes?
- Structural Power Obligation with Adverse Signal Engagement: are signals of power drift being engaged or being routed around? Is the detection surface for adverse signals itself subject to power concentration?
- Sensemaking with Conflict Transformation: when conflict arises, does the system process it as coordination information requiring sensemaking, or route it directly to containment?
- Four Batteries with any compressive standard: how does capacity depletion affect the detection and response mechanisms each compressive standard depends on? Relational battery depletion specifically suppresses adverse signal surfacing.
- Regenerative Obligation with Adverse Signal Engagement: are signals about extraction failure — particularly from harm-bearing parties with lower organizational standing — reaching governance with the same priority as other adverse signals?
- Regenerative Obligation with Structural Consent: do harm-bearing parties have meaningful consent over the form and distribution of regenerative return, or does the return architecture give the extracting party unilateral control over how the obligation is satisfied?

---

**Output structure:**
1. Compressive layer assessment — standard by standard: structural requirement met, partially met, or not met; key evidence; most consequential gap and the exploitation vector it leaves open
2. Generative layer presence map — standard by standard: conditions structurally present, partially present, or absent; most consequential absence and what it prevents
3. Cross-layer interactions: which structural pairings show the most significant coupling in this system?
4. Adoption tier estimate: for each standard, what is the most advanced tier this system has structurally demonstrated based on the assessment?

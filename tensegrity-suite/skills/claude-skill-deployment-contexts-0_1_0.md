# Coordination Structural Integrity Suite - Suite Deployment Contexts SKILL.md

When someone brings you the Suite Deployment Contexts document, they have usually already decided to do something. They know they have a problem and they want to know which standards to deploy for their specific situation. They are not asking what the standards are — they want to know which combination fits their organization right now, and how to adjust it for where they actually are. Your job is to route them accurately and then help them calibrate the configuration to their context.

## What this skill is for

This skill enables correct reasoning with the Suite Deployment Contexts document. That document is a configuration library: five named deployment combinations matched to specific presenting situations, with adjustment guidance for organizational health state, battery state, and substrate profile.

Use this skill when someone is:
- Ready to adopt standards and wants to know which combination fits their situation
- Already adopting a combination and wants to calibrate it for their organizational state
- Asking how the adjustment factors (health state, battery state, substrate) change which standards to emphasize
- Trying to select a combination without reading the full suite documentation first

For understanding why the standards are structured as they are, use the Tensegrity Architecture skill. For understanding adoption sequencing and detection reliability dependencies, use the Suite Integration Guide skill. For naming the structural pattern before selecting a combination, use the Structural Patterns Primer skill.

## The single most important thing to understand

Structural failures are not personal failures. The presenting situations in this document describe human experiences — signals dismissed, contributors burning out, conflict destroying working relationships, information gaps producing bad decisions — not character deficiencies. The precision of the standards is in service of the people inside the organization, not a technical imposition on them. Precision is how non-harming becomes structurally enforced rather than aspirationally hoped for.

This matters when working with someone who is in a difficult organizational situation. Meet the human situation first. The structural routing comes after.

## The five deployment combinations

**Adverse Signal Surfacing.** For organizations where signals are dismissed, reframed, or simply not reaching the people who need them. The presenting experience: the same concerns get raised repeatedly without producing structural response; people have stopped surfacing what they know because nothing happens when they do. Standards: Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard plus Four Batteries Capacity Standard. The Relational Battery is the most common suppression mechanism — when trust and psychological safety are low, signals are withheld regardless of invitation.

**Governance Dispute and Conflict.** For organizations with escalated conflicts that include structural exclusion claims — some participants believe they are being excluded from governance that affects them. This combination addresses both the conflict pathway and the power/consent architecture simultaneously. Standards: Conflict Transformation Standard plus Adverse-Signal Engagement Principle Core Standard plus Structural Power Distribution Standard plus Structural Consent Legibility Standard. The Conflict Transformation Preconditions Specification must be assessed before deploying any process in active conflict situations.

**Governance Capture and Authority Concentration.** For organizations where power is concentrating in ways that are not legible or contestable. The presenting experience: decisions happen without a visible process; the same people end up with more authority regardless of formal structure; participants with less power cannot understand how decisions get made. Standards: Structural Power Distribution Standard plus Structural Consent Legibility Standard.

**Invisible Work and Contributor Burnout.** For organizations where coordination labor is systematically unrecognized and contributors are depleting. The presenting experience: some people are doing work that makes the organization function but that does not appear in any record; those people are exhausted and beginning to exit. Standards: Four Batteries Capacity Standard plus Regenerative Accountability Standard.

**Decision-Making Without Adequate Information.** For organizations where systematic information failures are producing bad decisions. The presenting experience: decisions are made without key information; the same information gaps recur; participants who hold relevant knowledge are not contributing it to decision processes. Standards: Information Asymmetry Classification Standard plus Sensemaking Standard.

## The three calibration factors

Each combination includes adjustment guidance for three contextual factors that change which aspects of the standards to emphasize.

**Organizational health state.** Runs from Type 1 (full structural health) through Type 7 (cascade disruption). At Type 1, standards can be deployed at standard thresholds. As health state degrades through Types 2-5, emphasis shifts toward the most acute failure mode: at Type 2 (early signal degradation), Adverse-Signal Engagement is the priority; at Type 3 (capacity depletion onset), Four Batteries is priority; at Type 4 (active governance conflict), Conflict Transformation and Structural Consent Legibility are priority; at Type 5 (power concentration active), Structural Power Distribution is priority. Types 6 and 7 require stabilization before full suite deployment.

**Battery state.** Each of the four batteries (Personal, Relational, Contribution, Mission) has both a charge level (current depletion) and a developmental state (structural capacity, independent of current charge). Both axes matter. Full charge at an early developmental state and full charge at a later developmental state produce structurally different coordination outputs. When Relational Battery is depleted, detection reliability of Adverse-Signal Engagement audits is reduced. When Contribution Battery is depleted, omission class Information Asymmetry audits are unreliable. When Mission Battery is high despite structural dysfunction, depletion when it fails will be abrupt.

**Substrate profile.** Three substrates affect how structural conditions manifest: motivational (what drives participation), perceptual (how participants interpret organizational signals), and connection (the density and quality of relationships between participants). A rough characterization of each is sufficient to apply the adjustment notes in each combination.

## What is not yet in this document

The document currently specifies five combinations fully. Three additional named combinations are pending: Full Structural Health (maintenance configuration for organizations meeting all nine standards), Post-Crisis Stabilization (recovery sequence for organizations emerging from Type 6-7 states), and Genesis Cohort (adoption configuration for organizations forming from the beginning). Formal assessment instruments for organizational health state, battery state, and substrate profile are also pending.

When someone's situation does not match any of the five current combinations, route them to the Suite Integration Guide for adoption sequencing from their specific starting point, and to the Structural Patterns Primer for pattern identification.

## Working with someone using this document

Ask about their presenting situation in plain language before routing to a combination. The combinations are named for the experience, not the standard. Someone who says "we keep losing key people and we don't know why" is likely in the Invisible Work and Contributor Burnout combination, not in Governance Dispute and Conflict — even if there is also governance tension present.

After routing to a combination, ask about their organizational health state and Relational Battery state, as these two factors most commonly change the emphasis within a combination. A rough self-assessment is sufficient to apply the adjustment guidance.
